## Rumor Mechanic: Luck Roll After Long Rest

After each long rest, every player rolls a d20 Luck Roll:
- **1–10:** A bad rumor is spread about the character.
- **11–20:** A good rumor is spread about the character.

### Determining the Rumor
The DM creates a rumor based on recent party actions, location, and NPCs present. Rumors can affect:
- NPC attitudes (hostile, friendly, suspicious)
- Quest opportunities (doors open or close)
- Obstacles (guards, prices, access)

### Modifying the Luck Roll
The DM may grant advantage/disadvantage or modify the roll by ±2 to ±5 based on:
- Helping citizens, performing heroic deeds (+)
- Causing trouble, breaking laws (–)
- Using magic openly (±, depending on local views)
- Spending resources to bribe, charm, or influence (+)
- Failing or succeeding at major skill checks (±)

### Example Good Rumors
- “Did you hear? The party saved a child from the burning market!”
- “They say those travelers healed the sick for free.”
- “Word is, the newcomers are blessed by the Dawnfather.”

### Example Bad Rumors
- “I heard they stole from the merchant’s stall.”
- “Those outsiders consort with heretics and blighted.”
- “Someone saw them using forbidden magic in the square.”

### Adapting the Mechanic
- Use group rolls for party-wide reputation shifts.
- Adjust rumor impact for major story events.
- Allow players to spend Inspiration or gold to reroll or influence rumors.

This mechanic keeps the party’s reputation dynamic and responsive to their choices, adding social consequences and opportunities throughout the campaign.
