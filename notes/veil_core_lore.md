## **The Blight Veil — Core Lore**

The Blight Veil is not just a phenomenon — it’s a **cosmic wound** and a **veil of transformation** tied to the gods and the world’s hidden history.

### **Origins**

* Long ago, during the **Godfall** (the war in which mortal ambition and divine hubris collided), several deities were slain or shattered.
* The moment of their death tore rifts between the **mortal world** and the **otherworlds of spirit, dream, and void**.
* From these tears bled a luminous, shimmering haze — called *the Blight Veil*. It settled over regions where divinity had been most violently broken.

---

### **What It Is**

* The Veil looks like **mist, auroras, or a shimmer in the air**. Sometimes it’s seen as faint motes of dark light clinging to the horizon, other times a full curtain of light across the sky.
* Where it lingers, the laws of reality **bend**:

  * Time stutters or loops.
  * People hear whispers not their own.
  * Dreams and waking moments blur.
  * Plants and animals mutate, sometimes beautifully, sometimes grotesquely.
* The Concord calls it **corruption**. The Ashroot and other spiritual groups see it as a **sacred liminal state**.

---

### **Purpose / Function**

This is debated across the world — and you can let players uncover the truth as they dig deeper:

1. **Concord Doctrine**: The Veil is the *stench of heresy*, divine punishment left by false gods. They claim it must be suppressed or purged.
2. **Ashroot Belief**: The Veil is a **membrane between worlds**, where the voices of nature and the dead gods still whisper. If embraced, it could heal the world.
3. **Godscar Cults**: The Veil is a **cradle for rebirth** — one of the slain gods is *not truly dead*, and the Veil is its cocoon.
4. **Secret Truth (DM’s Choice)**:

   * The Veil is **not a curse** but a **metamorphosis** — the world is trying to evolve into its next form.
   * OR, more sinister: the Veil is **the scar tissue of reality**, and if it grows unchecked it will smother all mortal will, binding the world into one eternal divine dream.

---

### **Significance in the Campaign**

* **Visually iconic**: PCs will know they’re near the Blight Veil when skies shimmer oddly, shadows stretch, or music seems to come from nowhere.
* **Prophecy hook**: “When the Blight Veil tears, so too shall the age.” → It foretells a great change, but not whether it’s salvation or doom.
* **Mechanical hook**: Being near the Veil should have tangible effects:

  * Inspiration-like boons (wild surges of insight, prophetic visions).
  * Corruption-like drawbacks (mad whispers, sudden mutations, exhaustion).
* **Plot driver**: Factions will *fight to control it*. To some, it’s a weapon. To others, it’s hope. To the Concord, it’s the greatest threat to their order.

---

⚔️ **Think of the Blight Veil as The One Thing everyone in the world interprets differently.**

* To peasants: it’s a ghost light in the sky.
* To priests: heresy made manifest.
* To cultists: a holy promise.
* To scholars: the biggest unanswered question of their age.
* To your players: a mystery they can peel back session by session.
