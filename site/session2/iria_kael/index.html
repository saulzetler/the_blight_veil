    Iria Cael¶

    (pronounced EER-ee-uh KAYL) Alias: The Field Saint (rumor name whispered among Concord deserters) Race: Human Age: Mid 30s Alignment: Neutral Good → drifts toward True Neutral depending on how the party treats her Class (if needed mechanically): Former Cleric (Life Domain) / now an unaligned Healer Affiliation: Former Concord medic; now rogue operative loosely aligned with Cindersong sympathizers

    Appearance¶

    • Lean and sun-weathered, with ropey muscle and calloused hands.
    • Her dark hair is braided close to her scalp — practical, not aesthetic — streaked with early silver.
    • She wears a bloodstained Concord medical tabard turned inside out, hiding its insignia.
    • A simple bronze sunburst charm hangs around her neck, cracked down the center.
    • Her pack is organized and utilitarian, filled with bandages, tinctures, and a small folding scalpel that gleams like it’s seen more confessions than surgeries.
    • She carries no obvious weapon — but her belt hides a short, rusty dagger etched with the phrase: “Mercy is never clean.”

    Personality & Mannerisms¶

    • Voice: Calm, dry, deliberate — like someone who has learned that yelling never saves a life.
    • Demeanor: Tired compassion — still wants to help, but doesn’t believe in redemption anymore.

    • Behavioral tics:

    • Always checks her hands when nervous, as if expecting to see blood.

    • Avoids meeting eyes when lying.
    • Smells faintly of burnt herbs and antiseptic.
    • Core Impression: A woman who has performed mercy killings and never forgiven herself.

    Motivations¶

    Drive Description
    Preserve life, not faith. Once a devout medic of Lathander, she has lost faith in divine order but not in human worth.
    Uncover the rot in the Concord. She knows the Concord performs “purity trials” on its own wounded — experimenting with the Veil. She carries proof.
    Test others’ convictions. She probes everyone she meets: are you cruel because you must be, or because it’s easy?
    Atone without believing in forgiveness. She saves who she can, kills who she must, and expects no gods to notice.

    Behavior Toward the Party¶

    • If they save the dying soldier: She recognizes mercy when she sees it — becomes wary allies.

    • “You did right by him. That’s rarer than daylight these days.”

    • If they abandon or kill him: She grows cold and skeptical.

    • “You talk about justice, but all I see are new uniforms for old sins.”

    • If Fergie reveals his faith:

    • “Another Dawn-priest? Then you already know what light does when it burns too long.”

    • If Oscar speaks of the Concord:

    • “You’re their reflection, metal one. I hope you like what you see.”

    Dialogue Branches¶

    Initial Encounter (Roadside Scene)¶

    “If you’ve come to finish the job, at least let him die clean.” “I was a medic once. Concord field division. That was before I realized we were stitching soldiers back together just so the pyres had someone new to burn.”

    If the party heals or comforts the soldier:

    “You’re not like the rest. Maybe you’ve still got something worth saving.”

    If they kill him:

    “Mercy’s faster, at least. But don’t lie to yourself — you did it for you, not him.”

    Campfire Reflection (if she travels with them)¶

    “Do you ever wonder what comes after the Veil? Or do you just pray it never opens again?” “You can tell a lot about a person by who they decide is worth saving.” “I patched up enough zealots to know the holy light burns hottest on those who’ve never stood in shadow.”

    Revelation Scene (later encounter)¶

    If the party gains her trust, she reveals she carries a sealed Concord dossier — medical orders signed by Inquisitor Rellis authorizing the use of Veil fractures on “noncompliant test subjects.”

    “I saw what they did in the infirmary beneath the Sanctara. They called it purification. The Veil didn’t take them — it remembered them.”

    Secret Background¶

    • Past: Iria served in the Third Sun Division, a Concord field medic unit stationed along the Godscar. When the blight began infecting soldiers, she was ordered to experiment with “Veil exposure.” The results were catastrophic — bodies turned hollow, minds unthreaded. When she refused to continue, the Inquisitors marked her as a deserter.

    • Present: She’s been moving between Cindersong contacts, trading stolen medical knowledge for protection. She doesn’t fully trust Vaereth — suspects Cindersong’s motives aren’t as pure as they claim.

    • Secret: Iria is Veil-touched. During her experiments, a tear opened and marked her.

    • She occasionally hears echoes of the dead she’s treated.

    • Her blood glows faintly in moonlight.
    • She dreams of a burning ward filled with singing patients. She hides these symptoms, believing they’re penance, not power.

    Mechanical Notes (if you need stats)¶

    • AC: 12 (unarmored)
    • HP: 27 (5d8 + 5)
    • Speed: 30 ft
    • Attacks: Dagger (+4 to hit, 1d4+2 damage)
    • Spells (once cleric): Spare the Dying, Cure Wounds, Detect Poison and Disease, Calm Emotions

    • Special:

    • Veil Echo (1/day): When she stabilizes a dying creature, they experience a fleeting vision of the Veil — roll a d6; on a 1, they gain 1 level of exhaustion.

    • Unholy Insight: Advantage on Medicine checks to diagnose blight-related ailments.

    DM Hooks¶

    • She could become a temporary traveling companion — guiding them through Ashroot in exchange for safety.
    • She might later be captured by Concord and reappear in a moral dilemma rescue.
    • If she dies, her blood briefly opens a flicker of the Veil — revealing the same silhouette from Session 1.
    • If she survives long-term, she could become a moral foil to Fergie, or a reluctant confidante for Oscar.
