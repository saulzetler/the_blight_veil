    Eris’ Amulet (Revised Lore)¶

    Also called: The Witch’s Hook, The Bone-Tether, The Chain of the Widow (Requires attunement by a Fighter)

    💀 Why He Wears It¶

    He doesn’t wear it out of love. He wears it because he can’t take it off.

    When he found the ruins of their home — after the night of fire and blood — he discovered this amulet hanging amid the ashes, still warm to the touch. Out of fury and grief, he tried to smash it… and when he did, its chain melted into his skin.

    Since then, he’s felt her presence — not a loving whisper, but a taunting echo. It pulls him toward places where she’s been, where witches gather, where the Blight leaks through the world. Whether this is a curse or some perverse tether she left behind, he doesn’t know — but it’s the only link he has to her and their child.

    So he wears it for one reason:

    “If that bitch is still out there, this damned thing will lead me to her.”

    He hates it. He resents it. But he needs it. It’s both a leash and a compass.

    🩸 Stage I — Dormant (“The Hook in the Heart”)¶

    Unlocked upon attunement (cannot be removed once worn).

    The amulet is inert, but it feels — it tightens slightly around his neck when magic or witchcraft is nearby.

    Properties:

    • Hunter’s Pull. You can sense when a creature within 60 ft. casts a spell or uses a magical ability; the amulet tugs faintly in their direction.
    • Relentless Survival. When you are reduced to 0 HP but not killed outright, the amulet jolts you with necrotic energy. You instead drop to 1 HP and take 1 level of exhaustion. Once per long rest.
    • Cursed Attachment. You cannot remove or unattune from the amulet. Attempts to do so cause you to take 2d10 psychic damage and hear a whisper that says, “You’re not done yet.”

    Flavour: The amulet is like a parasite. It keeps him alive against his will, driven by vengeance and obsession. He wears it because breaking it might kill him — or worse, sever his last hope of finding the truth.

    ⚔️ Stage II — Awakened (“The Witch’s Snare”)¶

    Unlocked after encountering witches of the Blight or receiving a vision of Eris through the amulet.

    The amulet’s influence grows stronger — its bone veins pulse faintly. It begins whispering in battle, feeding on rage.

    Properties:

    • All previous effects, plus:
    • Veilstrike. Once per short or long rest, when you use Action Surge, your weapon attacks deal an additional 1d8 necrotic damage until the end of your turn.
    • Blood Compass. As an action, you can concentrate for 1 minute to learn the direction (not distance) of the nearest source of witchcraft or blight magic within 5 miles. Once per long rest.
    • Resentful Resilience. When you take damage that would reduce you below half HP, the amulet feeds on your pain: gain temporary HP equal to your Fighter level.

    Curse Deepens: He begins to see her in his periphery — flickers of her form in smoke or flame. Sometimes she taunts him, sometimes pleads. Sometimes, her voice sounds like his daughter’s instead. He drinks to silence them.

    Flavour: The amulet doesn’t just keep him alive — it keeps him angry. It wants him fighting, killing, feeding it with bloodshed. Whether Eris’ spirit drives it or something else does, it’s using his hatred as fuel.

    🔥 Stage III — Fully Awakened (“The Breaking of the Hook”)¶

    Unlocked after the major arc — when he chooses what to do with the amulet.

    This is the moment of decision: Does he break the tether and free himself (perhaps at the cost of his life or connection to his daughter)? Or does he embrace it, letting its corruption become part of him?

    ✨ Path of Severance — “The Broken Hook”¶

    He finally destroys the amulet — whether through witchfire, divine cleansing, or sheer will. The act leaves a black scar around his neck and a gaping silence where the whispers once were.

    Properties:

    • The curse ends; the amulet crumbles into ash.
    • Permanently gain +1 to Constitution and resistance to necrotic damage.
    • Once per long rest, when reduced to 0 HP, you can instead drop to 1 HP — your will alone keeping you alive. (No exhaustion penalty.)

    Flavour: The Blight’s whispers are gone. The world feels quieter — heavier. He’s finally free, but at a cost: the faint warmth that once hinted at his daughter’s soul is gone too.

    💀 Path of Embrace — “The Witch’s Blade”¶

    He chooses not to break it. Instead, he drives its chain into his wrist or chest, merging it with his body. The whispers stop — because now, they are his thoughts.

    Properties:

    • You gain all Awakened effects.
    • Blighted Fury. When you reduce a creature to 0 HP, you can make another attack immediately (no action required).
    • Unholy Endurance. Once per long rest, when reduced to 0 HP, you instead regain 1 HP and gain temporary HP equal to your Fighter level + Constitution modifier.
    • Aura of Fear. Enemies within 10 ft. have disadvantage on Wisdom saving throws while you’re conscious.

    Flavour: The amulet fuses with his veins, its bone etching crawling up his neck. His eyes glow faintly when he fights. He’s no longer just wearing the curse — he is it.

    🕯️ Summary of Motivations & Symbolism¶

    Stage Theme Why He Keeps It What It Represents
    Dormant Guilt & Survival Can’t remove it; it’s his only lead to his wife/child The chain of obsession
    Awakened Rage & Obsession Uses it as a compass to hunt her and witches Hatred turned into purpose
    Fully Awakened Choice & Identity Decides if he’ll break free or become the monster Closure through destruction or acceptance

    💭 Character Line Example¶

    “I don’t wear it for her. I wear it because it burns when she’s near. And one day, I’m going to follow that burn straight to her throat.”
