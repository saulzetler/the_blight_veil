    Items & Notes That Can Be Found¶

    • Scrap of Hymn: A torn fragment of parchment from Marla’s belongings. The script is written in an old dialect, partially burnt at the edges. A successful DC 13 Religion or History check reveals it is part of the Cindersong, a hymn said to echo with divine resonance. Its words shift slightly when read aloud, unsettling and beautiful.

    • DM Exposition: "You uncover a scrap of parchment, its edges burnt and the script shifting as you read—a fragment of something ancient and powerful."

    • Concord Proclamation Poster: A large sheet nailed to a post near the square. In bold ink it reads: “By decree of the High Council of the Concord, heresy shall henceforth be met with flame and steel. Any who harbor dissidents are subject to the pyre.” Players can take it, use it as proof of Concord severity, or later show it as propaganda.

    • Hidden Symbol (Cindersong Sigil): A small mark carved into the underside of the stage — three interlocked spirals forming a flame. A DC 15 Religion check identifies it as the mark of the Cindersong. Touching it briefly fills the character with warmth and the sound of faint singing, like a chorus from underground. It suggests the Cindersong have infiltrated the capital itself.

    Absolutely — let’s build some Session 1 discovery items for The Blight Veil that fit your campaign’s tone of witch-trial dread, decaying theocracy, and colonial corruption.

    These are meant to feel tactile and charged with symbolic or narrative power — not just loot, but story catalysts. I’ll include how players might come across each item, the required skill checks (with DCs), and a short description of what the item might hint at or do later in the campaign.

    Additional Items¶

    ⚜️ 1. Sanctara Prayer Coin¶

    Type: Religious trinket / clue
    Discovery: Found beneath a cracked cobblestone near the gallows or altar in Orrenval’s town square.
    Check: Investigation DC 13 to notice the faint circular groove in the stone that hides it.
    Description: A tarnished golden coin bearing the threefold eye of the Trifarix, one side rubbed smooth by years of use. Whispering a prayer while holding it makes the coin subtly vibrate.
    Significance: Might later serve as a key to a sanctified vault beneath the Sanctara, or react to divine corruption nearby.

    🔮 2. Phial of Blighted Ash¶

    Type: Alchemical component / corruption sample
    Discovery: Discovered in a forgotten apothecary cabinet or on the person of a burned heretic.
    Check: Arcana or Medicine DC 15 to recognize the ash as not ordinary soot but the remains of something “living.”
    Description: A glass vial of fine, grey-black ash that shifts as if breathing. Exposure to open air causes nearby insects to die.
    Use: May be used in alchemical crafting or to reveal corruption — mixing it with holy water causes a violent reaction.

    🕯️ 3. Tallow of the Condemned¶

    Type: Consumable / dark tool
    Discovery: Found in the personal belongings of an inquisitor, wrapped in linen and marked with a sigil of secrecy.
    Check: Religion or Arcana DC 14 to discern its ritual use.
    Description: A candle made from human tallow, which burns with a greenish flame and muffles sound within a 10-foot radius.
    Use: Grants advantage on Stealth checks in darkness for 10 minutes — but risks divine retribution (roll a d20 each use; on a 1, the nearest holy symbol cracks).

    🕰️ 4. Broken Chronometer of Saint Varn¶

    Type: Relic / curiosity
    Discovery: Hidden in a chest belonging to a local archivist or librarian sympathetic to heretics.
    Check: Investigation DC 12 to locate; Arcana DC 16 to identify.
    Description: An ornate brass timepiece that ticks backward. If wound, it rewinds six seconds of time once per day (effect: one failed saving throw can be rerolled once/day).
    Lore: Saint Varn was said to have “turned back the sun” during the Burning of Orrenval — the Trifarix later erased him from scripture.

    📜 5. Proclamation of Purity (Torn)¶

    Type: Historical clue / paper document
    Discovery: Found nailed to a half-collapsed notice board near the square.
    Check: History or Insight DC 10 to recall that this decree was repealed long ago.
    Description: A holy edict listing “approved virtues” and “signs of corruption.” The lower half is missing — possibly cut out by someone hiding the truth.
    Use: Clues to a forbidden sect that worshipped the Blighted Horizon; if completed later, reveals a name or sigil the Concord fears.

    🩸 6. Iron Band of Confession¶

    Type: Cursed ring / relic
    Discovery: Found on a skeletal hand reaching from beneath chapel rubble.
    Check: Perception DC 13 to notice the glint; Religion DC 15 to know its purpose.
    Description: A ring used by inquisitors to compel confession — once worn, it tightens when its wearer lies (1d4 psychic damage per lie).
    Use: Can be used to interrogate others — but the wearer is equally bound by its magic.

    🌿 7. Herbalist’s Ledger¶

    Type: Journal / lore item
    Discovery: Hidden behind a false panel in an old stall.
    Check: Investigation DC 14 to find; Nature DC 12 to interpret entries.
    Description: Lists herbs, poultices, and “forbidden tinctures of the green veins.” Marginalia mention experiments against the Blight.
    Use: Learning these recipes could allow players to brew minor anti-corruption salves or poisons later.

    🪶 8. Crow-etched Pendant¶

    Type: Arcane talisman / omen
    Discovery: Found hanging from a wooden cross above a sacrificial pit.
    Check: Religion DC 12 to know it represents a god long erased from the pantheon.
    Description: A black pendant in the shape of a crow’s skull. When held, nearby crows caw in unison.
    Use: Advantage on Wisdom (Perception) checks involving omens, but prolonged use may draw attention from forbidden entities.

    Skill Check Prompts (with DC suggestions)¶

    Persuasion/Deception Checks: - Convincing Inquisitor Rellis to doubt the execution: DC 14 Persuasion or Deception. - Rallying sympathetic citizens in the crowd to cause a distraction: DC 15 Persuasion.

    Intimidation Checks: - Intimidating Captain Deyran into standing down temporarily: DC 17 Intimidation.

    Religion/History Checks: - Interpreting the hymn scrap’s meaning: DC 13 Religion or History. - Recognizing the Cindersong sigil beneath the stage: DC 15 Religion.

    Stealth/Acrobatics Checks: - Slipping through the crowd unnoticed: DC 14 Stealth. - Dodging through collapsing stalls during a chase: DC 15 Acrobatics.

    Perception/Insight Checks: - Spotting the cloaked sympathizer who subtly helps them: DC 12 Perception. - Reading Rellis’s inner doubt: DC 13 Insight. - DM Exposition: "As you approach the Inquisitor, his grip tightens on his blade, and the crowd leans in, eager to see what you’ll do."
