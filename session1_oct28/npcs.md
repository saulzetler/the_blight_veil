### Key NPCs & Boxed DM Dialogue

**Marla Veinwright** – Defiant heretic, voice shaking but determined.
> “They call it heresy, but it is only truth — a song older than their chains. Will you let them burn me for a melody?”

**Inquisitor Rellis** – Fanatical, but with a spark of doubt.
> “The law is clear. Heresy must be purged… and yet, my heart falters. Why would the Dawnfather gift a song that tempts the innocent? Speak quickly — should I silence her?”

**Captain Deyran** – Brutal enforcer, eager to crush dissent.
> “Enough of this dithering, Rellis! The people crave justice! By fire and blade, we show them the price of rebellion!”

**Onlooker: Fearful Citizen** – Terrified of Concord authority.
> “Please, just let them burn her and be done with it.”

**Onlooker: Sympathetic Citizen (Cindersong sympathizer)** – Whispering support.
> “The song she sings… I’ve heard it in my dreams. Stranger, if you value truth, do not let them take her voice.”

# Motivations & Secrets

- Marla: Driven by a vision of a world where truth and song are not crimes, Marla risks everything to spread the Cindersong. She hopes to inspire rebellion and awaken the spirit of resistance in others, but beneath her defiance lies deep fear for her family, who are threatened by Concord reprisals. Secretly, she wonders if her faith in the hymn is enough to save anyone.
- Rellis: A lifelong servant of the Concord, Rellis is torn between his duty and a growing sense of doubt. He secretly keeps a forbidden hymn scrap, a relic from his childhood that reminds him of gentler times. Rellis fears that the law he enforces may be unjust, and he is haunted by dreams of the Dawnfather’s silence. He is vulnerable to persuasion, but terrified of losing his position and being branded a heretic.
- Deyran: Ambitious and ruthless, Deyran seeks promotion and recognition within the Concord. He despises heretics and sees the Blighted as a threat to order. Beneath his bravado, Deyran hides a personal loss—his sibling was taken by the Blight, fueling his hatred and driving his need to prove himself. He is quick to violence but secretly fears that the Concord’s methods may one day turn on him.
