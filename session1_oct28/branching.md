# Session 1 Branching Outcomes & Immersion Tips

### Branching Paths

#### 1. **Intervene Publicly (Confrontation)**
- **Scene Hook:**
    > Captain Deyran raises a torch to set the stage alight. “The Dawnfather’s light will burn away this corruption! Witness the price of heresy!”

- **Escalation Moments:**
  1. Citizens begin shouting — some in support, some in fear.
  2. Inquisitors draw steel if the players interfere.
  3. A Concord patrol rushes in as backup.

- **Encounter:**
  *2 Concord Inquisitors + 1 Captain Enforcer (CR ~1/4 - 2). Players must choose to fight or drive them off.*

- **Outcome:**
  - Victory: Players flee with Marla into the alleys.
  - Defeat: Players are captured.
- DM Exposition: "The torch’s flame flickers dangerously close to the stage, and the crowd’s shouts rise in a chaotic chorus. What do you do?"

---

#### 2. **Negotiate for Marla’s Release**
- **Scene Hook:**
    > Inquisitor Rellis hesitates, his eyes flickering between Marla and the flames. “Perhaps… the Council need not be burdened with another pyre. Who speaks on her behalf?”

- **Escalation Moments:**
  1. A rival citizen denounces the players as “witch’s allies.”
  2. Rellis demands evidence that Marla is harmless (Persuasion DC 14, Deception DC 14, Religion DC 13).
  3. Captain Deyran interrupts, furious that justice is being delayed.

- **Outcome:**
  - Success: Marla is released into their custody, though they are marked as suspect.
  - Failure: Execution proceeds unless players escalate.
- DM Exposition: "Rellis’s eyes dart between you and the crowd, searching for a reason to spare Marla. The tension is palpable."

---

#### 3. **Rescue and Escape (Stealth/Deception)**
- **Scene Hook:**
    > The crowd presses tighter, and in the confusion a gap opens near the stage. Marla’s eyes meet yours — desperate, pleading. The guards are distracted for a moment. This is your chance.

- **Escalation Moments:**
  1. Players slip through the crowd (Stealth DC 14).
  2. If spotted, a chase through the market erupts (Acrobatics DC 15).
  3. Environmental hazards (collapsing stalls, panicked citizens).

- **Outcome:**
  - Success: Players reach a safehouse contact who recognizes the hymn scrap.
  - Failure: They are cornered and captured.
- DM Exposition: "A sudden commotion opens a gap in the crowd. Marla’s desperate gaze meets yours—this may be your only chance."

---

#### 4. **Do Nothing / Observe**
- **Scene Hook:**
    > The torch falls, the crowd gasps, and the scent of burning cloth fills the square. The cries of Marla echo — until they don’t.

- **Escalation Moments:**
  1. The crowd grows restless; some weep, some cheer.
  2. A Cindersong sympathizer in the crowd makes eye contact with the PCs.
  3. That night, the party finds a coded message slipped into their lodgings: “You saw. Come to the catacombs.”

- **Outcome:**
  - PCs are marked as witnesses and potential recruits by the Cindersong.
  - They are also quietly noted by the Concord as suspicious.
- DM Exposition: "The torch falls, and the crowd’s reaction is a mix of horror and relief. The scent of burning cloth fills the air as Marla’s cries fade."

---

### Additional Narrative Beats Along Each Path
- **Rumors in the Crowd:** NPC gossip mentions the Blight Veil creeping closer, and “heretics singing storms awake.”
- **Inquisitor’s Prayer:** If the players interact with Rellis, he mutters: *“Dawnfather, grant me clarity — why does justice feel so heavy?”*
- **Hidden Supporters:** In the escape path, a cloaked figure trips a guard to help the players (an early ally from the Cindersong).

## Branching Outcomes (Expanded)
- If players split up, run simultaneous scenes and use minor NPCs to relay information.
- If players leave early, they may find coded messages or be followed by Concord agents.
- If players bribe, disguise, or incite a riot, adjust the scene with chaos, shifting allegiances, and new opportunities or threats.

## Immersion Tips
- Use crowd reactions, rumors, and random events (e.g., sudden storm, lost child) to keep the scene dynamic.
- Offer sensory details and minor NPC interactions to make the world feel alive.

### Session Goal
By the end of Session 1, the players are entangled with both the Concord and the Cindersong, whether they act, negotiate, or stand by. This sets the stage for future story beats where their allegiances and choices matter.
- DM Exposition: "As the dust settles, you realize your actions have drawn the attention of both the Concord and the Cindersong. The city will not forget what happened here."
