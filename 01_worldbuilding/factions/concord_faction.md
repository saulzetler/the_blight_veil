# **The Concord**

**Overview:**  
The Concord is the dominant governing power of the known world — a disciplined and unyielding regime born from the ashes of chaos after the Veil descended. It is ruled by the *Trifarix*, a Council of Three embodying the Concord’s founding ideals: **Might**, **Vision**, and **Guile**. Together, they maintain a delicate balance of strength, intellect, and cunning that preserves civilization against the encroaching Blight.

---

## **The Trifarix — Council of Three**

### **High Marshal Varric Thane — The Pillar of Might**

**Title:** Warden of the Concordant Armies
**Principle:** Strength through unity and conquest.
**Allegiance:** The Legion of the Standard (the Concord’s standing army and colonial enforcers).

**Profile:**
Varric Thane rose from a line of frontier-born soldiers — men and women hardened by endless skirmishes against the “heathen provinces.” A towering figure wrapped in ceremonial steel, he carries the Banner of the First Flame, a relic said to have burned away heresy during the founding wars.

To Thane, mercy is treason, and compromise is weakness. He believes that the Concord’s peace can only be maintained through overwhelming dominance — of body, of faith, and of will. His campaigns are brutal but efficient, his soldiers fanatically loyal.


**Past Events:**
- Personally led the Siege of Aster’s Hollow, where he burned an entire city to stop the spread of “blight contagion.”
- Rumored to have executed his own brother for questioning the Concord’s war on the southern tribes.
- Recently began clashing with the Pillar of Vision over the “purity” of new conscripts.
	Thane insists that the Concord’s armies must accept any able-bodied recruit, regardless of background, so long as they prove loyalty and strength. He believes that survival demands pragmatism and numbers, even if it means conscripting those with questionable lineage or minor Veil-touched traits.
	Elira Voss, however, demands strict spiritual and genetic screening. She fears that allowing the impure—those with suspected heretical beliefs, blighted ancestry, or exposure to forbidden magics—will invite corruption into the ranks. Her clergy push for rigorous tests, rituals, and surveillance, rejecting anyone who fails to meet their standards of purity.
	Their conflict has led to heated debates, stalled recruitment drives, and growing tension between the military and the Church. Some soldiers whisper that Voss’s standards are impossible, while others fear Thane’s leniency will doom the Concord to internal rot.

**Symbol:** A broken sword reforged and bound with iron wire — representing discipline through suffering.

---

### **Matriarch Elira Voss — The Pillar of Vision**

**Title:** Keeper of the Veiled Truths
**Principle:** Strength through enlightenment and divine order.
**Allegiance:** The Church of the Veil, the Concord’s state faith.

**Profile:**
Elira Voss is both revered and feared — a pale, unaging woman who claims to hear the whispers of saints lost beyond the Blight Veil. Once a scholar of natural philosophy, she turned zealot after a “revelation” during a pilgrimage where she vanished for three days in the ash wastes.

Now, she preaches that the Concord’s expansion is divinely ordained — that blight and heresy are tests meant to separate the pure from the false. Her temples double as schools and surveillance hubs; her clergy catalog “impure thoughts” as sins against the Concord’s unity.

**Past Events:**

- Authored the Scriptures of the Veil, a doctrine that redefined blight as a “holy sickness.”

- Accused of concealing miracles that contradict official Church teaching.

- Known to hold secret midnight sessions with “dreamspeakers” — individuals believed to channel forbidden knowledge.

**Symbol:** A single eye, painted closed, weeping black wax.

---

### **Lord Silven Corvane — The Pillar of Guile**  


**Title:** Chancellor of Coin and Whisper

**Principles:** Strength through cunning, information, and the unseen hand.

**Allegiances:** The Guild Compact — a network of spies, financiers, and merchant-lords who keep the Concord’s economy alive (and its people obedient).

**Past Events:**
- Brokered the Treaty of Ashen Reach, ending a rebellion by selling out both rebel leaders and their loyalists.
- Survived three assassination attempts — the latest by poisoned communion wine.
- Secretly funds an underground “truth press” that publishes heretical pamphlets about the Trifarix.

**Profile:**

Silven Corvane presents himself as a charming, soft-spoken aristocrat — the kind who never dirties his hands but always gets his way. Beneath the velvet lies a spider’s patience and a serpent’s smile. His influence seeps through trade, debt, and rumor alike.

He views the Concord not as a moral crusade but as a marketplace of power. He funds both sides of the colonial wars, sells relics smuggled from forbidden ruins, and quietly removes those who might upset the “balance” — often through whispers rather than steel.

**Symbol:** A coiled serpent biting its tail, its scales made of coins.

---

## **Philosophy and Law**

The Concord’s doctrine preaches that civilization must be preserved at any cost. The *Concord Law* ensures stability through rigid hierarchy, surveillance, and militarized order. Citizens are taught that unity is strength — dissent, heresy, and mutation are cancers to be purged.

# **The Capital: Orrenval & The Sanctara**

## Orrenval
Orrenval is the heart of the Concord, a city built atop the ruins of ancient temples and battlefields from the Godfall. Its origins lie in the unification of warring city-states, chosen for its strategic location and spiritual significance. Today, Orrenval is a sprawling metropolis of stone towers, bustling markets, and imposing statues of Concord heroes. The city is a center of governance, faith, and military power, where the Trifarix rules and the fate of the world is often decided.

## The Sanctara
The Sanctara is Orrenval’s holy chapel, originally constructed as a shrine to the Dawnfather and later expanded into a grand cathedral after the Godfall. Its foundations are said to rest on sacred ground where the veil between worlds is thinnest. In the current day, the Sanctara serves as both a place of worship and a seat of spiritual authority for the Concord. Its halls are filled with relics, murals depicting the Godfall, and the ever-present scent of incense. The clergy of the Sanctara oversee rituals, trials, and the cataloging of miracles, making it a focal point for both faith and political intrigue.

## **Relations with Other Factions**

- **The Faith:** A strained alliance — the Concord uses religion as a tool of control but fears the Faith’s growing zealotry.  
- **The Blighted:** Treated as abominations; exterminated or enslaved under the Law of Purity.  
- **The Free Peoples:** Viewed as anarchists and smugglers who threaten the Concord’s monopoly on trade and truth.

---

## **Notable Events**

- **Founding of the Trifarix after the Collapse:** In the aftermath of the Godfall and the collapse of old orders, three leaders united to form the Trifarix, establishing the Concord’s ruling council and restoring stability to a fractured world.
- **The Codification of Concord Law:** The Trifarix enacted a sweeping set of laws to enforce unity, suppress dissent, and regulate faith and magic. This legal code became the backbone of Concord society and its expansion.
- **The Purges of the First Blighted:** In response to the rise of Veil-touched outcasts, the Concord launched brutal purges to cleanse its lands, driving survivors into exile and cementing the regime’s reputation for ruthless order.
- **The Reclamation of the Iron Marches:** Concord armies retook the war-torn Iron Marches from rebel factions and blighted forces, securing vital trade routes and expanding the regime’s influence across the frontier.
