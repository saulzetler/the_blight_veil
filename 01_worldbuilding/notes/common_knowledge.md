## 🜂 Common Knowledge: The World Beneath the Blight Veil

### 1. The Concord

* The **Concord** is the ruling power over much of the known lands — a *theocratic empire* guided by the **Trifarix**, a council of three divine authorities said to speak for the gods themselves.
* The Concord claims to bring *order, light, and purity* to the world — though some whisper that their wars are fought as much for faith as for dominion.
* Its **capital city, Orrenval**, gleams with white stone and gold trim. At its heart stands the **Sanctara**, a towering chapel where the Trifarix hold court.
* The Concord’s influence reaches far, but its control thins at the fringes, where rumors of heresy, witchcraft, and the *Veil’s corruption* spread unchecked.

---

### 2. The Blight Veil

* The **Blight Veil** is not merely a phenomenon — it’s a *scar across the horizon*. A roiling, silvery mist that swallows the land, warps nature, and devours reason.
* People do not enter the Veil. Those who do either vanish entirely or return… changed.
* No one knows its true origin, though Concord doctrine insists it is *divine punishment* — a consequence of ancient sin.
* The Veil *moves*. Slowly, silently, but undeniably — encroaching year by year. Entire provinces have been “Veiled” and lost to history.

---

### 3. Faith and Doctrine

* The Concord worships the **Radiant Triune**, a unity of three divine aspects: *Judgment, Mercy, and Order*.
* Heresy, witchcraft, or unsanctioned miracles are punishable by death or purging.
* Holy banners, sermons, and relics are everywhere. Citizens are expected to attend sermons, tithe to the Sanctara, and report signs of corruption.
* The **Inquisitors** of the Concord are both feared and revered — they are said to *see the stain of the Veil* upon any soul.

---

### 4. The Current Era

* It is the **Year of Saint Velan’s Eclipse**, an omen of unrest. Strange lights have appeared along the Veil’s edge.
* Refugees from border towns speak of *voices in the mist* and *rivers that run backwards*.
* Orrenval is under growing tension: shortages, protests, and whispers that the Trifarix no longer agree behind closed doors.
* Still, the people cling to the belief that the Concord — and faith — will save them.

---

### 5. Daily Life Under the Concord

* Every town has a **chapel**, a **warden**, and a **record of purity** for each family. To live without one is to invite suspicion.
* **Purity checks** are common: priests and wardens travel to test citizens for signs of corruption — discoloration, unnatural dreams, unholy whispers.
* Many wear **charms or relics** (blessed rings, silver-threaded cloth, prayer etchings) to ward off the Veil.
* Trade and travel are tightly monitored; leaving one’s assigned district requires written sanction from the Concord.

---

### 6. The Veiled Terrors (Rumors)

* Children are taught cautionary tales: *The Hollow Saints, The Silver-Eyed, The Blightborne*.
* Some soldiers claim to have seen *living shadows* and *ashen figures* beyond the mist — remnants of the fallen world.
* It is said that **magic** used too freely draws the Veil’s attention. “Unbound power invites unbound ruin.”

---

### 7. Known Factions and Powers

* **The Trifarix** – the ruling three, each representing a face of the Triune faith.
* **The Concord Guard** – the empire’s disciplined military.
* **The Inquisition** – the purging arm of the faith, ferreting out corruption.
* **The Warden Orders** – regional enforcers maintaining local order.
* **The Cartographers of the Pale** – secretive explorers who map the creeping edge of the Veil.
* **The Heretic Chorus** (rumored) – those who reject the Triune and claim the Veil hides a forgotten truth.

---

### 8. Cultural Memory

* Everyone has grown up under the **shadow of sermons** warning of the Veil’s hunger.
* No one alive remembers a world without it. The Veil is as natural — and as feared — as death itself.
* Festivals still exist — bright, fervent, desperate attempts to hold onto normalcy — but joy feels like defiance these days.
