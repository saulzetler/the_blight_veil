# The Blight Veil — Act One Roadmap (Sessions 6–10)

> [!warning] DM Reference Only This is a planning document, not a session guide. Use it to orient yourself before prepping individual sessions. Treat session numbers as loose — beats can shift forward or back depending on player pace.

---

## Where We Are

Session 5 ended with the party descending into Rootspire's lower roots following an Oscar construct sighting, and a closing hook revealing the Concord is already present at the resonance sites. The Council's mandate — observe only — is already under pressure.

The party is mid-act. No backstory threads have fully detonated yet. The Concord is a distant shadow, not yet a direct threat. That changes over the next five sessions.

---

## Act One Structure at a Glance

```
Sessions 1–4   →   Inciting Incident + Trial A (established)
Session 5      →   Trial A closes / Trial B opens (Vessel-Null, Concord arrives)
Sessions 6–7   →   Trial B (resonance sites escalate, backstory threads begin)
Sessions 8–9   →   Trial C (a site fails, Concord becomes unavoidable)
Session 10     →   Point of No Return
```

## Sessions 6–7 — Trial B: The Sites Are Not Silent

**Dramatic question:** What is the Concord actually doing at the resonance sites — and can the party keep pretending to be observers?

### What's Happening in the World

The party begins investigating the remaining resonance sites under the Council's mandate. Each site hums with the same delayed harmonic pattern as the Sanctara's pulse — the Ashroot Resonance. The Concord's presence at the sites isn't aggressive yet. They are watching too. Two factions, both pretending to observe.

The sites themselves are not passive. They react to the party — especially to Oscar and Fergie.

### Key Beats to Hit

**Hog** — The storm-sigil on his gauntlet token surges near the first resonance site. It reacts to the harmonic anomaly, flaring with a jagged branching light. This is the first visible sign that Hog is carrying something old. No explanation yet — just the signal. NPCs notice. Selune of the Knots, if present, whispers that the pattern looks like the old sky-ward runes.

**Fergie** — His Porter Stone begins to double-pulse near a resonance site — two beats, one warm, one cold. It's faint and easy to miss. This is the first manifestation of Maria Zurita's echo bleeding through. Fergie can investigate or ignore it. Ignoring it makes the Stone unstable later.

**Oscar** — At least one of the resonance sites contains Verdancy signatures — biomechanical root structures that react to Oscar's presence as though recognizing a missing component. This is subtle. The site doesn't attack. It _reaches_. Whether Oscar acknowledges this is up to the player, but the DM should note it. The Concord observers at the site clock Oscar's reaction.

**Zennor** — His amulet burns cold at the edge of a resonance site. The first hint that Eris has been somewhere nearby — or something that carries her pattern has. This lands better if it's brief and unexplained. He hears nothing. The amulet just goes cold and stays cold for an hour.

### The Concord Problem

The party and a Concord scouting unit are working the same sites, not yet in open conflict. The Concord soldiers are professional, politely hostile, and clearly under orders to document — not engage. But they are _cataloguing_ the party. Oscar's designation gets logged. Zennor's former Dawnshield bearing gets noticed by a ranking officer who says nothing but doesn't look away.

The tension here isn't combat. It's the slow mutual recognition that both groups know more than they're saying.

> [!tip] Optional Beat — Kett's Secret If Kett (the Rootspire child) hasn't passed on what she saw near the lower roots, Sessions 6–7 are a good window. A Concord agent in civilian dress near Rootspire's edge — someone who doesn't belong. This gives the party early confirmation that Rootspire itself isn't safe from Concord eyes.

## Sessions 8–9 — Trial C: Something Goes Wrong

**Dramatic question:** What happens when the party can no longer observe from the outside?

### The Break

One of the resonance sites destabilizes during or just before the party's investigation. Not a full breach — more like a crack. The harmonic pattern shifts, the air goes heavy and luminous, shadows pulse in time with heartbeats. Something that should be dormant has been disturbed.

The Concord response is immediate and disproportionate. They move to seal the site, which means sealing it against everyone — including the party. Their "observer" status means nothing to a Concord field commander acting under emergency protocol.

This is the first moment where the party has to choose between the Council's mandate and what they can see in front of them.

### Character Threads That Should Surface Here

**Zennor** — The amulet stops being cold and starts burning. At the destabilized site, he hears something — Eris's voice or something wearing it. One line. Distant. It should be unnerving because it doesn't sound like a warning. It sounds like she's been waiting. This is the activation of his thread, not the resolution. He doesn't learn anything definitive. He just can't pretend the amulet is inert anymore.

The Dawnshield officer from Sessions 6–7 reappears here. They know who Zennor is. They don't move against him — yet. But they make clear that deserters don't stay lost forever.

**Hog** — The storm-sigil goes fully visible during the site disturbance. Everyone sees it. The Concord officer recognizes the pattern — it matches something in their sealed records about sky-ward ruins. Hog is now on their list in a different way than before. A Cindersong contact (druid, wandering, wary) seeks him out after the event and tells him the sigil is older than he is, and that the Concord killed the thing it belonged to.

**Fergie** — The doubled pulse from his Porter Stone becomes impossible to ignore at the unstable site. He sees a figure — briefly, through the resonance shimmer — holding a Stone shaped like his but inverted. She's gone before anyone else can look. This is Maria Zurita's first apparition. It should feel like a reflection in standing water, just a moment before the water moves.

**Oscar** — The Verdancy signatures at this site are stronger. The biomechanical growth is actively orienting toward Oscar. More pressingly: the Concord identifies Oscar by designation during the lockdown. They call them "Verdancy Unit 9" — not Oscar. This is the first time Oscar hears their original classification spoken aloud by someone with institutional memory of what they are. The Recall Directive is in motion.

### The Concord Moves

By the end of Session 9, the Concord has sealed at least one site and made it clear they consider the party a complication. They haven't attacked. They've done something more unsettling: they've filed a report. The party now exists in Concord records by name, designation, and observed harmonic signatures. The machinery has noticed them.

## Session 10 — The Point of No Return

**Dramatic question:** Who are these people to each other, and what are they willing to do about what they know?

### What This Session Needs to Accomplish

By the end of Session 10, the party should feel _stuck in the story_ — not trapped by external circumstance, but anchored by personal investment. The world has gotten its hooks into all of them. The Point of No Return is not a single event. It's a convergence of all four threads in the same session.

### The Shape of the Session

The Council of 8 summons the party. Not for a trial — for a reckoning. The Council knows the party has not been neutral observers. They also know the Concord has identified the party. Rootspire is now a pressure point.

The Council doesn't punish them. They ask a question instead: _Now that you know what you know — what do you intend to do?_

This is the party's moment to articulate, in character, what they are fighting for. The Council is not asking for loyalty. They're asking whether the party has chosen.

### The Four Threads Converging

**Zennor** — Someone in the Council knows the name Eris. Not a lot — a reference, a record, something old. Enough to confirm she is real and that the Veil has touched her. His daughter is not mentioned directly, but the Council indicates that what was bound in black fire doesn't simply vanish. The pink bracelet glows faintly in the Council chamber. Zennor can choose to say nothing or ask. Either way, he knows his grief is not the end of the story.

**Hog** — The Cindersong contact from Session 8–9 has a message for the Council: the sky-ward ruins are waking. The storm-sigil on Hog's token is connected to this. For the first time, Hog is told plainly that the thing inside him was stolen — and that the Concord committed the theft. What he does with that is his choice. But the Council won't pretend it isn't happening anymore.

**Fergie** — A Council member knows the term "Echo-touched." They don't explain Maria Zurita — they simply tell Fergie that his Stone is resonating with something that should not exist in this timeline, and that sooner or later that something will find him. This is ominous, not illuminating. The thread is live but unresolved.

**Oscar** — The Council reveals, gently, that Rootspire's Great Tree responded to Oscar's presence in a way it hasn't responded to anything in decades. The word they use: _recognized_. They don't know what Project Verdancy is. But the tree does. Oscar's path forward — toward Lumestra, toward Tessaline Marr, toward what they were made to be — begins here.

### The Closing Hook

The Council gives the party a choice that functions as the on-ramp to Act Two: they can leave Rootspire and move freely, or they can accept a deeper role in what the Council is preparing for. Either path leads forward. There is no safe neutral option anymore — that door closed when the Concord filed its report.

One more thing. The Concord messenger who arrived at the end of Session 4 with news from Orrenval — the Council reveals what that message contained. It is not good news. (Determine specifics during prep — but it should confirm that Orrenval knows the party exists and that the Trifarix is paying attention.)
