# **🌑 Blight Taxonomy (Environment-Dependent)**

### _For consistent but varied encounter design_

The Blight adapts to whatever it infects — fauna, flora, corpses, even stone and air. Each environment shapes the corruption differently.

## **1. Humanoid Blightforms (Urban, Ruins, Concord Conflict Zones)**

**Theme:** rot + fragmentation + psychic echoes  
Examples:

- Blighted Husks (zombie variant)
    
- Veilburned Speakers (whispering, lure-based undead)
    
- Stitchwalkers (limbs fused from multiple bodies, used in Concord purges)
    

**Behavior:**  
Driven, purposeful, drawn to harmonic fluctuations or emotional spikes.

**Oscar Interaction:**  
Purification often reveals a flash of humanity — the “lost person” underneath.

---

## **2. Beasts & Wildlife (Deep Forest, Ashroot, rivers)**

**Theme:** instinct + territoriality + fungal mutation  
Examples:

- Blight-haunted Stags (antlers budding with sickly crystals)
    
- Mossback Wolves (halves of their bodies colonized by parasite moss)
    
- Hollow Owls (ribcages hollow, filled with spores)
    

**Behavior:**  
Guard nests of Blight growth; react strongly to Veil tears.

**Oscar Interaction:**  
Purification is harder — may need two applications or radiant + Oscar’s touch.

---

## **3. Plant-Blight Hybrids (Groves, Root Systems, sacred sites)**

**Theme:** parasitic growth + mimicry + slow hunger  
Examples:

- Creeping Root-Terrors
    
- Weeping Tree Parasite Nodes (great hook for your Weeping Tree questline)
    
- Thorn-Crawlers (blight vines using old animal skeletons as scaffolding)
    

**Behavior:**  
Stationary or slow. Often guarding something — a tear, a sigil, an artifact.

**Oscar Interaction:**  
Terrarium reacts most strongly here. Purification always kills 1–3 terrarium plants.

---

## **4. Elemental / Environmental Blight (Caves, rivers, storms, places near Veil rupture)**

**Theme:** physics breaking down  
Examples:

- Blighted Mist (necrotic cloud with memory fragments)
    
- River-Taint (water that animates corpses or trees)
    
- Echo-Stones (rocks vibrating with unstable harmonics, explode violently)
    

**Behavior:**  
Not intelligent, but reactive.

**Oscar Interaction:**  
Purification works only to create a temporary safe zone, not to “heal” the entity.

---

## **5. Witchcraft-Amplified Blight (rare; linked to Eris, covens, major plot arcs)**

**Theme:** ritual purpose + sentience  
Examples:

- Veilmothers
    
- Sorrowbinders (witch-blight constructs)
    
- The Bound Child (late-campaign reveal hook tied to Zennor)
    

**Behavior:**  
Follows witch commands; capable of strategy beyond instinct.

**Oscar Interaction:**  
Purification causes visions, memory bleed, emotional destabilization.

---

# **🧭 How This Affects Session 3 and Beyond**

## **For the Blighted Zombie Encounter**

- The players are now seeing **one subtype**, a humanoid ruin-form.
    
- Later, they’ll notice completely different behaviors in the deep-root Ashroot.
    
- Oscar will realize his terrarium responds differently based on what the Blight “is.”
    

You can even describe:

> “The terrarium glows with a different tone — warmer for beasts, harsher for humanoids, and almost frightened for plants.”

---

## **Oscar’s Role in This System**

- He is not “the chosen one”—he is someone with a _rare harmonic frequency_.
    
- The terrarium acts like a resonant tuning fork that disturbs the Blight’s instability.
    
- The Council of 8 immediately becomes interested in this, because they monitor harmonics and Veil tears.
    

Oscar is a **scientific anomaly** in a magical ecosystem.

---

## **The Council of 8’s Perspective (preview)**

They see the Blight as:

- Not a disease
    
- Not a fungus
    
- Not a curse  
    **But a rupture of magical ecology.**
    

Oscar is:

- Proof the Veil isn’t just tearing
    
- It’s _reacting_
    
- And some people (Oscar) can “interface” with it
    

This gives you a clear reason why the Council will test the group.

---

## **Zennor Tie-In (long-term)**

Some humanoid Blightforms may carry faint echoes recognizable to Zennor, strengthening the amulet’s pull.

When a Blightform is witch-amplified, his amulet may burn cold or even whisper.  
This creates a triangle of meaning:

**Blight ←→ Witches ←→ Eris ←→ Daughter ←→ Zennor**
