# **ASHROOT ARENA — A Tactical Skirmish Game**

A fast 1v1 wartable contest played across the rebel camps and Concord barracks. Each “match” represents a skirmish between small squads.

Players spend gold to hire fighters, secretly assign roles, then clash using d10 rolls.

---

# **1. Setup**

- Each player receives **10 GP**, **12 GP**, or **15 GP** depending on desired game length.
    
- Each selects fighters from the **Unit Roster**.
    
- Fighters are represented by tokens, dice, or minis.
    

---

# **2. Round Structure**

Each round has **three phases**:

### **1) Deployment**

Players place their fighters face-down (or behind a screen), then reveal simultaneously.

### **2) Orders**

Each player may assign:

- **Knight**: _Choose HIT or DEFEND before rolling._
    
- **Others**: No pre-orders unless noted.
    

### **3) Clash Phase**

All attacks resolve simultaneously.

- Any fighter who meets their **Hit Threshold** deals 1 damage to a chosen enemy fighter.
    
- A damaged fighter:
    
    - **If able to sustain injury**, remains in play but gains the listed penalty.
        
    - Otherwise, dies and is removed.
        
- **Healer** triggers after damage (if alive).
    

### **4) End of Round**

If both sides still have fighters, continue to the next round.  
Game ends when one side has no fighters left.

---

# **3. Fighter Roster & Prices**

Balanced around gold scarcity + meaningful upgrades.

## **Knight — 4 GP**

**Reliable Protector / Duelist**

- **HIT Mode:** Hits on **7+**
    
- **DEFEND Mode:** Blocks one hit on **5+**
    
- Must choose mode before rolling.
    
- Strong tactical flexibility, good for screening high-value units.
    

## **Healer — 3 GP**

**Support Unit / Sustain Engine**

- On **5+**, heal **one injured ally** (removes injury penalty, cannot revive dead).
    
- Low offense; best in longer fights or with Champions/Juggernauts.
    

## **Juggernaut — 5 GP**

**Slow, Heavy Bruiser**

- Hits on **8+**
    
- **Can sustain 1 injury** → first injury imposes **–1 to future HIT roll** (so needs 9+).
    
- On death blow, removed.
    
- Excellent frontline meat shield.
    

## **Pawn — 1 GP**

**Swarm Unit**

- Hits on **9+**
    
- Dies immediately on any damage.
    
- Best as screens, lucky finishers, or to overwhelm elite-focused squads.
    

## **Champion — 7 GP**

**Premium Elite Unit**

- Hits on **3+**
    
- Can sustain **one injury** with **no penalty**.
    
- Second injury kills.
    
- Hyper-efficient damage dealer; expensive but defining.
    

## **Wizard — 6 GP**

**Area-Caster / High Risk, High Reward**

- Can target **1 or 2 enemies**.
    
- Needs **7+** to hit each.
    
- Each successful hit deals 1 damage.
    
- Squishy: dies on any injury.
    
- Counters swarm tactics; vulnerable to assassins/knights in Hit mode.
    

---

# **4. Combat Targeting Rules**

- **Each fighter chooses a target** before rolling.
    
- Wizard chooses **up to 2 targets**.
    
- You may focus-fire or spread attacks.
    

---

# **5. Injuries**

Some fighters can sustain injury:

- Injured units remain active next round.
    
- If sustained injury imposes a penalty, apply immediately.
    

**Injury Tracking Tip:** Turn the miniature sideways or place a token under it.

---

# **6. Victory Conditions**

You win when:

- The opponent has no fighters left, **or**
    
- You voluntarily concede when outmatched.
    

Optional tournament scoring:

- **1 point** per match win.
    
- **+1 point** for each surviving unit at end.

# **8. Optional Variants**

### **Fog of War**

Hide fighter types until first damage is dealt.

### **Terrain Modifiers**

Add a 1-square board:

- High ground: +1 to hit
    
- Cover: –1 to be hit (Knights love this)
    

### **Prestige Fighters**

Winning fighters gain +1 to hit in the next game.

# **NEW ARCHETYPES**

## **1. Assassin — 5 GP**

**Precision Killer / Stealth Opener**

Designed to punish high-value units and fragile casters.

**Abilities**

- **Backstab:** On the _first round they roll_, Assassin hits on **6+**.
    
- After the first round, they hit on **8+**.
    
- **Marked Target:** Before rolling, the Assassin chooses **one enemy**. If they hit, that target takes **2 damage** (enough to instantly kill Champions, Juggernauts, or any unit that can’t take two hits).
    
- Dies on any damage (cannot sustain injury).
    

**Role in the meta**

- Threatens Champions and Wizards immediately.
    
- Forces opponents to screen key units with Knights or Pawns.
    
- Weak in prolonged fights.
    

---

## **2. Battle Caller — 4 GP**

**Tactical Commander / Buff Specialist**

A non-attacking support unit that manipulates the squad’s effectiveness.

**Abilities**

- **Command Aura:** Choose **one of your fighters** before rolling.  
    That fighter gains **+1 to their hit roll** for this round.
    
- **Rallying Shout (once per match):** At the start of any round, give **all your units** +1 to hit this round.
    
- If the Battle Caller is killed, all allies immediately lose the buff.
    

**Stats**

- Does **not attack**.
    
- Dies on any damage.
    

**Role in the meta**

- Increases reliability of low-roll units (Pawns, Juggernauts).
    
- Creates powerful one-round burst turns with Champion or Wizard.
    
- Opponent must decide whether to focus them early or endure their buffs.
    

---

# **How They Fit the Game's Balance**

### **Assassin**

- Answers **Champions** and **Wizards**.
    
- Price sits between Wizard and Knight for fairness.
    
- High early-round impact → declines over time.
    

### **Battle Caller**

- Introduces _team synergy_.
    
- Balances buffing with fragility.
    
- Very strong when paired with:
    
    - Wizard (hitting 6+ for two targets)
        
    - Champion (hitting 2+)
        
    - Juggernaut (more consistent)
