### **The Edge of the Ashroot**

The land bleeds out from Orrenval’s clear-cut ring, where stumps and splintered trunks jut from the soil like broken teeth. The air still smells faintly of ash and iron — the ghost of the forges that fed the Concord’s hunger. Early light seeps through a pale dawn mist, cold and colorless, pooling in the furrows carved by wagon wheels long abandoned.

You follow the remnants of an old logging road — rutted, half-claimed by moss and root — where rusted saws hang from forgotten scaffolds and felled pines lie in ordered rows, rotting in silence. The forest here feels _held_. No wind stirs. No bird dares speak. Every sound your party makes — each footfall, each drawn breath — feels far too loud beneath the watch of invisible eyes.

Somewhere behind, the walls of Orrenval still burn faintly in your memory; ahead, the treeline swallows the path whole. You cross into it knowing you have left the last light of the Concord behind.

### 🎭 **After the Read-Aloud**

_(Give a 5–10 second pause before speaking again — let the silence land.)_

Then, in a lower, quieter tone:

> The road ahead is narrow, and the forest presses close on both sides.  
> The air’s cold enough to bite, but you can still feel the warmth of the city fires somewhere behind you.  
> You’ve been walking for maybe half an hour now — the sound of your footsteps and the creak of your gear filling the quiet.
> 
> What does that silence feel like for each of you?  
> Who breaks it first?

_(Let them respond — they might describe posture, pace, muttering, etc. Build from that.)_

---

### 🗣️ **Follow-Up Prompts to Spark Dialogue**

You can drop one or two of these depending on their energy level:

1. **On Escape:**
    
    > When you glance back through the mist, what thought sticks with you most about your actions in Orrenval? Regret? Relief?
    
2. **On the Road:**
    
    > Who’s leading the group down the path, and how does everyone else fall in behind them?
    
3. **On Trust:**
    
    > You’ve all just fled the same danger — but do you trust the people walking beside you?
    
4. **On the Land Itself:**
    
    > The forest feels _watched_. Does that make you more cautious… or more defiant?
    
5. **On Quiet Moments:**
    
    > After a while, your boots fall into rhythm. If someone speaks to break that rhythm, what do they say?
    

---

### 🪶 **Optional Immersion Beat**

If they’re slow to open up, you can narrate a small sensory cue:

> A crow croaks somewhere deep in the woods, echoing once before silence folds back in.  
> The sound hangs between you, like a question waiting for someone to answer it.

### **The Deep Path**

Hours pass, and the scars begin to fade. The rhythm of your boots turns soft, swallowed by damp earth and a rising breath of life beneath it. The trees here grow taller, their bark veined with pale silver where dawnlight breaks through the canopy. The air no longer smells of iron but of loam and cold sap; each inhale feels older than your lungs.

The undergrowth hums with unseen motion — the rustle of ferns, the whisper of branches bending where no wind moves. Once-orderly clearings have collapsed into living labyrinths of vine and thorn, and the road itself has become more memory than stone. The forest seems to _listen_, and when you pause, it feels as if it waits to see what you will do next.

Then you begin to notice small wrongnesses. A leaf that turns to ash when touched. The faint shimmer of dust in the air, pulsing as though breathing. A ring of mushrooms blackened at their edges. These are not marks of Concord — but of something deeper, older, waking beneath the roots.

Somewhere ahead, the Ashroot calls — a low note that vibrates in the ribs and never quite resolves. Whatever lies within, it is _alive_, and it has noticed you.
