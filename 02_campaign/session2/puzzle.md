## **Scene: The Echoing Reliquary**

Song: Cinematic Documentary Underscore Strings
### **Location Description**

Deep in a forgotten dell, surrounded by skeletal birch trees, lies a **stone monolith half-sunken into the soil** — its surface worn smooth except for faint carvings that seem to shimmer when touched by light. The air here is unnaturally still; even the birds do not sing.

Those who approach hear an **almost imperceptible hum** under their feet — a resonant frequency that seems to match the rhythm of their own heartbeat.

> **Read-aloud description:**
> 
> “The grove is silent but not quiet. Beneath the stillness hums something deep — a note that thrums through the soles of your boots. The moss-covered stone ahead is old, carved with the faint outline of a sigil you’ve seen before… the mark of the Cindersong. Something in this place is waiting to be heard.”

## **Investigation Clues**

### **1. The Sigil (DC 14 Investigation or Arcana)**

The sigil carved into the monolith is the **Cindersong emblem**, shaped like a spiral of three tongues of flame, each ending in a small musical glyph.

- A successful **Arcana** check reveals that the glyphs correspond to **musical notes**: A, D, and F.
    
- A **Religion** check may recall that Cindersong relics often react to sound, especially **harmony and rhythm**.
    
[[cindersong_sigil.png]]
---

### **2. The Torn Parchment (DC 12 Investigation / found nearby)**

Hidden beneath a patch of roots or half-buried under leaves, the party finds a **fragment of parchment**, the edges charred as though once part of a songbook. The parchment shows a few bars of **musical notation** — incomplete — and a short, cryptic inscription written beneath in flowing script:

> **“The voice of flame is never alone.  
> Three notes for three tongues,  
> One must rise as the other falls.”**

_(Optional Arcana or Performance DC 14 check)_

- A character who succeeds recognizes that the phrase “rise as the others fall” could refer to **a melody where one note ascends in pitch while the others descend** — or **a simple three-note harmony sung in sequence**.
    

---

### **3. Environmental Clues (Perception DC 13)**

Several **stone reliefs** around the grove depict figures singing toward a column of flame.

- If someone speaks, hums, or sings near the monolith, the hum beneath the ground **responds faintly**, shifting in pitch or intensity.

## 🎶 **Torn Note Clue: “The Third Tongue”**

**Physical description:**  
A small, irregular scrap of parchment, singed at one corner and damp from the forest air. The ink has bled in places, but fragments of musical symbols and poetic lines remain. It looks like it was once part of a larger page — perhaps a hymn, ritual chant, or coded message.

> _Faint writing can be made out between the stains and burn marks:_
> 
> _“…each flame must find its voice—  
> One to rise, one to fall, one to hold true.  
> Alone they are silence; together, a song.”_
> 
> _Below the words are three small glyphs shaped like stylized tongues of fire, each pointing in a different direction. One curves upward, one downward, and one straight ahead._
> 
> _(On a successful DC 13 Arcana or Performance check)_ the player realizes these markings could correspond to **three voices or tones** that must harmonize — the upward tongue representing a higher note, the downward a lower, and the straight one a central pitch.

### 🗝️ **Narrative Use**

You can place this scrap:

- Half-buried under leaves near the **monolith’s base**.
    
- In the mouth of a **small woodland creature’s den**, as if stolen.
    
- Wedged between two **roots or stones**, gently singed.
    
- Or even discovered earlier in the session — later revealed to be key once the players find the monolith.

## **The Puzzle Solution**

To unlock the secret chamber, the players must **replicate the melody of the Cindersong** by humming or playing the correct notes.

- The sequence corresponds to the **A–D–F** tones — but it must be **performed with a rise-fall pattern**. First all three hold the note A then one goes up to D and then another drops to F.
    
- If they hum dissonantly, the hum deepens and the ground vibrates — a warning before the puzzle resets.
    
- When they get it right, the monolith’s carvings **ignite with a faint ember glow**, and the ground rumbles as **a circular stone hatch** opens at its base.
    

> _“As your voices weave in harmony, the hum grows stronger until it feels like the grove itself is breathing with you. The sigil flares — molten lines tracing through the stone — and with a hiss of steam, the earth parts to reveal a descending stair of blackened glass.”_

## **The Reward Chamber: The Ember Vault**

Inside lies a small stone chamber lined with red crystal veins that pulse like living embers. The air smells faintly of incense and ash. Three pedestals hold relics — one for each “tongue of flame.”

### **Treasure Options (Choose 1–3):**

1. **The Emberheart Pendant**
    
    - _Wondrous Item (Rare)_
        
    - Once per day, when you take fire damage, you can absorb up to 10 points of it, storing it as glowing heat. As a bonus action, release it to deal that same amount of fire damage in a 10 ft radius.
        
2. **Song of the Veiled Choir** _(Scroll or Lyric Fragment)_
    
    - _Spell Scroll (3rd level, can be cast by humming or speaking aloud)_
        
    - When read or sung, casts _Silence_ in a 30-ft radius, but those within can still hear each other’s heartbeats — as if in sync.
        
    - Can be learned by Bards as a special tune, granting advantage on Performance checks tied to the Cindersong.
        
3. **Cindersong Blade** _(Weapon, requires attunement)_
    
    - When swung, it emits a harmonic vibration. Deals an additional 1d4 thunder damage on hit.
        
    - Once per long rest, can emit a **“resonant strike”**, releasing a 15-ft cone of concussive sound (DC 15 Con save or be knocked prone).
        

Here are descriptive, in-world style narrations for each item — evocative enough to intrigue your players without spelling out the mechanics. Each reads as something you could narrate aloud when the party discovers, studies, or first wields the object.

---

### **The Emberheart Pendant**

> The pendant is warm even before it’s touched — a slow, living warmth that seeps through the air around it.  
> At its center rests a shard of amber glass, and within that glass, a fleck of red light pulses faintly, like a slumbering ember that never fades. When the wearer draws near open flame, the pendant seems to stir — the glow quickens, its warmth deepens, and for a heartbeat the air hums with a faint tremor of heat.  
> Those who have carried it speak of dreams filled with smoke and crackling whispers, as if something within the pendant is remembering the fires it has known.

---

### **Song of the Veiled Choir** _(Scroll or Lyric Fragment)_

> The parchment hums with an inaudible vibration — more _felt_ than heard, as if the ink itself hums against the skin. Its notation is strange: looping sigils and breath marks that seem to suggest both melody and heartbeat.  
> When whispered or hummed, the sound dies unnaturally in the air, swallowed by stillness — yet in that silence, you can feel the pulse of those nearby. It’s as though the song binds listeners together, not by sound, but by rhythm.  
> Some say the tune was first sung by those who wished to speak freely in the midst of fear — a hymn meant not to be heard, but _felt_.

---

### **The Cindersong Blade**

> Forged from blackened steel laced with fine, copper-red veining, this blade hums faintly when drawn — a tone that rises and falls with the wielder’s motion, like a single note searching for harmony.  
> When it strikes, the air ripples; dust and leaves stir as if the world itself flinched. To some, the hum grows louder with each swing — not from the blade, but from somewhere deeper, as though unseen voices were trying to match its pitch.  
> Those who wield it too long report that the sword’s song begins to echo their heartbeat — and that when it finally crescendos, silence follows.

## **Alternative Outcomes / Fail States**

- **If the players hum the wrong melody three times**, the sigil flashes with heat and releases a wave of psychic dissonance — each creature makes a DC 14 Wisdom save or takes 2d6 psychic damage and hears whispering voices saying:
    
    > “The flame does not sing for the faithless.”
    
- Clever players could instead **record the hum with magic** (like _Minor Illusion_) and attempt to match it precisely, bypassing the musical interpretation.
    

---

## **Atmospheric Additions**

- When they leave the chamber, the grove remains humming faintly, and if they return later in the campaign, they might hear the tune again — hummed by someone else nearby.
    
- The parchment’s reverse side could contain the faint outline of **another Cindersong location**, teasing future discoveries.
