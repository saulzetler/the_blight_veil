## 🧩 Scene Purpose & Mood (Recap of Step 1)

**Tone:** desperate, tense, and unifying.  
**Player takeaway:** “These rebels _fight like hell_—and they’ve just bled beside us.”  
**NPC takeaway:** “These outsiders stood their ground; they’re worth trusting.”

## 🏕 Step 2 – Scene Setup: The Riverside Camp

### **Setting Description (Read-aloud flavor)**

> The river runs slow and black beneath a shroud of fog.  
> Lantern light glints off wet stones where the rebels have set a small fire behind a screen of reeds.  
> The night air hums with insects and the hush of running water.

### **Structure**

|Player Approach|DM Beat|Consequence|
|---|---|---|
|**They keep watch** (passive Perception ≥ 13 or active DC 15)|Hear distant bootfalls, metallic clink, or see light across the river.|They can set traps, douse the fire, prep an ambush, or hide (Stealth vs Perception + 3).|
|**They relax**|The first indication is a barking order and the flash of radiant light slicing through the fog.|The patrol begins in optimal position; one rebel NPC takes a surprise round wound.|

**Environmental details**

- Muddy bank → half movement unless on the rocky ridge.
    
- River = difficult terrain (Dex save DC 12 or be swept 10 ft downstream per round).
    
- Sparse cover (fallen logs, tents, overturned boats).

## 💡 Step 4 – Signature Mechanic: _The Lantern Line_

### **Concept**

The Concord deploy radiant lanterns that project cones or auras of blinding light. The battlefield becomes a shifting lattice of visibility and danger.

**Base Rules**

- **Three Lanterns** at the start, placed in a rough line across the camp (20-ft-radius bright light, 20-ft dim beyond).
    
- Any _Ashroot_ or _party_ creature that begins its turn in bright light takes **2 radiant damage** (DC 12 Con save half).
    
- While in bright light, creatures **can’t hide** and have **disadvantage on Stealth**.
    
- Breaking a lantern (AC 13, HP 10) creates a **flash** (Dex save DC 13 or blinded 1 round).
    
- Each round on Commander’s turn, he can **Reposition Lantern (Line Formation)**—move any lantern 15 ft as hovering drone.
    

**Tactics**

- Rebels try to fight from shadow; Concord try to corral enemies into the light.
    
- River mist counts as _dim light_—players may shove or lure enemies into it.
    

---

### **Legendary-Style Actions (Commander Verrin Kael)**

He can take **1 legendary action** after another creature’s turn (2 per round max):

|Action|Effect|
|---|---|
|**Commanding Radiance (1 action)**|Move one lantern up to 15 ft and immediately emit a **pulse**: all creatures within 10 ft make Con save DC 13 or take 1d8 radiant and be **revealed** (no Stealth until end of next turn).|
|**Parry & Riposte (1 action)**|+2 AC until start of his next turn; if a melee attack misses him, he makes 1 attack back.|
|**“Hold the Line!” (1 action)**|One ally within 30 ft can make a weapon attack as a reaction or move half speed without provoking.|

If he’s blight-infused (Phase 2), replace _Commanding Radiance_ with:

> **Veil Surge (1 action):** Lanterns flicker dark; all light zones invert into shadow for 1 round. His blade attacks deal necrotic instead of radiant.

## 🎭 Step 5 – Flexible Introduction

Keep this encounter **modular**:

- **Foreshadowed:** If players pass Perception, give them 1–2 rounds prep (hide, set snares, quietly awaken rebels).
    
- **Immediate Ambush:** Lanterns flare on, initiative instantly; use the surprise mechanic.
    
- **Partial Ambush:** One lantern ignites prematurely—half the camp awake, half flat-footed.
    

This flexibility means you can let _player attentiveness_ decide the opening tension.

## 💀 Step 6 – Aftermath & Rewards

**Rebel Reactions**

- Surviving rebels clasp the PCs’ hands, bloody but grateful.
    
- If any rebel died, there’s a quiet riverside burial at dawn. A few tokens left on the cairn.
    

**Loot**

- **From Commander Kael:**
    
    - _Lantern of Veil-Ward Light_ (Uncommon Wondrous Item)
        
        - Emits 20-ft bright/20-ft dim light.
            
        - Once per long rest: as a bonus action, turn the light pitch-black for 1 minute, creating _magical darkness_ that only the bearer can see through.
            
    - 40 gp in Concord scrip; broken badge.
        
- **Others:**
    
    - Purifier’s silver holy symbol (10 gp).
        
    - Scout’s horn (usable for signal or later story clue).
        
    - Mixed rations, ammo, and 15 gp total.
        

**Story Payoff**

- The rebels spread word of the party’s bravery.
    
- Their next contact in Ashroot treats them as proven allies.
    
- The recovered lantern might later be recognized by higher-ranking Concord agents.

## 🌙 **Version 1 — Victory**

> The final blow lands with a metallic crack.  
> Captain Kael staggers, lantern light bleeding through the cracks in his armor.  
> For a heartbeat, his form glows from within — the white of his Concord sigil flickering, twisting — and then it gutters out.  
> He collapses to his knees by the river’s edge, blade sinking into the mud.  
> The water takes him, pulling his reflection apart in ripples.
> 
> Around you, the Lantern Line falters. One by one, the beacons flare and dim, their light fading into smoke that drifts low across the water.  
> The fog closes in again.
> 
> No one speaks.  
> The rebels stand motionless, faces cut with mud and blood, their breath steaming in the chill.
> 
> The only sound is the slow hiss of the river reclaiming its dark.
> 
> Somewhere in that quiet, the first hint of trust passes between you and them — wordless, fragile, real.
> 
> And for the first time tonight, the light is gone.

## ⚡ **Version 2 — Escape**

> The river explodes with motion — shouting, splintered light, the crack of steel against shield.  
> A voice screams _“Fall back!”_ as a lantern bursts overhead, showering sparks into the fog.
> 
> The Commander’s corrupted glow tears through the haze behind you, his sword swinging arcs of sickly light that flash against the reeds.
> 
> Then you’re running.  
> Feet hammering through mud, the world reduced to the sound of breath and water and the ghosts of your own heartbeats.
> 
> Behind you, the Lantern Line burns like a broken spine across the shore — lights flaring, sputtering, dying one by one until only darkness remains.  
> The last thing you see is the river swallowing the glow, until even that is gone.
> 
> You and the rebels collapse in the fog, the night closing around you.  
> No words. Just the quiet knowledge that you lived, and they did too.
> 
> The river keeps whispering — steady, endless, indifferent — as the scene fades to black.
