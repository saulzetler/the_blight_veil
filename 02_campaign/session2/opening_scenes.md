## 🌲 ACT I: The Road Through Ashroot

### **Scene 1 – The Defiled Shrine of the Dawn**

**Tone:** desecration, quiet guilt, fading faith

#### **Setup**

A few hours into their flight from Orrenval, the group follows a mud track through thinning mist. The trees are unnaturally tall, their canopies blotting out most of the sun. The first sign of civilization comes in the form of a broken shrine to **Lathander** — Fergie’s god, the Radiant Dawn.

#### **Read-Aloud Description**

> The path bends through a hollow where the light struggles to reach.  
> Half-buried in moss lies a small roadside altar — stone cracked, gilding worn away.  
> Once, a sunburst symbol crowned its face, but now it’s been **scored through with black pitch**, the rays of dawn marred into curling, ashen tendrils.  
> The offering bowl is filled with rainwater turned red by rust, and a single Concord insignia dangles from a branch above — a warning, or a signature.

#### **DM Guidance**

Let the silence breathe. Then turn to Fergie’s player:

> “You recognize the prayer lines beneath the grime — the same words you once recited at sunrise. How does it feel to see the Light defiled like this?”

Encourage Fergie to respond emotionally or ritually: cleaning it, praying, breaking it further, ignoring it — each tells the table who Fergie is.

To deepen the moment, another player might notice faint footprints near the shrine — suggesting someone else still tends it, despite the desecration.

**Optional Hook:**  
A faint warmth still lingers under the ruin; a successful _Religion_ or _Arcana_ check could reveal traces of radiant magic twisted into shadow. Someone _wanted_ the Light to suffer.

### **Scene 2 – Rusted Concord Metal**

**Tone:** memory, identity, unease

#### **Setup**

Not long after the shrine, the group passes through a patch of fog where something metallic gleams.

#### **Read-Aloud Description**

> The undergrowth parts around the shape of a rusted **Concord breastplate**, half-swallowed by earth and roots.  
> The armor bears the faint sigil of the Concord — sunburst over scales — though the emblem has been scraped almost to nothing.  
> Inside the breastplate, dried vines have grown through the ribs of the corpse that once wore it.

#### **DM Guidance**

Turn to Oscar’s player:

> “You recognize the make — this armor was standard issue to Concord soldiers, likely from the same foundries that forged your own plating. Do you feel familiarity… or revulsion?”

Encourage introspection:

- Does Oscar kneel to inspect it?
    
- Does he feel the pull of memory or programming?
    
- Does he see himself in this rusted relic?
    

If other players engage, they might ask about his serial number (“05C4R”) or the moment he was awakened — which naturally transitions into shared character talk.

**Optional Hook:**  
A _Perception_ check reveals faint etching under the rust — the same three-ring sigil of Cindersong, freshly carved.  
Someone might have hidden the body here intentionally.

### **Scene 3 – The Roadside Wounds (Combined: Dying Soldier + New NPC)**

**Tone:** moral dilemma, humanity in collapse, interrogation of ideals

#### **Setup**

As night approaches, the forest path opens into a clearing strewn with the wreckage of a small skirmish. A **Concord supply wagon** has been overturned. Cindersong graffiti marks its side — recently painted. Two soldiers lie dead, one dying. A fifth figure sits by the wreck: an armed traveler, ragged but alert. This will be your **new NPC** — a field medic or deserter named [[iria_cael]], a woman in her thirties with sun-scorched skin and a calm, suspicious manner.

#### **Read-Aloud Description**

> The scent of blood and iron taints the damp air. A shattered wagon lies beside the road, its crates splintered open.  
> Two Concord soldiers are sprawled nearby, pale and still. A third gasps weakly, hand pressed to a stomach wound.  
> Beside him kneels a woman with her hands slick with crimson — not in armor, not Cindersong, just a traveler with a healer’s kit and eyes that weigh every one of you before she speaks.  
> “If you’ve come to finish the job,” she says quietly, “at least let him die clean.”

#### **DM Guidance**

Let the table pause here — this is your _moral crucible_.

- If they choose to **help**, the act might cost them stealth or resources but earn Iria’s trust.
    
- If they **walk away**, Iria condemns them: “You’re no better than them.”
    
- If they **kill him**, she doesn’t stop them — but later questions _why_.
    

After the choice, Iria becomes your vehicle for **pointed questions**:

> “You talk of justice. But whose fire burned in the square?”  
> “You saved that heretic woman, but you left a soldier to rot. What kind of light do you follow?”  
> “That one—” _(nods to Oscar)_ “—you’ve built him in their image. Don’t you see it?”

Encourage the party to justify themselves, to each other and to her.  
She’s not a villain — just someone who’s seen too much and wants to know what they stand for.

**Optional Hooks:**

- If they save the soldier, his dying words hint that Rellis survived and is hunting them.
    
- If they kill him, his blood seeps into the ground, and for a brief moment, **the Veil hums** — as if feeding on his death.
    
- Iria may later reappear — healer, informant, or moral foil — depending on how they treated her.

## ⚙️ **DM Notes: Tone and Flow**

- Keep pacing **slow and deliberate**. These aren’t obstacles — they’re emotional mirrors.
    
- Between scenes, describe the forest subtly changing: trees more twisted, sunlight duller, air thick with gold dust — suggesting the Veil’s proximity grows.
    
- Use **ambient silence** as a cue — the fewer sounds, the more players fill the space with character.
    

**Scene Order Suggestion:**

1. Shrine (Faith tested)
    
2. Rusted Armor (Identity questioned)
    
3. Wagon & Iria (Morality confronted)
    

By the end of these, your players will have spoken their beliefs out loud — to the world, to one another, and to themselves — without ever needing a campfire monologue.
