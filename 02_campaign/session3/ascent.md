# 🌳 **ASCENT TO THE CROWN — DM GUIDE**

_The Social Gauntlet Between the Market & the Council Trial_

## **Purpose**

Before the party can reach the Heartwood Crown and the Trial Chamber, Rootspire requires them to:

- navigate increasingly restricted levels
    
- speak with wardens, apprentices, and shapers
    
- prove their intentions socially
    
- handle suspicion about their connection to the Veil
    
- experience the city’s living architecture growing more reactive as they climb
    

This path is **not combat**—it’s a narrative bottleneck that rewards charisma, clever talk, or creative storytelling.

---

# **STRUCTURE**

The ascent is broken into **four gates**, each with a scene, checks, consequences, and roleplay prompts:

1. **The Mid-Spiral Watch** – subtle screening
    
2. **The Heartwood Threshold** – formal questioning
    
3. **The Upper Bough Ward** – emotional + Veil-sensitive test
    
4. **The Crown Gate** – final persuasion before the trial
    

Each section has a **Read-Aloud**, **NPC demeanor**, **checks**, and **fail-forward consequences** that still guide them upward, but with tonal shifts.

---

# 🌿 **1. MID-SPIRAL WATCH**

_(Just above the Market; casual but alert)_

### **Read-Aloud**

> The spiral walkway narrows as you climb. Lantern-fungus glows in grooves along the bark, and two figures step from a hollowed alcove—light armor, wooden masks, spears of carved ashroot.  
> A soft vibration passes through the rail beneath your hands, as if the tree is paying attention.

### **Who They Are**

**Lira and Heron**, the same watchful Gatewardens who observed the party in the Market. Still calm—but more curious now.

### **What They Want**

- to confirm the party hasn’t caused trouble
    
- to check if anyone’s magic reacted strangely
    
- to make sure the group intends to seek the Boughwarden and not wander the inner levels unescorted
    

### **Checks**

- **Persuasion (DC 12)**: Friendly passage, mild curiosity.
    
- **Deception (DC 14)**: They don’t buy it entirely, but allow passage.
    
- **Performance (DC 12)**: A respectful gesture or explanation impresses them.
    
- **Intimidation** immediately worsens your reputation but doesn’t stop the climb.
    

### **Fail-Forward**

If they sense deception/conflict:

- They escort the party upward at a distance.
    
- They whisper to the next checkpoint, raising suspicion (higher DC +1 later).
    

### **Flavor Notes**

If Oscar’s terrarium glows here, Lira leans in:

> “The tree hummed when you stepped near. That is… unusual.”

# 🌲 **2. HEARTWOOD THRESHOLD**

_(Between the middle city and the administrative level)_

This is a carved interior hallway lit with vein-like saplight. More solemn, more official.

### **Read-Aloud**

> The stairway opens into a ribbed chamber of living wood. The ceiling bows like an enormous leaf, and soft amber lines pulse slowly across the walls. Three wardens wait here—robes woven from root-fibers, staves marked with glowing sigils.  
> They study you with quiet intensity.

### **Who They Are**

The **Heartwardens**, attendants to Boughwarden Thalan. Pragmatic, neutral.

### **What They Want**

- to evaluate the party’s **purpose**
    
- to ensure they understand Rootspire is not a Concord stronghold
    
- to confirm intent to meet Thalan, not bypass him
    

### **Checks**

Choose one approach:

- **State purpose plainly** → **Insight (DC 12)** reveals they appreciate honesty.
    
- **Persuasion (DC 13)** → they trust the group’s motives.
    
- **Deception (DC 15)** → they allow passage but mark the group internally.
    
- **Show a trinket reacting** → **Arcana/Nature (DC 12)** impresses them.
    

### **Fail-Forward**

If they aren’t convinced:

- They insist on a brief audience with Thalan **now**, not later.
    
- This accelerates the story but makes Thalan more cautious (slightly harsher tone).
    

### **Read-Aloud – Passing Through**

> The Heartwardens step aside as the saplight brightens. The path upward tightens, the air cooling as you climb toward the higher boughs.

# 🌬 **3. THE UPPER BOUGH WARD**

_(Windy ledges, living branches, unstable harmonics)_

### **Read-Aloud**

> The walkway opens to a high perch where branches sway in the shifting breeze. Sigils etched into the bark shimmer faintly as you step close, pulsing in time with the wind.  
> A lone apprentice stands waiting—nervous, wide-eyed, clutching a carved staff that hums softly.

### **Who They Are**

**Selune of the Knots**, the Rootshaper Apprentice from the Market, called up via rumor or intuition.

### **What They Want**

- to confirm the strange harmonic shift connected to the party
    
- to observe Oscar’s terrarium
    
- to understand Zennor’s amulet
    
- to know whether the group fears or seeks the Council
    

### **Checks**

This scene is **more emotional** than bureaucratic.

- **Persuasion (DC 12)**: Calm and honest answers reassure her.
    
- **Arcana (DC 13)**: Explain trinket interactions in a way she can follow.
    
- **Insight (DC 12)**: Understand her anxiety—she fears messing this up.
    
- **Intimidation** → immediate distrust; she sends word that the party is “discordant.”
    

### **Fail-Forward**

If they spook her:

- She steps back, touching a harmonic sigil that briefly flares blue.
    
- A tremor runs through the branch beneath them.
    
- Selune runs ahead to warn Thalan.
    

The party still may ascend—but the Crown Gate wardens will be tense.

### **Read-Aloud – Ascent Beyond**

> The wind shifts colder as the pathway narrows. The bark beneath your boots becomes smoother, older, almost glassy with age.

# 🌟 **4. THE CROWN GATE**

_(Final barrier before the Trial Chamber)_

### **Read-Aloud**

> The path ends at a tall arch of intertwined branches grown into a natural doorway. Boughwarden Thalan stands before it—bark-plate armor, staff embedded with dim white sap-stones.  
> The doorway behind him remains closed, a seamless sheet of grown wood.

### **Who he is**

Thalan a warden who maintains the harmonic locks of the Council’s domain.

### **What he Wants**

- final confirmation the party is ready for the Eightfold Resonance
    
- reassurance the group understands the gravity of entering the Crown
    
- confidence that the party has not lied or hidden dangerous intent
    

### **Checks – A Social Gauntlet**

Each PC gets a moment to speak or demonstrate intent.

Options (choose one per PC):

- **Persuasion (DC 14)**: Formal request for audience.
    
- **Honesty** (auto-success if sincere, judged by roleplay).
    
- **Deception (DC 15)**: Only works if motive is noble.
    
- **Performance (DC 13)**: Harmonic hum or respectful gesture.
    
- **Nature/Arcana (DC 14)**: Show understanding of Veil instability.
    

### **Success**

> The sap-stones flare in unison.  
> The doorway splits down the center, roots curling away like unfurling petals.  
> Warm amber light spills from within—waiting.

### **Mixed / Fail**

The doorway still opens, but:

- harmonic glow is colder
    
- the chamber hums in lower tones
    
- Council Trial DCs increase by 1
    
- one manifestation may appear more skeptical
    

This ties directly into the mood described in the Trial

# 🌑 **TRANSITION TO THE TRIAL**

### **Read-Aloud (Leading Into the Trial Chamber)**

> The passage narrows as you step inside. The air grows still.  
> The hum in the wood deepens, vibrating in your bones.  
> Eight faint sigils glow across the curved chamber walls—waiting.  
> No Council member stands within.  
> Only presence.
> 
> The trial begins.

(Then you move directly into your Eightfold Resonance Trial.)
