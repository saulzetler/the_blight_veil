Organized by stall, with 5–10 items each. Prices are meant to feel “tight” at 20 GP but still allow fun purchases.

# 💰 **Suggested GM Pricing Adjustments**

You can shift prices based on how the party behaves in the market:

### **If the party is polite / helpful / curious**

- Reduce all prices by **10–20%**
    
- Nyris or Selune throws in bonus herbs or sap-pebbles
    

### **If they’re rude / suspicious / weapon-drawn**

- Increase prices by **20–30%**
    
- Certain merchants refuse to sell them anything magical

# 🌿 **Higher-Value Market Items (20–50 GP)**

### _Available only from specialty merchants (Nyris, Root-Carvers, Mossfire Corner)._

---

## 🔶 **1. Ember-Sap Filament Coil** – **25 GP**

A thin cord of hardened orange sap wound around a bone spindle.

- **Use (1/day):** As a bonus action, you can heat the filament to cut through rope, vines, or soft wood.
    
- **Combat Use:** Deal 1 fire damage to an adjacent creature or object you press it against.
    
- **Notes:** Great for improvisation, infiltration, or wilderness challenges.
    

---

## 🟦 **2. Whisper-Resin Charm** – **30 GP**

A palm-sized charm that suppresses sound in a soft radius.

- **Effect:** Once per long rest, activate to muffle noise in a **5-ft radius** around you for **1 minute**.
    
    - Speech becomes whisper-level only.
        
    - Useful for stealth checks or secret conversations.
        
- **Not Silence:** Spells and combat aren’t blocked; it just reduces volume.
    

---

## 🌫️ **3. Lumin-Moth Lantern** – **35 GP**

A jar containing a single bioluminescent “moth” bound by soft sigils.

- **Effect:** Emits bright light (10 ft) and dim light (10 ft).
    
- **Special Action (1/day):** Release a flash that imposes **disadvantage on one enemy’s next Perception check** or ability to spot the party.
    
- **Recharges:** The moth “settles” back into the jar after 1 hour.
    

---

## 🟤 **4. Rootshaper’s Climbing Spurs** – **30 GP**

Curved wooden spurs grown to fit boots.

- **Effect:** Advantage on **Athletics checks to climb wooden or natural surfaces**.
    
- **Bonus:** Reduce fall damage by **1d4** if falling along bark or branches.
    

---

## 🌕 **5. Sap-Glass Prism (Veil-Sensitive)** – **40 GP**

A triangular shard of refined sap-glass.

- **Use (Action):** Hold it up to magical sigils, harmonics, or illusions.
    
    - Grants **advantage on Arcana checks** to analyze or dispel natural/sigil-based magic.
        
- **Special:** Shows faint blue cracks when the Veil trembles.
    

---

## 🧡 **6. Mossfire Pocket Stove** – **22 GP**

A tiny folding stove powered by smoldering mossfire pellets.

- **Effect:** Creates fire/heat instantly without fuel.
    
- **Skill Benefit:** Advantage on **Survival checks** for cooking, drying gear, or avoiding exposure.
    
- **Combat Use:** You _can_ try to use it creatively, but it deals only 1 fire damage.
    

---

## 📘 **7. Apprentice’s Knot-Grimoire (Nonmagical)** – **28 GP**

A small handbook showing knot-forms used by Rootshapers.

- **Benefit:** When investigating **natural sigils, Rootspire structures, or tree-harmonics**, gain **+1 on Arcana or Investigation checks.**
    
- **Roleplay Bonus:** Locals respect those who know the craft.
    

---

## 🔵 **8. Concord Salvage Lens (Cracked)** – **45 GP**

A damaged scouting lens taken off a fallen scout.

- **Effect:** Grants the ability to see **twice as far** in dim light for 10 minutes (2/day).
    
- **Drawback:** When activated, a faint blue blink appears—Concord design, noticeable on a failed Stealth check (DC 12).
    

---

## 🪵 **9. Bound-Root Buckler (Wooden Shield Upgrade)** – **50 GP**

A wooden shield reinforced with magically grown fibers.

- **Stats:** Functions as a normal shield, but once per long rest you can:
    
    - **Reduce bludgeoning damage by 2** from one attack that hits you.
        
- **Not magical:** Doesn’t bypass resistances or raise AC beyond normal +2.
    

---

## ✴️ **10. Singing Hollow Stone** – **32 GP**

A smooth river stone with a hollow inside that resonates softly.

- **Use (Bonus Action):** Hum into it to create a **1-round distraction**, granting:
    
    - Advantage to one party member’s next **Deception** or **Performance** check.
        
- **Once per short rest.**
