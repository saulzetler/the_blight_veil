# 🌳 **Rootspire Market – DM Guide**

_For when the party explores the bustling trade ridge of the great tree-city._

[[rootspire_market.png]]

---

## 🔰 **Scene Overview**

The Rootspire Market sits along a broad, spiraling root-ridge at the city’s mid-lower level. It’s where Rootspire’s practical craftsmanship meets old magic that hums quietly beneath every surface. This is a **safe but politically tense** space—locals are curious, but protective.

The market is the best place for the party to:

- gather rumors
    
- pick up side quests
    
- interact with notable Rootspire NPCs
    
- feel the living city react to them
    
- see signs of Veil-instability
    
- glimpse tensions with Concord scouts
    

---

# 🎭 **Read-Aloud: Entering the Market**

> The root-ridge widens into a sprawling marketplace layered across living wood. Small stalls cling to the curling roots like barnacles—woven baskets of herbs, carved tools, glowing sap-glass vials.
> 
> The air smells of moss, smoke, and sweet resin. Faint harmonic tones drift from somewhere deep in the trunk, threading through conversations like a second, quieter language.
> 
> Lantern-fungus glows softly overhead. Children dart between stalls; hunters return with game; a trader carefully hangs a broken Concord shield like a quiet challenge.
> 
> The entire market feels alert—watching you as much as you’re watching it.

---

# 🌾 **Market Geography & Points of Interest**

- **Saplight Aisle:** potion stalls, herbal goods, moss-brews, glowing vials
    
- **Root-Carver Row:** tools, bows, wooden fetishes, sigil-etched charms
    
- **Hunter’s Walk:** forest meats, hides, trophies (including Concord relics)
    
- **Mossfire Corner:** subtle magical goods, Veil-reactive botanicals
    
- **Overlook Bridge:** vantage point over lower levels; children often sit here
    
- **Veil-Scarred Knot:** a patch of bark faintly discolored from recent tremors
    

---

# 🧰 **Skill Checks in the Market**

### **Market-Wide Checks**

- **Perception (DC 12):** Notice harmonic vibrations, subtle saplight flares, people watching the party closely.
    
- **Insight (DC 12):** Most locals are cautious but not unfriendly; they’re quietly gauging the party’s intentions.
    
- **Investigation (DC 13):** Spot hidden sigil carvings beneath stall counters; notice certain sellers marking the party’s presence.
    
- **Nature (DC 13):** Recognize Veil-touched plants reacting to Oscar’s terrarium.
    
- **History (DC 14):** Recall old stories about Rootshapers and living trees.
    

### **Finding Clues About Local Problems**

- **Overheard rumors (easy DC 10):** missing cobbler, sigil vandalism, Concord scouts.
    
- **Talking with children (DC 10 Persuasion):** Kett gives unfiltered insight about strange things the tree “said” or “felt.”
    
- **Stallside gossip (DC 12 Persuasion):** Traders share fears about saplight flickers or blighted wildlife.

# 🧍 **Key NPCs in the Market**

Use these NPCs dynamically—they wander, interact, and respond to the party’s presence.

## **Mossfire Apothecary – Nyris the Sapbrewer**

_Primary merchant, quest-giver, weird-magic hook_  

- Sarcastic, blunt, calls Oscar “Green-heart.”
    
- Sells potions, Veil-reactive reagents, and one overpriced magical item.
    
- **Quest Hook:** Needs rare reagents—Weepsap, blighted beetle husks, or luminous moss.
    
- **Special Reaction:** Studies Oscar’s terrarium intensely; senses discord in Zennor’s amulet.
    
---

## **Rootshaper Apprentice – Selune of the Knots**

_Curious young mage; primary lore-hook_  

- Fascinated (almost overwhelmed) by Oscar’s terrarium.
    
- Knows the tree “stirred” when the party arrived.
    
- **Quest Hook:** Shows the party a hidden sigil chamber.
    
- **Behavior:** Energetic, scattered; runs off when she senses harmonic shifts.
    

---

## **Market Scout – Briar Fenstep**

_Guide, troublemaker, shortcut-provider_  

- Friendly, cocky, fast-talking.
    
- **Quest Hook:** Retrieve a stolen pack.
    
- **Secret:** Once failed the Council trial—deep insecurity.
    
- **Offer:** For a few silver, he’ll show faster routes through Rootspire.
    

---

## **Rootspire Child – Kett**

_Atmospheric hook for Oscar’s story / Veil foreshadowing_  

- Quiet; watches the terrarium glow.
    
- **Clue:** Saw a Concord agent near the lower roots but is afraid to tell adults.
    
- **Emotion:** Attached to Oscar quickly; interprets tree “moods.”
    

---

## **Old Wren – Tavern Host of the Sighing Boughs**

_Social hub; rumor engine_  

- Dry humor, bitter but kind.
    
- **Quest Hook:** Missing delivery of redroot grain.
    
- **Rumors:** Missing cobbler, forbidden sigil-carver, growing Veil tremors.
    

---

# 🌲 **Atmospheric Events While Shopping**

### **1. Veil Tremor**

A faint ripple runs through the bark. Saplight flickers blue.  
Locals tense but continue shopping.

- **Arcana/Nature (DC 13):** The Veil is unstable; the tree feels it.
    

---

### **2. Harmonic Echo**

A vendor’s wooden counter hums softly when Oscar passes.

- **Performance (DC 12):** Hum back; a small sigil briefly lights up.
    
- **Failure:** A discordant crackle; bystanders glance uneasily.
    

---

### **3. Market Watchers**

Two Gatewardens (Lira & Heron) observe from a distance.  
  
They aren’t hostile—only measuring the newcomers.

---

### **4. Blighted Wildlife Rumor**

A hunter arrives with a half-blighted stag.  
Dark veins pulse faintly in its hide.

- **Medicine (DC 14):** Veil corruption spreading faster than normal.
    
- **Insight (DC 12):** The hunter fears the blight is nearing Rootspire.
    

---

# ⚖️ **Market Consequences System**

### **Positive Actions**

- Polite bartering
    
- Helping a lost child
    
- Returning stolen goods
    
- Good conversation with Selune or Nyris
    

**Effect:**  
Rumors become friendlier; prices drop; Briar shares a shortcut for free.

---

### **Negative Actions**

- Pushing past vendors
    
- Showing Concord markings or sigils
    
- Speaking rudely to Wren
    
- Drawing weapons in a crowd
    

**Effect:**  
Gatewardens shadow the party; Boughwarden Thalan hears quickly; prices rise.

---

# 🎯 **Quest Hooks from the Market**

### **1. The Missing Cobbler**

Rumor from Wren or Briar.  
Last seen around the root-bridges.

### **2. Forbidden Sigil Carvings**

Selune or Nyris mentions them.  
Someone is carving sigils beneath bridges—dangerous ones.

### **3. Weeping Tree Reagent Hunt**

Nyris wants Weepsap or other rare materials from deeper groves.

### **4. Concord Scout Tracks**

Kett saw someone suspicious.  
Gatewardens quietly validate this if asked.

### **5. Saplight Instability**

Locals worry the saplight fluctuation means a larger tremor is coming.

---

# 🗝️ **Consequences Leading Toward the Council Trial**

If players make a good impression in the market:

- Thalan (Boughwarden) sends word he wishes to speak privately.
    
- Locals openly mention that the tree “reacted” when the party arrived.
    
- Selune insists the Council “will want to hear this” and leads them toward the Heartwood.
    

If they make a poor impression:

- The Gatewardens escort them to Thalan under suspicion.
    
- Fewer people talk to them; quests dry up.
    
- The Council trial becomes more severe (higher DCs, colder manifestations).
    

Thalan’s evaluation is directly influenced by market behavior.  

---

# 🌀 **If the Party Splits**

Handle the market as modular zones:

1. **Nyris’ Stall** → magic/reagent quests
    
2. **Root-Carver Row** → rumors & sigil clues
    
3. **Hunter’s Walk** → blight & Concord clues
    
4. **Overlook Bridge** → emotional or atmospheric scenes with Kett
    
5. **Sighing Boughs** → Wren’s social hub
    

They can each pick up different information that later converges.

---

# 🚪 **Exit Points from the Market Scene**

Players can leave the market naturally when they:

- follow a quest hook
    
- get summoned by Thalan
    
- decide to climb upward toward the taverns
    
- chase Briar toward a shortcut
    
- pursue rumors of a Concord agent
    
- investigate glowing sigils
    

The scene concludes naturally once they’ve gathered enough pressure to move deeper into Rootspire.
