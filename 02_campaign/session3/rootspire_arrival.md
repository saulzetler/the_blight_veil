# 🌳 **Rootspire – Arrival DM Guide**

_A functional settlement built atop ancient magic; a living city shaped by generations of refugees, druids, rebels, and the lingering power of the Veil._

---

## **1. Purpose of the Scene**

When the party arrives at Rootspire, you want them to feel:

- awe at the living architecture
    
- subtle unease at the old magic humming beneath it
    
- the political tension between practical survival and ancient traditions
    
- curiosity toward the Council of 8
    
- recognition from Oscar’s terrarium and faint echoes for other PCs’ trinkets
    

Rootspire is **not a druid utopia** — it is a living settlement shaped by necessity, old craft, and cautious defiance of the Concord.

---

## **2. Approaching Rootspire**

### **Read-Aloud**

> The forest parts as the land rises into a mound of tangled roots — thick as river barges and woven like cables.
> 
> Above them towers a single giant tree, its bark spiraled with age, its canopy blotting out the dying light. Homes and walkways cling to its trunk like lantern-lit nests, each one glowing amber against the dark green.
> 
> Rope bridges creak between branches. Running water trickles through carved wooden channels that feed moss gardens shimmering faintly with Veil-light.
> 
> The city hums as though the whole tree is dreaming.
> 
> Welcome to **Rootspire**.

---

## **3. The Gatewardens – First Social Interaction**

Two masked sentries step forward beneath a natural arch of roots. They embody both practicality (gear, rope, small blades) and old magic (sigil-etched bark armor).

### **Read-Aloud**

> “Halt,” one says, voice calm. “Rootspire greets travelers… but does not trust them.”
> 
> The other tilts their head. “State your purpose, and speak truthfully. The tree listens.”

### **Checks**

- **Persuasion (DC 13):** Friendly welcome, light guidance.
    
- **Insightful honesty (DC 13):** They note the party’s sincerity.
    
- **Deception (DC 15):** They sense it; still allow entry but remain stiff.
    
- **Mentioning Rilwen or the rebel camp:** Advantage on Persuasion.
    
- **Mentioning the Council:** They become more serious, not hostile.
    

### **If players push or threaten**

Gatewardens do not escalate — but they _mark_ the party in Rootspire’s rumor network. This affects later interactions.

---

## **4. Entering Rootspire — Sensory Experience**

### **Read-Aloud**

> The tunnel carved into the trunk opens into a vast ribbed chamber of living wood. Lantern-fungus glows in soft pools of amber light. Walkways spiral upward and downward, branching like veins.
> 
> The air is warm near the base, cooler above. Voices echo strangely — carried through the wood as if the city itself is listening.
> 
> People move along the roots: families, scouts, druids, traders, all navigating the organic pathways with practiced ease.

### **Atmosphere & Mechanics**

- **The tree moves subtly**, like a sleeping giant shifting its weight.
    
- Light shifts with mild bioluminescence.
    
- Harmonic tones are faint but everywhere.
    
- Sound carries unnaturally well — Perception checks get +2 for overhearing conversation.
    

### **Oscar’s Terrarium Reaction**

As they cross the threshold:

> Oscar’s terrarium glows faint green for a heartbeat, its plants trembling as if brushed by a breeze that no one else feels.

Nature (DC 14): The tree is reacting to the terrarium’s harmonic frequency.

---

## **5. Rootspire Architecture — What Players Learn Quickly**

Rootspire is a **hybrid of practicality and old magic**:

### **Functional Features**

- Rope ladders
    
- Branch bridges
    
- Rainwater troughs feeding cisterns
    
- Stone firepits carved into stump hollows
    
- Sleeping platforms woven from living root-twine
    
- Children playing in knot tunnels
    

### **Old Magic Features**

- Doors that shift when touched (reaction based on emotional tone)
    
- Vein-like saplight forming brief glowing lines
    
- Ancient sigils carved by the First Shapers
    
- Whisper-tunnels where sound travels too cleanly
    
- Secret hollow-spaces sealed with harmonic locks
    

These details matter later when the Council trial references harmonics and living structures.

## **6. Immediate Locations the Players Will See**

### **Rootwalk (Ground Level)**

- Market on root ridges
    
- Herbalists and potion-makers
    
- Hunters returning with forest game
    
- A broken Concord shield hung as a trophy
    

### **Heartwood (Mid-Level)**

- Taverns (The Sighing Boughs)
    
- Guest hollows
    
- Boughwarden’s administrative chambers
    
- Moss gardens glowing with faint Veil energy
    
- Communal fire circles
    

### **Canopy Spiral (Upper Level)**

- Aerial walkways
    
- Lookouts
    
- Archers practicing
    
- Sigils carved into branch undersides
    
- The wind shifts temperatures quickly at this height
    

### **The Crown (Restricted)**

- Sealed pathways
    
- Harmonic locks
    
- Echoes of Council influence  
    **Players cannot go here until after the trial.**

## **7. Quick Roleplay NPCs (Use as Needed)**

### **Gatewardens (Initial)**

Calm, stoic, responsible for Rootspire’s safety.

### **Boughwarden Thalan**

Leader of Rootspire; pragmatic, observant.  
Knows the Council will want a trial.

Quotes:

> “The Council listens, but does not answer lightly.”

> “If the tree stirred when you entered… they will want to see you.”

### **Rootshaper Apprentice**

A curious young wood-mage who notices Oscar immediately.

> “Your terrarium — it hummed. That doesn’t happen.”

### **Mossfire Apothecary**

Sells potions, including a too-expensive magic item.  
Offers quests for rare reagents.

### **Children of the Root**

Street kids who can guide players for a copper or two. Useful for flavor.

---

## **8. Optional Encounters During Exploration**

### **A. Harmonic Sigil Reaction**

A wall-carved sigil flickers as the group passes. If anyone hums or touches it:

- **Performance/Nature (DC 12):** Sigil sings back.
    
- Failing generates faint feedback (no damage, just eerie).
    

A direct prelude to the Council trial.

---

### **B. Veil-Stir in the Root-Chamber**

If you want foreshadowing:

- Sap briefly glows blue near Oscar
    
- Light dims for a heartbeat
    
- A muffled tremor ripples through the bark
    

Locals shrug:

> “It’s been doing that since the Veil tore.”

---

### **C. Overheard Rumors**

Use these to seed quests:

- “Concord scouts were spotted near the eastern roots.”
    
- “A cobbler’s gone missing again — left his stall mid-day.”
    
- “Someone’s been carving strange sigils at night.”
    
- “A blighted stag wandered too close to the lower groves…”
    
- “They say the Council’s been restless since the Veil tore.”

## **9. The Boughwarden’s Interview**

Before the trial, Rootspire’s leader always evaluates newcomers.

### **Read-Aloud**

> Thalan stands inside a hollow chamber lit by soft saplight. His gaze is sharp — not unkind, but measuring.
> 
> “Rootspire shelters many, but trusts few. Your arrival stirred something in the old wood. I need to know why.”

### **Checks**

- Straight conversation works
    
- Insight (DC 12) reveals he’s cautious but hopeful
    
- Persuasion (DC 13) convinces him to endorse them to the Council
    
- Deception (DC 15) gets partial trust
    
- Mentioning the rebels or a blighted encounter earns advantage
    

### **Outcome**

He guides them to the **Trial Chamber**, warning:

> “The Eight do not appear in the flesh. They manifest through this place — through the roots and the harmonics.
> 
> If they find you wanting, the chamber will not open.”

This leads directly into the trial.

---

## **10. Quest Hooks Before or After the Trial**

If players want to explore before meeting the Council:

- **The Weeping Tree Problem**
    
- **Concord scouts on the outer roots**
    
- **Cobbler side quest (flip-flop hook)**
    
- **A rare reagent hunt for the apothecary**
    
- **Tracking whoever is carving forbidden sigils**
    
- **Investigating saplight fluctuations**
    

You can run 1–2 small quests before the trial if pacing calls for it.

---

## **11. If Players Cause Trouble**

Rootspire is not a police state, but consequences exist:

### **Light Trouble**

- Rumors spread
    
- Prices increase
    
- Gatewardens tail them quietly
    
- Boughwarden seeks “clarification”
    

### **Major Trouble**

- They’re brought before the Boughwarden for questioning
    
- The Council requires a big service before granting a trial
    
- Rootspire becomes uneasy territory socially
    

This encourages careful play without punishing agency.
