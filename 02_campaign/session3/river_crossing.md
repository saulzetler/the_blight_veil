# **Ashroot River Crossing – DM Guide**

The party reaches the river right beside the **abandoned Ashroot rebel camp** — tents half-collapsed, embers dying, the forest air still carrying the scent of wet ash and pine. Whatever the rebels used to get across isn’t usable anymore, forcing the party to adapt.

---

## **Read-Aloud**

> The rebel camp ends at the riverbank in the morning the fires reduced to ember. Just beyond the last tent, the ground falls away into the dark rush of the river.  
> The current churns and the river flows with great force. 
> Scattered along the bank lie the remains of a crossing method: snapped rope and splintered stakes.  
> The rebels made it to this side of the river… but their way back across is gone.

---

# **DM Notes**

### **Why the Ashroot Rebels’ Method No Longer Works**

These rebels used a **makeshift rope-ferry** supported by:

- Three ash-stakes
    
- A rope stretched across the river
    
- A stabilizing sigil-stone (not magical, but used as a counterweight and anchor)
    

Overnight, **Veil-warped stormwater** from upriver surged through:

- The center stake was **ripped free**.
    
- The rope snapped and dragged downstream into debris.
    
- The sigil-stone cracked, listing halfway into the water.
    

The rebels left in a hurry and **didn’t have time to rebuild**.

This forces the party to improvise — but the clues remain.

---

# **Nearby Rebel Camp Details**

Scatter these around the tents and river edge:

- **Half-burned plans** showing a sketched ferry design.
    
- **A crate of climbing spikes** (some bent, some usable).
    
- **A broken longbow** and a **bundle of reed shafts**.
    
- **A small leather pouch** of resin used to waterproof ropes.
    
- **An Ashroot talisman** shaped like a twisted root — nonmagical, but symbolic.
    
- **Footprints** showing where rebels hauled the ferry line.
    

---

# **Investigation Opportunities**

Let players discover tools, materials, or clues through checks in the rebel camp or along the riverbank.

### **Investigation Checks (DC 13–15)**

- **DC 13**: Notice the **faint drag marks** where the ferry rope was pulled free; lead to submerged rope fragment.
    
- **DC 14**: Find the **cracked sigil-stone wedge**, revealing how it was used to anchor the ferry.
    
- **DC 15**: Spot **a path of footprints on the far bank**, confirming rebels crossed shortly before dawn.
    
- **DC 14**: Discover **rebel waterproof resin**, helpful for raft-building.
    
- **DC 13**: Notice that the toppled hollow tree trunk nearby was **partially moved before** — rebels abandoned the idea.
    
- **DC 15**: Uncover a **hidden supply stash**: 10 ft of good rope, 4 climbing spikes, 2 intact planks.
    

Include anything the players might need to reward exploration and encourage creativity.

---

# **Riverbank Features**

Present these as interactive environmental elements:

- **Submerged rope coil** (jammed under driftwood).
    
- **Two intact stakes** still planted in mud.
    
- **Hollow, fallen trunk** angled toward the river.
    
- **Blight-fungal bloom** pulsing faint heat and reddish spores.
    
- **Broken ferry plank** (buoyant but unstable).
    
- **A stone platform** where the rebels stood to load supplies.
    
- **Loose flat stones** perfect for stepping stones if the current can be slowed.
    

---

# **Crossing Methods**

Below are structured options, but allow hybrids and improv — this scene is designed to reward unusual thinking.

---

## **1. Rebuild the Ashroot Ferry**

Use rope, planks, stakes, and recovered materials.

**Skill Checks**

- _Survival or Investigation (DC 13)_ — understand original rebel design.
    
- _Athletics (DC 14)_ — pull rope free of debris.
    
- _Survival (DC 15)_ — rebuild the knots and anchor tension.
    
- _Group Strength (DC 12)_ — operate the makeshift ferry under strain.
    

**Fail Consequences**

- The ferry swings into the current: _Dex Save DC 14_ or someone falls in.
    

---

## **2. Harmonic Current-Stilling (Mystical + Environmental)**

Even though these rebels weren’t using magic, the cracked sigil-stone still resonates faintly with the Veil’s instability.

Players can exploit this.

**Approach**  
Matching a humming frequency temporarily “thickens” or stills the current for enough time to hop across via stones or a fallen log.

**Skill Checks**

- _Performance (DC 15)_ or _Arcana (DC 14)_ — find the harmonic.
    
- _Nature (DC 13)_ — plan a safe timed crossing path.
    

**Complication**

- Failed tone attempts cause water surges → _Dex Save (DC 13)_ or 1d4 necrotic splash.
    

---

## **3. Fell the Hollow Tree Trunk (Physical, risky)**

The party can finish what the rebels started.

**Skill Checks**

- _Perception (DC 12)_ — notice it’s hollow but sturdy.
    
- _Athletics (DC 15)_ — push or wedge it.
    
- _Tool Use (Axes, etc.)_ — cleanly notch it so it falls properly.
    
- _Group Strength (DC 14)_ — final shove.
    

**Complication**  
Disturbing nearby fungal bloom releases spores.  
_Con Save DC 13_: disadvantage on all checks for 10 minutes.

---

## **4. Improvised Raft**

Use recovered planks, rope, stakes, resin, and driftwood.

**Skill Checks**

- _Survival (DC 14)_ — build stable raft.
    
- _Athletics or Acrobatics (DC 13)_ — stay balanced on crossing.
    
- Each round: roll 1d4 for water surge event.
    

---

## **5. Swim Across (High risk)**

The current is dangerous due to Veil vibrations.

**Skill Checks**

- _Athletics (DC 16)_ — fight the current.
    
- _Con Save (DC 14)_ — cold + magical turbulence.  
    Failure means being swept downstream to a secondary riverbank encounter.
    

---

## **6. Magic-Assisted Crossing (Unstable)**

Teleportation flickers; shapeshifting warps slightly; levitation hums.

**Skill Checks or Notes**

- Wild Shape must resist harmonic dissonance → _Wis Save (DC 12)_.
    
- Teleport-type spells risk short displacement → _Cha Save (DC 10)_.
    

Magic remains viable but tempered by Veil flavor.

---

# **Consequences & Aftermath**

On the far bank:

- Footprints of 4–6 Ashroot rebels moving toward Rootspire.
    
- A torn scrap of rebel green-and-brown cloth caught on a briar.
    
- A faint trail of etched root-shaped sigils marking their path.
    
- Evidence they were traveling **quickly**, possibly pursuing a lead or fleeing Concord patrols.
    

Optional escalations if the party made noise:

- A Concord scouting wisp drifting nearby.
    
- A Veil-warped creature observing from the trees.
    
- A faint shift in the river’s harmonic tone — like something upstream responded.
