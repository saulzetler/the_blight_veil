# **🩸 The Blighted Dead — Single-Creature Encounter**

## **Scene Setup**

This encounter should hit _tone_ more than numbers: eerie, tragic, and unlike any undead they’ve fought.

**Location Options (choose depending on travel path):**

- A shallow river crossing at dusk
    
- A pocket of still air beneath twisted roots
    
- A fallen Concord scouting post overtaken by Blight
    
- A stone marker humming faintly with harmonics
    

Choose a place where Oscar can reasonably move close enough for the terrarium reaction to matter.

---

# **🎭 Read-Aloud**

> The trees thin, and the forest quiets in a way that feels wrong—like the air itself is holding its breath.
> 
> Ahead, something shambles into view: a man once, his skin stretched tight and grey, veins glowing with a faint, sickly shimmer. Blight clings to him like wet soot. Every step leaves a smear of blackened moss.
> 
> Its head jerks toward you—not with hunger, but with _recognition_, as if drawn by something deeper.
> 
> The forest murmurs in a low, discordant hum. The creature answers in a cracked gasp.

---

# **⚔️ Blighted Husk (Unique Variant)**

**Medium Undead (Blighted), Chaotic Malfunction**  
**AC** 14 (blight-hardened tissue)  
**HP** 45  
**Speed** 30 ft.

**Str** 15 | **Dex** 10 | **Con** 16 | **Int** 3 | **Wis** 7 | **Cha** 5

### **Traits**

- **Blight-Forged Flesh.**  
    Resistant to: _bludgeoning, piercing, slashing, fire, cold, lightning, thunder, force, psychic._  
    Immune to: _poison._  
    **Vulnerable to radiant.**
    
- **Necrotic Reversal.**  
    When the Husk takes **necrotic damage**, it heals that amount instead.
    
- **Blighted Pulse (Aura).**  
    Any creature that starts its turn within 5 ft. must succeed a DC 12 Con save or take **3 necrotic** and have veins darken unnaturally for 1 round.
    
- **Harmonic Sensitivity.**  
    The Husk is attracted to the resonant pulse of Oscar’s terrarium. It prioritizes moving toward him unless engaged in melee.
    

### **Actions**

- **Claw (Multiattack).** +5 to hit, 1d8+3 slashing + 1d4 necrotic.
    
- **Death Spasm (On Death).** Releases a puff of Blight spores. DC 13 Con save or take 1d6 necrotic and cough violently (no mechanical effect beyond flavor unless you want a long-term blight tracker).
    

### **Skill Checks (During Combat)**

- **Arcana or Nature (DC 13):** Realize radiant damages it strongly.
    
- **Medicine (DC 14):** Notice the body is half-grown with Blight fungus and half-rotted human.
    
- **Perception (DC 12):** Notice Oscar’s terrarium reacts to it.
    

---

# **🌱 Oscar’s Terrarium Reaction (Mid-Encounter Cue)**

As the Husk gets within 15–20 ft:

**Read-Aloud (Terrarium Stirring):**

> Oscar’s chest glows faintly—soft green at first, then brighter, like sunlight through water. The ferns inside curl inward as if bracing against a storm. Something ancient stirs.

### **Mechanics**

- Oscar can make a **DC 12 Wisdom (Nature)** check as a bonus action to focus on the terrarium.  
    Success → he understands it wants to _interfere with the Blight_.
    
- The creature reacts violently to Oscar’s proximity (narrative focus, not a mechanical buff).
    

---

# **🌿 The Reveal: Oscar Can Heal Blight**

This should happen **at zero HP**, **when Oscar touches the Husk**, or **if the party tries to stabilize it**.

## **Trigger Option A: On Creature’s Death**

> As the creature collapses, the light in Oscar’s terrarium surges. Vines press against the glass—aching, urgent.
> 
> The Husk trembles. For a moment, beneath the rot, you see the shape of a man.

Oscar may choose to reach out instinctively.

### **If Oscar touches the Husk:**

The terrarium’s plants **wither slightly** (see Consequences).  
Green light flows from his chest into the corpse.

**Read-Aloud:**

> The Blight recoils. Its veins darken, then crack like burnt bark. For the briefest heartbeat, the corruption lifts—revealing the face of someone who once lived here. A whisper of gratitude escapes cracked lips… before the body finally goes still.

---

## **Trigger Option B: During Combat (If Oscar gets bold)**

When Oscar uses an action to “try something,” bend over the terrarium, channel nature, etc.:

- Allow a **Wisdom check (DC 15)**.
    
- On success → same effect: radiant purification burst.
    
- Deals **2d6 radiant to the Husk** (double damage via vulnerability).
    
- Oscar takes **1d6 psychic** from the strain and plants wilt—this foreshadows cost.
    

---

# **🌑 Consequences of the Reveal**

### **Terrarium Damage**

After healing or purifying:

- Some plants inside turn black, shrivel, or collapse.
    
- They regrow **after a long rest** (or don’t, depending on your long-term arc).
    

### **Mechanical Consequence**

Oscar gains a limited-use ability:

### **🌿 Purify Blight (1/day)**

As an action, Oscar channels the life within his terrarium into a creature or patch of corrupted terrain.

**Effects:**

- Deals **2d6 radiant** to a Blighted creature (double on vulnerability).
    
- OR stabilizes a Blighted creature/humanoid, cleansing its corruption momentarily.
    
- OR removes a 5-ft area of Blight growth.
    

**Cost:**  
Oscar takes **1d4 psychic** or **1 level of exhaustion** (your choice)  
**and** 1–2 plants inside the terrarium temporarily die.

---

# **🛠 DM Notes**

- Reinforce that Oscar didn’t _choose_ this—his terrarium responded instinctively.
    
- This should feel mystical, fragile, rare.
    
- The rebels and the Council of 8 later will notice this instantly.
    
- This sets him apart as someone attuned to the Veil in an unusual way.
    

---

# **🌬 Consequences & Lore Hints**

After the encounter, give small threads:

- The Husk’s last expression is peaceful.
    
- The forest hum quiets, as if observing.
    
- A faint sigil-shaped pulse appears in the terrarium glass for a moment—a harmonic pattern.
    

**Nature/Arcana (DC 13):**  
This is _not_ normal druidic magic. It’s a harmonic resonance tied to the torn Veil.

**Higher (DC 16):**  
It resembles patterns seen in Blight-maps or rebel sigil carvings.
