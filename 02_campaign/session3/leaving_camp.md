# **🌲 Transitioning Away from the Ashroot Rebels**

## **Read-Aloud (Arrival Back at the Rebel Camp)**

> The rebels gather beneath the dim canopy, their lanternlight flickering green across bark and steel. Rilwen watches the treeline with steady eyes; Iraen tends a small pot of river-water simmering over a low fire. Cadran wipes mud from his blade, glancing at you with something like respect—tempered, but real.
> 
> “You fought well,” he says. “Ashroot could use more blades like yours. But… your path shouldn’t end here.”
> 
> Rilwen folds her arms. “There are answers west of the river. Past the old roots. Seek the Council of Eight. If anyone still speaks against the Trifarix openly, it’s them.”
> 
> Iraen shakes her head. “Just don’t expect an audience. They choose their own allies. They always have.”
> 
> The rebels exchange glances—strategic, unreadable. You get the sense that this is not dismissal… but direction.

---

## **DM Notes — Rebel Motivation & Tone**

- The rebels respect the party but see them as **too important to keep**.
    
- They know the Council of 8 is a rare and powerful ally in the shadow war.
    
- They are strategic, not deferential—sending the party away is a **tactical calculation**, not an act of reverence.
    
- The rebels are aware: the Council is _notoriously selective_, and even **Concord leaders fear them**.
    

Use dialogue like:

- “We’re not pushing you off—we’re pointing you where you’ll matter.”
    
- “If the Council turns you away, don’t take it personal. They’ve denied entire battalions.”
    
- “The Tree-City stands at the forest’s crown. Find the Gatewardens. They’ll know where the Council gathers next.”

## **Player Agency: What the Party Might Want Before Leaving**

### **1. Long Rest at the Camp**

Fully supported. Rebels keep watch.  
**Trigger Zennor’s Nightmare (based on amulet & bracelet files)**

**Read-Aloud (Zennor’s Dream):**

> The fire dims. Sleep drags Zennor under like cold water.
> 
> He stands in a field of ash. Something small is clutched in his hand—a pink bracelet, charred around the edges. The wind carries a voice, thin and childish: _“Why didn’t you come back?”_
> 
> A second voice coils behind him—Eris’ whisper, warm as a knife: _“You were always too late.”_
> 
> Shadows gather into hands. They reach for him. The amulet burns cold. The bracelet tightens.
> 
> He wakes choking on smoke that isn’t there.

**Mechanical Effect:**

- Zennor takes **1 level of exhaustion** _unless_ he succeeds a DC 13 Wisdom save.
    
- The amulet grows warm for the next hour, subtly tugging west (toward future witchcraft events).

## **2. Asking the Rebels for More Info on the Council of 8**

**DM Summary of What the Rebels Know (and don’t):**

- The Council operates from **shifting chambers** hidden throughout the Ashroot borderlands.
    
- Their closest “public” point of contact is the **Tree-City Rootspire**
    
- The Council actively opposes the Trifarix’s suppression of ancient magics.
    
- They’re divided internally—some radical, some cautious.
    
- They have **agents in Orrenval**, known only as “The Quiet Branch.”
    

Rebels should emphasize:

> “Don’t go seeking the Council like pilgrims. Go like people ready to be judged.”

## **3. Rebel Escort Contingency (Rilwen Thorne)**

### **Default:**

Rebels point the players toward the Tree-City and **stay behind to finish their sweep**.

### **If Players Ask for an Escort:**

- DC 14 Persuasion → **Rilwen** volunteers reluctantly.
    
- She insists the journey must be quick and quiet.
    
- Cadran and Iraen remain behind:
    
    - Cadran: “We’ve got Concord scouts sniffing at our heels.”
        
    - Iraen: “Three of us go missing, they’ll notice. One slipping away? Maybe not.”
        

### **If Players Offer Payment or Aid:**

- Automatic success.
    
- Rilwen’s attitude improves → she becomes a soft ally for later Council politics.
    

### **If They Intimidate or Coerce:**

- Rilwen refuses; Cadran intervenes.
    
- The party risks souring rebel relations permanently.
    
- Use social consequences, not combat.

## **4. Players Who Don’t Want to Leave Yet — Contingencies**

### **A. “We want to help the rebels more first.”**

Offer small optional tasks:

- Repair a broken perimeter ward
    
- Help move a wounded scout
    
- Gather herbs with Iraen (may reveal a small nature clue about the Blight)
    
- Hunt a blighted deer spotted at dusk (foreshadowing the zombie encounter)
    

These take **1–2 hours in-world** and do not derail the transition.

### **B. “We want to storm deeper into the Ashroot with the rebels.”**

Rebels strongly advise against it—it risks exposing the entire camp.  
If players insist:

- They can accompany a **1-hour scouting loop** with Rilwen
    
- Roll for a single encounter (Concord patrol, blighted fauna)
    
- After: rebels reiterate the urgency of seeking the Council.
    

### **C. “We want to recruit the rebels to travel with us.”**

Rebels respond with a firm but respectful refusal.  
Justification:

- They must hold territory
    
- Concord pressure is rising
    
- The Council would distrust a large group of rebels arriving with outsiders
    

Rilwen alone is the only possible escort.

## **5. Guidance Toward the Tree-City**

### **Read-Aloud (Rebel Directions):**

> “Follow the river north until the waters run sweet. You’ll see a rise in the land—a tangle of roots as wide as a hill. That’s Rootspire. A city grown from one tree older than the Concord itself.
> 
> Find the Gatewardens. They’ll judge whether you’re worth sending to the Council. And… mind your words. The Council listens deeper than your tongue.”

### **Tone:**

Ancient, mysterious, uneasy.  
The rebels understand the Council wields power on the same scale as sanctioned Concord magic, but **not aligned** with the Trifarix.

---

## **6. Potential Additional Outcomes to Prepare**

### **If Players Attempt to Follow the Concord Trail Instead:**

- They encounter signs of a larger Concord push coming from Orrenval
    
- A scout warns: “If you go that way, you’ll walk into a spearline.”
    

Redirect back toward the Tree-City.

### **If They Split the Party:**

- Rilwen only escorts **one group**
    
- Other group faces a forest complication (lost, minor hazard, Blight hotspot)
    

### **If They Try to Steal Rebel Supplies:**

- Rebels respond defensively but try to avoid killing the party
    
- This triggers a tense negotiation
    
- Long-term relations with Ashroot rebels harmed
