## DM Prep Notes

> [!warning] DM Only
> - The Council said observe, don't intervene. This scene is a test of that instruction — not because the resonance is overtly dangerous, but because it is clearly significant and the party has just come from a scene full of action and revelation. The temptation to do something will be real. Let it sit. Nothing here requires intervention. Reward restraint.
> - **The terrarium sync is the scene's emotional centrepiece**, not the NPC encounter and not the visual spectacle of the clearing. Build toward it. Let the Mira conversation and the atmospheric build prime the party, then let the sync happen in the silence between beats.
> - **The pulse is real.** It is the Ashroot Resonance — one of four sites the party has been tasked with observing. It is connected to the Veil Core in Orrenval via the same harmonic network, delayed by weeks. Something is learning to harmonise. The party cannot know this yet. Mira cannot know this. But the DM knows that what is happening in this clearing is the Veil developing a second voice.
> - **Mira is not a plant, not an agent, not secretly significant.** She is a local woman who found the clearing thirty years ago when she was grieving and the stone felt like company. She has been visiting ever since. She knows its rhythms better than anyone alive, and she knows nothing about why. She is the human face of the Veil's relationship with ordinary people — not victims, not worshippers in a formal sense, just people who found something and stayed near it because it helped them feel less alone.
> - **The Concord visit Mira mentions** is significant. The stone went quiet for nearly a year afterward. Vision scholars measured it. A reasonable inference — available to a sharp player via History DC 14 — is that the scholars performed some kind of dampening or extraction. The stone recovered. It is louder now than before the visit. Draw your own conclusions.
> - **The boot prints in Scene 06** were left by the Harmonic Assessment team visiting this clearing before the party arrived. The team has already been here. They have already seen the stone. They know what it is, at least partially. The party is not ahead of the Concord. They are behind it.

## The Journey to the Clearing

The walk from Rootspire to the clearing takes approximately two hours on foot through the Ashroot proper — no road, just the kind of path that forms when enough feet go the same direction over enough years. The party will notice the path without being told what it is.

Use the journey for quiet conversation and recovery. The lower roots were heavy. The air up here is cold and smells of pine and wet earth. The Great Tree is visible above the canopy for the first stretch, then disappears behind the ridgeline.

**One beat on the journey — mandatory:**

About forty minutes out, the birdsong stops. Not gradually — between one step and the next, it is simply absent. The insects are gone too. The wind continues. The trees move. But the living sound of the forest has emptied out of the air ahead of them.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — The Silence Arrives
> You don't notice it at first — the absence of something rarely announces itself. But somewhere in the last hundred yards the forest has gone quiet in a way that has nothing to do with you moving through it. The birds were there. Now they aren't.
>
> The wind still moves the branches overhead. The trees are alive, the light is normal, the path is still underfoot. But the world ahead has excused everything with a pulse from it, and what's left is the sound of your own footsteps and breathing.
>
> Thirty yards further, you feel the ground change underfoot. Not the terrain — the ground itself. It is faintly warm through the soles of your boots, as if something low and slow is radiating heat from a long way down.

## The Clearing

The clearing is roughly sixty feet across, roughly circular, as if the trees made a collective decision to stand back from a particular point. The bark of every tree within the clearing's edge is pale and smooth — no moss, no lichen, no insect trails. The canopy overhead is thin here, admitting more sky than the surrounding forest.

At the centre: a stone. Unremarkable at first glance — half-buried, irregular, approximately the size of a large suitcase. Grey-brown. Lichen-free, like the trees. Older than the surrounding growth.

The ground around it is soft and warm. The closer the party gets, the warmer.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — Entering the Clearing
> The treeline opens and you step into something that has been waiting to be stepped into.
>
> The clearing is still in a way the forest outside it isn't — the wind that moved the canopy behind you doesn't reach here. The light falls differently: not brighter, just more present, as if the air itself is paying closer attention. At the edge of your vision, where the treeline meets the clearing, shadows pulse. Once. Slowly. Like a heartbeat at one-third speed.
>
> At the centre: a stone. Half-buried. Nothing about it announces itself. It is simply there, the way old things are simply there — not placed, not forgotten, just continuous.
>
> You feel it before you hear it. A resonance in the sternum, below the register of sound — slow, rhythmic, patient. Like something enormous breathing very quietly a long way down.

*Give the party a moment to orient — take in the space, discuss, approach or hold back. Then let them move at their own pace toward the stone.*

## The Pulse — What It Does

The pulse occurs every three minutes. It is not perfectly regular — it drifts slightly, the way a heartbeat drifts, which is part of what makes it feel alive.

**During each pulse:**

- The air grows luminous — not brighter, more present. Dusk-light quality, as if the afternoon has briefly remembered what sunset feels like.
- Shadows in the clearing contract slowly toward the stone, then release.
- Any reflective surface — a blade, still water, a polished buckle — shows the person looking into it wearing a faint smile. When they look directly at their face, the smile is not there. When they look away and back, it is.
- The warmth in the ground intensifies at the stone's immediate vicinity — within five feet, the soil is warm enough to be noticeable through gloves.
- Birds do not return during the pulse. They return in the silence between, briefly, then leave again.

> [!warning] DM Only
> The pulse is the Ashroot Resonance. Its frequency matches the Sanctara's harmonic output in Orrenval, delayed by approximately three weeks. This delay is the key detail the Council of 8 wants the party to document — it suggests the resonance is not emanating from the stone but passing through it, like a signal through a relay. The stone is not the source. It is an antenna. The source is deeper, further, and the party will not find it this session.
>
> The smiling reflections are a Veil atmospheric tell — the Veil membrane is thin here, and the micro-distortions in how light processes through it produce the reflection anomaly. It is not sinister. It is physics, again, of the deeply strange variety. A character who has seen both this and the lower roots carvings (figures with faces turned away from the divine centre) might notice that the reflections are the inverse — the divine looking back at them, rather than them looking away from the divine. This connection is available as a DC 16 Insight or Religion check. File it away.

## NPC: Mira Ashwick

*Mira arrives approximately 10–15 minutes after the party reaches the stone — she comes on her own schedule, which she has maintained for thirty years.*

> [!warning] DM Only
> Mira is not related to Maren from Scene 02 — the shared surname is coincidental and reflects a common Ashroot family name in the region. If the party notices and asks, Mira will say Maren is her aunt. You can lean into this connection or walk it back — a familial relationship between the two women who tend the two significant sites in this session is thematically resonant and adds warmth to the world. Your call.

| | |
|---|---|
| **Full name** | Mira of the Ashroot (uses no family name in introduction — "just Mira") |
| **Age** | Early 50s |
| **Appearance** | Weathered in the specific way of people who spend time outside by choice rather than necessity — sun-lined, unhurried. Dark hair going grey at the roots, worn loose. Simple working clothes, good boots. Carries a canvas satchel with offerings: dried flowers, a small candle, a folded piece of cloth with a knot pattern on it. |
| **Demeanour** | Warm, pragmatic, observational. Not naive — she has a clear-eyed relationship with the world. She does not mistake the stone for safe or simple. She simply does not find it frightening. |
| **Voice / tone** | Unhurried. She speaks the way people speak when they are used to silence and don't feel the need to fill it. Comfortable with pauses. Asks good questions. |
| **What she's doing here** | Leaving an offering. She does this every week, roughly, when the walk feels right. She times it loosely by the pulse — when she can feel it from the village, she knows it is time to come. |
| **What she knows** | The stone has been here her whole life and her mother's whole life. Her mother called it the Listening Rock and the name stuck. It has always pulsed, but the pulse has changed over the years — quieter for a long period, getting louder again now. She knows when the Concord came through. She remembers exactly, because the stone went quiet the same week they left. |
| **What she believes** | She does not have a theology about it. She comes because it helps her think. "Some places just hold more of the world than others," she says. "This is one of those places." |
| **What she will not do** | She will not tell the party to leave. She will not tell them to stay. She will not pretend to know more than she does. She will not be frightened of the party even if they do strange things near the stone — she has seen stranger. |

**Sample dialogue:**

**On arriving and finding the party:**
> *"Oh, visitors. That's new."*
> *[Sets her satchel down, looks at the stone, looks at the party.]*
> *"You're from the city. I can tell by the boots. What brings you out to the Listening Rock?"*

**On the stone's history:**
> *"My mother came here. Her mother before her, I think, though I only have the story secondhand. It's always been here. It's always done this."*
> *[A beat.]*
> *"The pulse. You've felt it by now. Everyone does, eventually. Some people find it frightening. I never have. It feels like — company. Something very patient, paying attention."*

**On the Concord visit:**
> *"They came through — oh, eight years ago now, maybe nine. Three of them in Vision robes, instruments I didn't recognise, very polite, very thorough. Asked me questions I couldn't answer. Measured things. Left without saying what they found."*
> *[Quieter:]*
> *"The stone went quiet that same week. Nearly a year of silence. I kept coming anyway. Then one morning I felt it again, faint, and I cried. Embarrassing thing to admit."*
> *[She does not seem embarrassed.]*
> *"It's been getting louder every month since."*

**On Oscar specifically:**
> *[She looks at Oscar the way Maren did — thoughtful, not unkind.]*
> *"The stone likes you. Or notices you. Hard to tell the difference with something that old."*
> *[A pause.]*
> *"It's doing something different while you're here. The pulse interval. It's shorter."*

**On being asked to leave or step back:**
> *"I've been coming here for thirty years. I'll give you your space, but I won't be told to go. Not from here."*

## The Terrarium Sync

*This is the scene's emotional centrepiece. Time it carefully — after the party has settled into the clearing, after Mira has arrived and the Listening Rock conversation has run its course, during a natural pause.*

If Oscar is near the stone — within ten feet — the sync happens on the next pulse without prompting. If Oscar is hanging back, Mira's observation ("the stone likes you — the pulse interval is shorter") is the gentle push.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — The Sync
> The pulse comes — you feel it before it arrives, the familiar pressure in the sternum, the warmth rising through the soles of your feet —
>
> And the terrarium answers.
>
> Not brightly. Not dramatically. The plant inside goes completely still — stiller than still, the particular stillness of something that has stopped pretending not to be paying attention — and then, slowly, it pulses. Once. In time with the stone. The same frequency, the same rhythm, the same interval.
>
> For three seconds, maybe four, the terrarium and the Listening Rock are breathing together.
>
> Then the terrarium dims. Not extinguished. Dims — the way a lamp dims when you turn the wick down deliberately. It is not damaged. It is not distressed.
>
> It looks, if a plant in a small glass container can look like anything, like something that has just received a message it has been waiting a very long time to receive.

*Hold the beat. Do not narrate past it. Let the table respond.*

> [!warning] DM Only
> What just happened: the Listening Rock is a relay for the Ashroot Resonance. The terrarium — Oscar's containment mechanism — recognised the resonance frequency passing through the stone and briefly synchronised with it. The "dimming" afterward is the terrarium releasing a small amount of accumulated resonance back into the relay — not depleting itself, not being depleted. Contributing. Like a voice joining a chord for a moment, then falling quiet.
>
> Oscar is not in danger. The terrarium is not damaged. But Oscar has just functioned — passively, unconsciously — as part of the resonance network. The same network the Concord monitors from Orrenval. If anyone on that Assessment team had instruments running at the moment of the sync, they would have seen an anomalous reading from this location. They were here earlier today. Their instruments may have been left active.
>
> Do not surface this information now. Plant it as a thought the DM holds going into Scene 06.

## Zennor's Grief — The Pulse's Emotional Shape

*This is a quiet, private beat. Deliver it to Zennor's player directly, or describe it to the table if the moment is right.*

> [!tip] Zennor
> The pulse has a quality that the others might feel as pressure or resonance or physical sensation. Zennor feels it differently.
>
> It has a shape. The shape is familiar. It is the particular weight of grief that has been carried so long it has stopped being grief and become something closer to identity — the thing that organises everything else around it. He knows this weight. He has been living inside it.
>
> The pulse is not his grief. It is older than him, larger than him. But it recognises the shape of his, the way two different songs can share a chord. For the duration of the pulse — three seconds, four — he does not feel alone inside it.
>
> Then the pulse releases and the ordinary darkness comes back.
>
> Nothing has changed. Nothing has been resolved. But something in the stone knows what he carries, and that is, unexpectedly, not nothing.

> [!warning] DM Only
> Do not force this beat if Zennor's player is not in the right headspace for it or if the table's energy is wrong. Read the room. This is one of the session's most intimate character moments and it works best when it arrives quietly, without announcement. If the moment doesn't land naturally, save it for the next resonance site.

## Documenting the Observation

The Council tasked the party with observing and reporting. Encourage them — gently, through fiction rather than DM instruction — to record what they find. A character with proficiency in Arcana, History, or Nature could make a formal record. Mira might offer paper and charcoal from her satchel.

What a thorough report to the Council of 8 should contain:
- Location and physical description of the site
- Pulse frequency and interval (approximately three minutes, slight drift)
- Atmospheric effects during pulse: luminosity, shadow contraction, reflection anomaly, temperature elevation
- Evidence of prior activity: cleared ground, pale-barked trees, warmth suggesting long-term resonance
- NPC account of historical changes (Concord visit, year of silence, current intensification)
- Terrarium sync event (if Oscar has disclosed the terrarium's nature — if not, this may be recorded without full context)

> [!warning] DM Only
> When the party presents this report to the Council of 8, the Council will respond to the terrarium sync detail with particular intensity — more than the party will expect. The Council has been waiting for confirmation that a Dawnswell vessel is resonating with the Ashroot network. This is confirmation. What the Council does with that information is a question for a future session. For now, file it.

## Transitioning Out

The scene ends when the party has what they came for and begins the walk back to Rootspire. Mira stays. She has more time to spend with the stone today.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — Leaving the Clearing
> Mira doesn't watch you go — she's already settled at the stone's edge with her candle lit and her eyes closed, the pulse moving through her the same way it moved through you, but slower, quieter, like she's been practising the receiving of it for a long time.
>
> The clearing falls behind you. The treeline closes. The birdsong returns — gradually at first, then all at once, as if a signal was given.
>
> The walk back to Rootspire is two hours. The light is going later than expected. The path is familiar now. The ground underfoot is cooling.
>
> Somewhere behind you, every three minutes, something breathes.

*Move to Scene 06.*

## Threads This Scene Advances

- **The Council of 8's task** — First resonance site observed and documented. Three remain. The Council will be interested in all of it, but particularly the terrarium sync. The party has done their job. They do not yet know that the Concord has done the same job, earlier, today.
- **Oscar's arc** — The terrarium sync is the third signal this session that Oscar is something specific. The lower roots gave them the question. The Hollow Nave gave them the mirror. The Listening Rock gave them the first involuntary evidence that whatever they are is already in communication with something larger. Oscar did not choose to sync. The terrarium did not ask permission. That is the thing to sit with.
- **The Ashroot Resonance** — The party now has direct observational data on one of four sites. They have Mira's account of the Concord dampening and the recovery. They have the pre-Concord locus history from the History check. The picture of a resonance network that predates the Concord — and that the Concord has been quietly monitoring and suppressing for decades — is beginning to form.
- **The Concord threat** — The Assessment team was here before the party. Their instruments may have been active during the terrarium sync. Scene 06 makes this concrete. The party is not observing the Concord's movements. The Concord is observing theirs.
- **Mira as a recurring NPC** — She knows the other two sites in the region. She has thirty years of observational data on the Listening Rock that no Vision scholar has access to. If the party cultivates this relationship, she becomes one of the most valuable sources of ground-level Veil intelligence in the Ashroot.
