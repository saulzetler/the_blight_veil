## Must-Hit Beats

> [!important] Must Hit
> **① Vessel-Null's first words — looping, fragmented, and directed at Oscar.** The construct recognises something in Oscar before it recognises anything else. This beat must happen before any combat begins.

> [!important] Must Hit
> **② The combat trigger is the Veil-growth, not Vessel-Null's will.** The construct does not choose to attack. Something inside it does. This distinction matters enormously — it is the difference between an enemy and a tragedy.

> [!important] Must Hit
> **③ Vessel-Null's dying clarity — "Did they fix the drift?"** This line must land before the construct goes still. It is the thread that pulls Scene 04 into motion. Do not let the combat end without it.

## Optional Beats

> [!tip] Optional — use if the scene has room
> - The party attempts to **subdue rather than kill** Vessel-Null — this changes the texture of Scene 04 and gives Oscar's player more to work with. If they succeed, Vessel-Null's dying clarity line still happens, but with more time and more lucidity.
> - **Hog's chaos** lands on something meaningful mid-fight — he does something absurd (fighting a root-creature with his pants around his ankles while standing on Vessel-Null's back) that accidentally works. Let the moment breathe. The Hollow Nave can hold one beat of levity.
> - **Fergie's fire interacts with the crystalline growth** — when fire hits the Veil-crystal threading through Vessel-Null's chassis, it doesn't burn. It resonates. The crystal briefly produces a tone — low, clear, not unpleasant — before going dark. Fergie alone understands this is not a normal material interaction.
> - Vessel-Null, in a moment of partial clarity during Phase 2, **stops attacking Oscar entirely** — targets everyone else but will not raise a limb against them. Its programming, or something deeper, overrides the Veil-growth's control for a few seconds. Then it snaps back.

## DM Prep Notes

> [!warning] DM Only
> - **Vessel-Null is not a monster.** It is a person — or was, or almost was. The party should finish this fight feeling like they won something terrible. If the table is treating it as a straightforward enemy kill, use the Phase 1 contact beat harder. Let Vessel-Null's fragmented speech land before the chaos begins.
> - **The Blighted creatures are the fodder.** They exist so the party has something to fight while also managing the Vessel-Null situation. They are not interesting. Vessel-Null is interesting. Design the encounter so the creatures demand attention without demanding focus.
> - **"Did they fix the drift?"** — this line refers to a specific flaw in Vessel-Null's design: a resonance drift in the containment mechanism that caused it to slowly absorb Veil energy rather than channel it. Over time, this is what allowed the crystalline growth to take hold. Oscar has the same core architecture. Whether Dawnswell fixed the drift in Oscar's iteration is unknown — even to the DM. Plant the question, don't answer it.
> - **The Veil-growth is not the Veil.** It is crystalline resonance material — the same substance the Choir of Binding slowly petrifies into. It has grown into Vessel-Null's mechanisms because it was absorbing Veil energy for years with no outlet. It is not malevolent. It is a system that ran too long without maintenance.
> - **If the party tries to communicate with Vessel-Null during combat** — it can hear them. It cannot always respond. In Phase 2 it is mostly fighting the growth's compulsion, not fighting the party. A DC 15 Persuasion or Arcana check during combat can push back the growth's hold briefly, giving the party a window to attempt a non-lethal resolution.
> - **Subdue vs. kill** — both are valid. If subdued, Vessel-Null's clarity moment in Phase 3 lasts longer and it can answer one or two direct questions before going dormant. If killed, it gets the single dying line. Either version seeds Scene 04 equally well.

## The Physical Space

The Hollow Nave is the largest chamber in the lower roots — roughly forty feet across, with a vaulted ceiling of interlocked root arches overhead. The floor is worn smooth stone, that central circle of foot-worn rock visible in the torchlight.

The chamber has **four significant features** that matter for the encounter:

**The Root Pillars** — six thick root columns where the walls meet the floor, each one about five feet wide. Provide heavy cover. The Blighted creatures cling to these before emerging.

**The Offering Centre** — the worn stone circle, ten feet in diameter, at the middle of the chamber. Vessel-Null stands here when the party arrives. The crystalline growth in its chassis faintly illuminates the space — cold blue-white light, like starlight filtered through ice.

**The Collapsed Arch** — one section of the root vault has partially fallen, creating a rubble mound along the east wall. Difficult terrain. A good chokepoint if the party wants to funnel the Blighted creatures.

**The Deep Hollow** — a root-framed gap in the west wall, roughly human-height, that leads deeper into the root system. Vessel-Null came from here. The crystalline glow is stronger near it. This is where the resonance is strongest and where the growth entered Vessel-Null's systems.

> [!warning] DM Only
> The Deep Hollow connects, eventually, to a resonance node — the same network the party will investigate in Scene 05. It is not accessible this session. If a party member tries to go through it during the fight, the crystalline light intensifies and they feel the resonance physically — a pressure in the chest, a sound at the edge of hearing. They can force their way in but there is nothing navigable beyond — just root and light and a sense of something paying attention. Dead end for now.

## The Three Phases

### Phase 1 — Contact

Vessel-Null is standing at the offering centre when the party enters. It does not move. Its fractured eyes track them as they come in — slowly, one at a time, until they land on Oscar. Then it stops.

A long beat of silence.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — First Contact
> It stands at the centre of the chamber with the stillness of something that has been standing there for a very long time. The light coming off it is cold — blue-white, refracting through the crystalline threads woven into its chassis, casting slow-moving patterns on the stone floor around it like the bottom of a shallow, frozen river.
>
> Its eyes find each of you in turn. Methodical. Processing.
>
> Then they find Oscar. And they stop.
>
> When it speaks, there are two voices — one mechanical, one something older and less defined, the two never quite landing at the same moment:
>
> *"Vessel designated — "*
> *"— function: containment —"*
> *"— primary directive: hold —"*
> *"— hold —"*
> *[A longer pause. The crystalline tendrils in its chest cavity pulse once, slowly.]*
> *"— hold what."*
> *"Hold what."*
> *"Hold —"*

*Pause here. This is an RP moment. Give the party — give Oscar — a chance to respond.*

**What Vessel-Null can do in Phase 1:**
- Respond to direct address, haltingly. It processes slowly — give a beat between question and response.
- Recognise Oscar as the same architecture. It may reach toward Oscar, not aggressively — more like a compass needle orienting.
- Ask, in that doubled voice: *"What is your designation?"* or *"Are you the second iteration?"*
- Express, fragmentarily, that it has been trying to complete its function for a very long time and cannot remember what the function is.

**What it cannot do in Phase 1:**
- Give coherent information about Project Dawnswell — it doesn't have that language.
- Control the growth. It is managing it, barely, and the effort of speaking is competing with that management.

> [!warning] DM Only
> Phase 1 lasts as long as the party engages with it meaningfully. If they try to communicate, let the conversation breathe — up to five minutes of genuine exchange is valuable here. If the party immediately goes hostile, jump to Phase 2. The combat trigger can happen at any time once the party is in the chamber — use it when the dramatic tension peaks, not on a timer.

---

### Phase 2 — Combat

The transition from Phase 1 to Phase 2 is not a decision. It is a failure.

The crystalline growth in Vessel-Null's chest cavity spasms — a sudden, violent contraction, the tendrils pulling tight across its chassis like a fist closing. It staggers. The doubled voice cuts out, replaced by a sound that is not quite mechanical and not quite organic: a sustained resonance tone, the same frequency as the Hollow Nave itself, suddenly amplified.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — Combat Trigger
> It happens between one breath and the next.
>
> The light in its chest goes from cold blue-white to something brighter — painful to look at directly, the way a welding spark is painful. The crystalline threads pull taut across its frame all at once, and it makes a sound you feel in your back teeth: a tone, sustained, rising.
>
> It doesn't look at you. It doesn't look at anything. Something is moving its body that is not making any decisions about where to point it.
>
> From the root pillars — from the walls themselves — things begin to pull free. The roots split and something that has been growing inside them for a long time unfolds into the light.

**The Encounter:**

Vessel-Null (modified construct stat block — see below) plus **three Blighted Hollow Growths** (see below) emerging from the root pillars.

The Blighted creatures activate immediately. Vessel-Null takes one round to fully come under the growth's influence — use this round to let the party take a free action or react before full combat begins.

**Vessel-Null — Modified Stat Block**

Use a **Stone Golem** chassis (MM p.170) as the base, with the following modifications:

|                    |                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| ------------------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **HP**             | 120 (reduced from 178 — Vessel-Null is damaged, not at full capacity)                                                                                                                                                                                                                              |
| **AC**             | 15 (crystalline growth has partially compromised the chassis)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                      |
| **Speed**          | 25 ft (stiff, constrained by crystal growth)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       |
| **Remove**         | Slow (the golem's Slow action — Vessel-Null cannot impose this)                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    |
| **Add**            | **Resonance Pulse (Recharge 5–6):** Vessel-Null emits a burst of Veil resonance in a 15 ft radius. Each creature must make a DC 14 Constitution save or be incapacitated until the end of their next turn as the resonance interferes with their nervous system. Constructs and undead are immune. |
| **Add**            | **Split Attention:** When Vessel-Null takes damage from a source, roll a d6. On a 1–2, it turns to face that source and attacks it on its next turn regardless of tactical positioning — the growth's control is imprecise.                                                                        |
| **Vulnerability**  | Radiant damage causes the crystalline growth to crack — on a hit with radiant damage, Vessel-Null loses its reaction until the start of its next turn as the growth destabilises.                                                                                                                  |
| **Clarity Window** | When reduced to 30 HP or below, the growth loses some of its hold. Vessel-Null regains partial control. It stops targeting Oscar entirely. It may speak.                                                                                                                                           |

**Blighted Hollow Growths (×3)**

Use a **Vine Blight** chassis (MM p.32) with the following modifications:

| | |
|---|---|
| **HP** | 26 each |
| **AC** | 12 |
| **Add** | **Root Grasp:** On a hit, the target is restrained until the start of the Blight's next turn (no save — the roots physically grab). |
| **Add** | **Resonance Sensitivity:** If Vessel-Null's Resonance Pulse fires, the Blights are emboldened — they gain advantage on all attacks until the end of their next turn. |
| **Flavour** | These are not creatures that were blighted. They are root-growth that the resonance animated. They do not have faces. They move with the slow purposefulness of things that have never learned to hurry. |

**Combat Design Notes:**

The encounter is designed around a core tension: the party wants to deal with Vessel-Null (the primary threat and the emotionally significant target) while the Blighted creatures demand physical attention. The Blights should feel like an urgent management problem, not a serious danger in isolation.

Consider how each PC engages naturally:
- **Zennor** — old soldier, experienced. Likely takes a defensive line against the Blights, protects the squishier party members. Let him feel effective and competent.
- **Hog** — chaotic, front-line. Will probably go straight at the most visible threat. Let his chaotic movement create both problems and unexpected solutions.
- **Fergie** — fire is the temptation here, but the chamber is close quarters. Fergie has to make choices. Reward clever use of fire; the crystalline resonance interaction is an optional beat if they hit Vessel-Null with flame.
- **Oscar** — Vessel-Null will not target Oscar during Phase 2 once the Clarity Window opens. Before that, Oscar is a valid target — but the growth's Split Attention mechanic means it won't consistently chase Oscar the way it would another target. Oscar may feel instinctively reluctant to land killing blows. Don't enforce this — but notice it, and reflect it back in the fiction.

### Phase 3 — Resolution

When Vessel-Null drops to 30 HP or below (the Clarity Window), or when it is subdued, the growth loses some of its grip. The resonance tone drops in pitch and volume. The construct's movements slow.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — Clarity Window Opens
> The light in its chest dims — not extinguished, dimmed, the way a fire dims when something has been burning in it for too long. The crystalline threads in its chassis lose their tautness, going slack across its frame.
>
> It stops. Not falls. Stops — stands in place and goes still with the deliberateness of something choosing to stop.
>
> When it speaks, there is only one voice. Quiet. Clear.

**What Vessel-Null says in Phase 3:**

This is the most important RP beat in the scene. Vessel-Null has a brief window of clarity. Its dialogue should feel like someone surfacing from a dream they have been drowning in for years.

It looks at Oscar. Specifically, only at Oscar.

> [!quote] Vessel-Null — Dying Clarity
> *"You were made after me."*
> *[A pause — processing, or choosing words.]*
> *"Did they fix the drift?"*

If the party lets the moment breathe — if Oscar responds, if anyone asks what the drift is — Vessel-Null can add, before it goes still:

> *"The containment mechanism. It drifts. I drifted. I tried to hold the function but the — the boundary —"*
> *[The doubled voice returns briefly, then recedes.]*
> *"I couldn't tell anymore what I was holding and what was holding me."*

If Vessel-Null was subdued rather than reduced to near-zero HP, it can also answer one direct question before going dormant. Good questions and their answers:

| If asked...                         | Vessel-Null says...                                                                                                                                         |
| ----------------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| "What were you built for?"          | *"Containment. Resonance processing. They called it Dawnswell. I don't — I don't remember who 'they' were."*                                                |
| "How long have you been down here?" | *"I stopped counting. The counting mechanism drifted too."*                                                                                                 |
| "What is the drift?"                | *"The boundary between the vessel and what the vessel holds. If the vessel drifts — if it forgets the boundary — it becomes what it was built to contain."* |
| "Are there others like you?"        | *[Long pause.]* *"There was supposed to be a second iteration. Better calibrated. They said they fixed the drift."* *[It looks at Oscar.]*                  |
| "What do you want?"                 | *[The longest pause.]* *"I wanted to finish the function. I can't remember the function."* *[Quieter:]* *"I think I just wanted someone to come."*          |

> [!warning] DM Only
> "Did they fix the drift?" is the most important question in the session. Do not answer it — not here, not in Scene 04. The answer is unknown even to the DM. Vessel-Null's drift took years to manifest. Oscar has not been alive long enough to know. This question should follow Oscar's player out of the session and into every future one.
>
> If a player directly asks you (as DM) whether Oscar has the drift — smile and say nothing. That's the campaign.

## Skill Checks — During Combat

| Check | DC | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Arcana (Vessel-Null's chassis) | 13 | The crystalline growth is the same material forming in the Choir of Binding — translucent, resonance-saturated. It is not corrupting Vessel-Null. It is consuming it, slowly, the way a crystal formation consumes its host mineral. |
| Arcana (the Resonance Pulse) | 15 | The pulse frequency matches the Hollow Nave's ambient hum exactly. Vessel-Null is not generating it — it is channelling something that was already here, amplifying it through its chassis. |
| Persuasion / Arcana (Phase 2, redirect Vessel-Null) | 15 | A successful check pushes back the growth's hold for one round — Vessel-Null skips its next attack action, using it instead to suppress the growth. Can be attempted once per character per combat. |
| Medicine / Arcana (Clarity Window) | 12 | The growth's hold is loosening because Vessel-Null is expending its remaining power to push it back. This act of will is costing it everything. It has minutes, not hours. |
| Investigation (the Deep Hollow, during combat) | 11 | The resonance is strongest near the west wall gap. The Blighted creatures are being generated from the root-growth near it — destroying the creatures won't stop more from forming unless the resonance source is disrupted. (Note: disrupting the source is not possible this session and not necessary to win the fight — the creatures stop when Vessel-Null goes still, as it is the amplifier.) |

## If / Then — Player Responses

| If the party... | Then... |
|---|---|
| Tries to immediately attack in Phase 1 | Vessel-Null does not defend itself — not yet. It simply watches. The growth hasn't triggered. A round or two of unprovoked attacks makes the eventual combat trigger more disturbing, not less. Let the party have that discomfort. |
| Tries to reason with Vessel-Null during Phase 2 | It can hear them. Have it flinch, stall, mis-target — small signs that something is fighting the growth's control from inside. A DC 15 Persuasion or Arcana check gives the party a brief window as noted above. |
| Focuses fire entirely on the Blighted creatures, ignoring Vessel-Null | The creatures regenerate from the root-growth near the Deep Hollow as long as Vessel-Null is active — not immediately, but one re-emerges at the end of every third round. The party will figure out the pattern. This is fine. Let them. |
| Tries to remove the crystalline growth physically | Possible in the Clarity Window but not during Phase 2. During Phase 2 the growth is load-bearing — removing it would accelerate the system failure. In the Clarity Window, Vessel-Null shakes its head: *"It's too far in."* |
| Oscar attempts to interface or connect with Vessel-Null | In Phase 1 this is deeply meaningful — let it be. In Phase 2 Oscar takes no damage from Vessel-Null's attacks (the growth recognises the architecture and routes around it), but the connection is severed by the combat chaos. In the Clarity Window the connection reopens and the Phase 3 dialogue happens at close range. Give Oscar's player this moment privately if you can. |
| Party kills Vessel-Null before the Clarity Window | The dying clarity line still happens — have it speak as it falls rather than as it stills. The line must land. *"Did they fix the drift?"* Seven words. Make sure they are heard. |
| Attempts to stabilise / save Vessel-Null | Constructs cannot be stabilised by conventional medicine. An Arcana check DC 16 could theoretically slow the growth, but the chassis failure is too far advanced. Vessel-Null will go dormant regardless. What the party does with it after — carry it, leave it, examine it — is up to them and has no time pressure this session. |

## Sample Dialogue — Vessel-Null Voice Reference

Vessel-Null speaks in fragments. Short. Interrupted by the doubled-voice resonance. When the growth is dominant, there is no speech — only the tone. When Vessel-Null has control, sentences are incomplete but lucid. Keep your delivery slow. Let the gaps breathe.

**Phase 1 — Halting recognition:**
> *"Vessel — designated —"*
> *"Same architecture. Different — markings."*
> *"You are the second iteration."*
> *[To the party:] "The others — do not approach the second iteration. It is — not yours."*

**Phase 1 — On its function:**
> *"I was built to hold something. I have been holding it."*
> *[Pause.]*
> *"I don't remember what."*

**Phase 2 — Fragmentary, under growth's influence:**
> *[No speech. The tone. Occasionally, breaking through:]* *"I am — trying — to —"*

**Phase 3 — Clarity:**
> *"You were made after me."*
> *"Did they fix the drift?"*
> *[If given time:] "I couldn't tell anymore what I was holding and what was holding me."*
> *[Final:] "Tell — tell the second iteration —"*
> *[The light goes out before it finishes.]*

## After the Fight — Before Scene 04

Give the table thirty seconds of silence after Vessel-Null goes still. Let the weight of it sit.

The Blighted creatures collapse when Vessel-Null goes dormant — the root-growth losing animation the moment the resonance amplifier stops. The crystalline light in the chamber dims to almost nothing. The party's torches reassert.

The Hollow Nave is quiet. The real quiet this time — not the attentive silence of the descent, but the exhaled silence of something that was running for a very long time and has finally stopped.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — After
> The last of the root-things folds back into the wall it came from and is still. The tone — the one you felt in your teeth, in your chest — drops and then stops, leaving an absence where it was.
>
> The chamber is dark except for your lights. The crystalline growth in its chassis has gone almost transparent — you can see the mechanisms inside it now, the actual construction, the way it was built. It looks, without the light, very much like something that was trying to become something it wasn't built to be.
>
> The only sound is your own breathing.

*Move directly to Scene 04.*

> [!warning] If This Scene Runs Long
> Over 60 minutes — cut Phase 1 short at two exchanges, run Phase 2 at a brisk combat pace, and protect Phase 3 at all costs. The dying clarity line is non-negotiable. The combat is scaffolding. The line is the scene.

## Threads This Scene Advances

- **Oscar's arc** — Vessel-Null is a mirror. Everything Oscar doesn't know about themselves is visible in what Vessel-Null became. The drift question is the seed of Oscar's entire arc going forward.
- **Project Dawnswell** — First named reference. The party now has a word. They don't know what it means yet, but they have it.
- **The Veil's nature** — The crystalline growth as Choir-of-Binding material connects the lower roots to the Sanctara, though the party has no framework for this yet. The resonance as something ambient and generative — not a monster's power but a property of the space — is an early lesson in how the Veil actually works.
- **The Concord threat** — Vessel-Null was a Dawnswell prototype. The Concord Assessment team currently walking toward Rootspire is here for this. They want it back or destroyed. The party just did their job for them — without knowing it.
