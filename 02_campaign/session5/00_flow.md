## Scene 01 — Cold Open: "The Messenger"

**[[01_open]] | 15–25 min | Rootspire central square | Fixed — always first**

### What This Scene Does

Delivers the session's ticking clock. A courier named Pell brings word that a Concord Harmonic Assessment unit is approaching — three days out, he says. Both facts are the scene. The party learns there's a construct like Oscar sighted in the lower roots. That's their move.

### Must-Hit Beats

> [!important] ① News arrives — Concord unit inbound, three days out. ② The clock is set — something is coming regardless. ③ Lower roots lead is surfaced — this is where the party is going.

### Running Notes

- Let the party have one or two lines of post-Session-4 chat before Pell appears.
- Pell **touches his satchel strap** when he's omitting something. Use it **twice** — when asked about the message source, and when confirming the timeline. If the players catch it: DC 12 Insight.
- The letter's seal was already broken before Pell received it. Someone read it in transit and let it continue. Surface this only if they check (DC 14 Investigation).
- The Guile sigil beneath Vision marks on Pell's documents: only surface if a player specifically investigates his papers (DC 14 Perception).
- Pell leaves Rootspire the same day. He's done with this job.

### Key Atmosphere Beats (Layer In)

- The Great Tree's bark has a hairline fissure at the square's edge. Getting wider, people say.
- A vendor sells crude carved figures that look like a warforged. Three of them. Made after someone described seeing one below.
- A child stares at Oscar: _"Does it hurt, being made of metal?"_ Then runs.

### Scene 01 → Scene 02 Transition

> [!warning] DM Transition Note — Say This Before Moving On _"Pell takes his coin and is gone before you've finished reading the letter twice. The crowd moves around you like water around stones. Somewhere below your feet — below the roots, below the foundations, below everything Rootspire was built on — something that looks like Oscar has been seen. The letter can wait. The descent can't."_

**Trigger:** Party decides to head toward the lower roots. **If they stall:** A local in the lower market mentions the construct was seen again this morning. The clock is running. Redirect them down. **If they go to the Council of 8:** One of the Eight says only: _"We are aware. This changes your timeline, not your task. Begin the descent."_ Back to Scene 02.

## Scene 02 — Descent: "Into the Lower Roots"

**[[02_descent]] | 20–30 min | Beneath Rootspire | Follows 01**

### What This Scene Does

Establishes the lower roots as uncanny rather than hostile. Seeds Oscar's arc through the terrarium's orientation. Grounds the Veil's presence as ambient and ancient. Delivers Maren if there's room for a warm human moment.

### Must-Hit Beats

> [!important] ① Atmosphere lands — uneasy, not threatened. Something predates them here. ② Oscar's terrarium orients — pointing ahead and slightly down, toward a frequency, not toward light. ③ Signs of habitation — Maren's offerings. Someone tends this place. The party is not the first to move through this dark with purpose.

### Running Notes

- The groundskeeper at the iron gate asks no questions if they have a Council chit or a good story.
- The descent takes approximately one hour of careful movement — use it for atmosphere and discovery, not navigation challenge.
- **No random encounters on the descent.** The danger is psychological.
- The shadow pulse in the first gallery (slow, rhythmic contraction) is real — it's the Ashroot Resonance moving through the root mass. DC 14 Arcana to identify it.
- **Maren Ashwick (Optional):** Late 60s, broad-shouldered, canvas apron, unnaturally steady lantern flame. She tends the lower roots — has for 30 years. She calls the Hollow Nave "the Old Shrine." She knows something changed recently: _"The air down near the Shrine — it hums. Didn't used to hum."_ She will not lead them further down. She won't say why.

### Veil Cues — One or Two Per Descent

| PC     | Cue                                                                                                                                                 |
| ------ | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Zennor | A hollow at eye level. Inside: a tiny carved figure, dressed in a scrap of cloth. Carefully tied. He alone notices it.                              |
| Oscar  | Their reflection in still water shows them standing completely still while the world moves. When Oscar moves, the reflection catches up — normally. |
| Fergie | The darkness between light sources has colour — ember-orange. It moves when Fergie isn't looking directly at it.                                    |
| Hog    | Roots have grown in the unmistakable shape of a fighting stance. Wide base. Extended arm. Definitely just root growth.                              |

### Pre-Concord Carvings (Optional — Halfway Down)

Thirty kneeling figures. Faces turned _away_ from a deliberately blank centre. Inscription: _"We held it here before the city knew to fear it."_ DC 14 History: predates Rootspire by 400+ years. DC 15 Arcana: the blank circle is not empty — something of what was venerated here still is here.

### Scene 02 → Scene 03 Transition

The passage widens into the Hollow Nave. Read the arrival text:

> [!quote] The passage widens and then opens — properly opens, into a space large enough to stand in fully, to spread your arms. The roots have pulled back here, curving around the walls like pillars that decided not to fall inward.
> 
> The stone beneath your feet is worn smooth in a rough circle at the centre, the way stone gets worn by feet returning to the same place over centuries.
> 
> And at the far end — still. Watching. A shape that is not root and not stone. That has a chassis. That has eyes, and those eyes are lit.

> [!warning] DM Transition Note — Say This Before Moving On _Don't say anything. Let the read-aloud land. Cut directly to Scene 03. The Hollow Nave describes itself._

**Time check:** If Scene 02 is running past 30 minutes — cut Maren, cut the carvings, cut the Veil cues. Run only the three musts. The Hollow Nave read-aloud closes the scene regardless.

## Scene 03 — Combat: "The Hollow Nave"

**[[03_combat]] | 45–60 min | The Hollow Nave | Follows 02 — cannot be separated from 04**

### What This Scene Does

Three-phase encounter: contact with Vessel-Null → combat → dying clarity. The combat is scaffolding. The line _"Did they fix the drift?"_ is the scene.

### Must-Hit Beats

> [!important] ① Vessel-Null's first words are fragmented and directed at Oscar. This happens **before** any combat begins. ② The combat trigger is the Veil-growth — not Vessel-Null's will. This is not an enemy. It is a tragedy. ③ **"Did they fix the drift?"** This line must land before Vessel-Null goes still. Non-negotiable. Protect it.

### The Three Phases

**Phase 1 — Contact (RP — up to 5 minutes)** Vessel-Null stands at the worn stone centre. Eyes track the party one by one. Stop at Oscar. The doubled voice:

> _"Vessel designated — function: containment — primary directive: hold — hold — hold what. Hold what. Hold —"_

Let Oscar respond. Let anyone speak. Vessel-Null can:

- Recognise Oscar as the same architecture. May reach toward them — compass needle finding north.
- Ask: _"What is your designation?"_ or _"Are you the second iteration?"_
- Express, in fragments, that it has been trying to complete a function it can no longer remember.

Phase 1 lasts as long as RP sustains it. Combat trigger is dramatic, not timed.

**Phase 2 — Combat** The crystalline growth spasms. A sustained tone rises — felt in back teeth. The construct stops choosing where to point itself.

_Blighted Hollow Growths (×3)_ pull free from the root pillars simultaneously.

**Vessel-Null stat block (Stone Golem base, modified):**

- HP: 120 | AC: 15 | Speed: 25 ft.
- Remove: Slow action
- Add: **Resonance Pulse (Recharge 5–6)** — 15 ft radius, DC 14 Con save or incapacitated until end of next turn. Constructs/undead immune.
- Add: **Split Attention** — on taking damage, roll d6. On 1–2, it turns to face that source on its next turn regardless of positioning.
- Radiant damage cracks the crystal — on radiant hit, Vessel-Null loses its reaction until start of next turn.
- **Clarity Window opens at 30 HP.**

**Blighted Hollow Growths (Vine Blight base, modified):**

- HP: 26 | AC: 12
- Add: **Root Grasp** — on hit, target is restrained until start of Blight's next turn (no save).
- Add: **Resonance Sensitivity** — if Vessel-Null's Pulse fires, Blights gain advantage on all attacks until end of their next turn.
- Blights collapse automatically when Vessel-Null goes dormant.

**PC Combat Notes:**

- Zennor — natural defensive line vs. Blights. Let him feel effective.
- Hog — will go straight at the biggest threat. Let his chaos create one absurd moment that accidentally works. Give it room to breathe.
- Fergie — if fire hits the crystalline growth, it resonates instead of burns. A single low clear tone, then dark. Fergie alone recognises this as a non-standard material interaction.
- Oscar — Vessel-Null will not target Oscar once the Clarity Window opens. Before that, Split Attention may route around them anyway. Do not force Oscar's reluctance to deal killing blows — but notice it.

**Phase 3 — Dying Clarity (Clarity Window — RP)** At 30 HP or below, the growth loses its grip. One voice. Quiet. Clear.

> [!quote] _"You were made after me."_ _[Pause.]_ _"Did they fix the drift?"_

If the party lets the moment breathe — if Oscar responds — Vessel-Null can add:

> _"The containment mechanism. It drifts. I drifted. I tried to hold the function but the — the boundary — I couldn't tell anymore what I was holding and what was holding me."_

**If subdued (not killed):** One direct question can be answered before dormancy. Key answers:

|Question|Answer|
|---|---|
|"What were you built for?"|_"Containment. Resonance processing. They called it Dawnswell."_|
|"How long have you been down here?"|_"I stopped counting. The counting mechanism drifted too."_|
|"What is the drift?"|_"The boundary between the vessel and what the vessel holds. If the vessel drifts — if it forgets the boundary — it becomes what it was built to contain."_|
|"Are there others like you?"|_[Long pause.] "There was supposed to be a second iteration. Better calibrated. They said they fixed the drift."_ [Looks at Oscar.]|
|"What do you want?"|_"I wanted to finish the function. I can't remember the function."_ [Quieter:] _"I think I just wanted someone to come."_|

### After the Fight — Before Moving On

Give the table 30 seconds of silence after Vessel-Null goes still. The Blights fold back into the walls. The tone drops and stops.

> [!quote] The last of the root-things folds back into the wall it came from and is still. The tone — the one you felt in your teeth, in your chest — drops and then stops, leaving an absence where it was.
> 
> The chamber is dark except for your lights. The crystalline growth in its chassis has gone almost transparent — you can see the mechanisms inside it now, the actual construction, the way it was built. It looks, without the light, very much like something that was trying to become something it wasn't built to be.
> 
> The only sound is your own breathing.

### Scene 03 → Scene 04 Transition

> [!warning] DM Transition Note — Say This Before Moving On _"Vessel-Null is still. Whatever was running it — fighting for it and against it at the same time — has stopped. The question is still in the air. 'Did they fix the drift?' Seven words and nothing to answer them with. Not yet. Oscar — you're looking at something. What is it?"_

Cut directly to Scene 04. Do not pause between scenes.

## Scene 04 — Revelation: "Oscar's Mirror"

**[[04_revelation]] | 15–20 min | The Hollow Nave | Follows 03 directly — cannot be separated**

### What This Scene Does

Oscar finds the crystalline housing inside Vessel-Null's chest — the predecessor to their terrarium. The question shifts from _"What am I?"_ to _"What was I made to be?"_ and underneath that, unspoken: _"Does that have to be what I am?"_ Level-up lands here.

### Must-Hit Beats

> [!important] ① Oscar finds the crystalline housing — a secondary chamber inside Vessel-Null's chest, built to hold something specific. Its architecture is identical to Oscar's terrarium. Not similar. The same principle. ② The question shifts. Present the questions without stating them. Create the conditions and protect the silence. ③ Level-up is called. Frame it as transformation, not a mechanical pause.

### Running Notes

- **Do not answer the drift question.** Not here. Not in Scene 05. Oscar doesn't know. That's the campaign.
- **The terrarium response:** When the housing is nearby, the plant goes completely still — then turns to face the housing. Recognition, not distress. Two tuning forks. (DC 16 Arcana to surface this framework.)
- **Oscar's choice:** At the right moment, ask Oscar's player directly: _"What does Oscar tell the party about what they found?"_ Do not steer it. Whatever they say is correct. Note it — it will matter.
    - _If Oscar shares everything:_ Scene 05's terrarium sync lands as confirmation. The party has context.
    - _If Oscar shares nothing:_ Scene 05 lands in isolation. Oscar alone understands what is happening. More painful, arguably better.
    - _If Oscar shares some:_ Oscar is already deciding who they are in front of the party. This is the beginning of who Oscar will become.
- **Vessel-Null's body:** Ask the party what they do with it. No time pressure. No correct answer. What they choose says something.

### PC Quiet Moments (Use One or Two)

|PC|Beat|
|---|---|
|Zennor|The worn stone circle at the chamber's centre. Foot-worn over centuries. He stands in it. It fits him without trying. He knows this feeling. He's been standing inside it for years.|
|Hog|In the gap left by a dissolved Blight — a stone, fist-sized, perfectly smooth, perfectly round. Warm. Faintly humming. No natural explanation. He should probably keep it.|
|Fergie|The crystalline growth is dimming on Vessel-Null's chassis. Near the joints where it was thickest — the dying light is ember-orange. The same colour as the dark on the descent.|

### The Level-Up Frame

> [!quote] There is a stillness in this chamber that feels different from the silence you descended through. That was the quiet of something waiting. This is the quiet of something that has just happened — the particular hush that follows a door opening, when the air from two rooms is still deciding how to mix.
> 
> You are not the same people who came down the stairs. You've fought something that didn't want to fight you. You've heard a question that has no easy answer. Something in the world has noticed. Take a moment. You've earned it.

While the table handles mechanics, check in quietly with Oscar's player: _"What does Oscar do with the housing?"_ and _"What does Oscar tell the party?"_ These are questions to hold, not answer right now.

### Scene 04 → Scene 05 Transition

> [!quote] Read Aloud — Beginning the Climb The way out is the way you came. The passage feels different going up — narrower somehow, the dark less attentive. As if whatever was watching on the descent has turned its attention elsewhere.
> 
> Halfway up, without deciding to, all of you look back down. The Hollow Nave's light — Vessel-Null's light — is gone. There is only dark below now.
> 
> Then the dark pulses. Once. Slowly. And is still.

> [!warning] DM Transition Note — Say This Before Moving On _"The surface air hits you after an hour underground — cold, pine, wet earth. The Great Tree is up there. The sky is real. And somewhere two hours' walk into the Ashroot, one of the four resonance sites is waiting for you to observe it. The Council's task is still active. The clock is still running. Which direction?"_

**Time Check:** If Scenes 01–04 have run past 2 hours, consider whether Scene 05 runs today or defers to Session 6. Scene 06 (the boot prints) can anchor without Scene 05 — relocate the prints to Rootspire's outer gate. Do not skip Scene 06 under any circumstances.

## Scene 05 — Resonance: "The Listening Rock"

**[[05_resonance]] | 25–35 min | Forest clearing, 2 hours from Rootspire | Follows 04 — deferrable to S6**

### What This Scene Does

First resonance site observed. Oscar's terrarium syncs with the pulse — briefly, involuntarily, then dims. Mira names the stone. The party learns the Concord has been here before. Scene 06's boot prints land harder after this.

### Must-Hit Beats

> [!important] ① The resonance is felt before it is seen — pressure in the chest, warmth through the soles. ② Mira arrives and names it the Listening Rock. Her thirty years of observation reframes the resonance from threat to something older and stranger. ③ Oscar's terrarium syncs with the pulse — same frequency, same rhythm — then dims. This lands differently depending on what Oscar shared in Scene 04. Protect it either way.

### Running Notes

- **The Council said observe, don't intervene.** Nothing here requires action. The temptation will be real. Let restraint feel like the right call.
- The pulse fires every ~3 minutes with slight organic drift.
- Pulse effects during each cycle: luminous air quality (dusk-light), shadow contraction toward the stone, reflection anomaly (party sees themselves smiling — gone when looked at directly), warmth intensifies within 5 feet of the stone.
- Birdsong stops on approach (~40 min out). Returns briefly between pulses, then leaves again.

### Mira Ashwick (NPC)

Early 50s. Sun-lined, unhurried. Dark hair going grey. Canvas satchel with offerings. Arrives 10–15 min after party reaches the stone — she comes on her own schedule.

Key dialogue moments:

- **On the stone's history:** _"My mother came here. Her mother before her, I think. It's always been here. It's always done this. The pulse feels like company. Something very patient, paying attention."_
- **On the Concord visit:** _"They came through — eight, maybe nine years ago. Instruments I didn't recognise. Very polite. Left without saying what they found. The stone went quiet that same week. Nearly a year of silence. I kept coming anyway. Then one morning I felt it again, and I cried."_
- **On Oscar specifically:** _"The stone likes you. Or notices you. Hard to tell the difference with something that old. The pulse interval — it's shorter while you're here."_
- **On what she thinks it is:** _"I think it's a place where something very large is very close to the surface. Like you can hear a voice through a wall, but you can't make out the words. Except the wall has been there so long everyone forgot to wonder what's on the other side."_

### The Terrarium Sync — The Scene's Centrepiece

Time this after Mira's conversation has run its course, during a natural pause. If Oscar is within 10 feet of the stone, the sync happens on the next pulse automatically.

> [!quote] The pulse comes — the familiar pressure in the sternum, the warmth rising through the soles of your feet —
> 
> And the terrarium answers.
> 
> Not brightly. Not dramatically. The plant inside goes completely still — stiller than still — and then, slowly, it pulses. Once. In time with the stone. The same frequency, the same rhythm, the same interval.
> 
> For three seconds, maybe four, the terrarium and the Listening Rock are breathing together.
> 
> Then the terrarium dims. Not extinguished — dims, the way a lamp dims when you turn the wick down deliberately.
> 
> It looks, if a plant in a small glass container can look like anything, like something that has just received a message it has been waiting a very long time to receive.

**Hold the beat. Do not narrate past it. Let the table respond.**

### Zennor's Grief — Optional, Read the Room

If the moment is right — deliver this privately or to the table:

The pulse has a shape. The shape is familiar. The specific weight of grief carried so long it has stopped being grief and become identity. Zennor knows this weight. The pulse is not his grief — it is older and larger. But it recognises the shape of his. Two different songs sharing a chord. For the duration of the pulse he does not feel alone inside it.

Then the pulse releases. Nothing has resolved. But something in the stone knows what he carries.

### Scene 05 → Scene 06 Transition

> [!quote] Read Aloud — Leaving the Clearing Mira doesn't watch you go — she's already settled at the stone's edge with her candle lit and her eyes closed, the pulse moving through her the same way it moved through you, but slower, quieter, like she's been practising the receiving of it for a long time.
> 
> The clearing falls behind you. The treeline closes. The birdsong returns — gradually at first, then all at once, as if a signal was given.
> 
> The walk back to Rootspire is two hours. Somewhere behind you, every three minutes, something breathes.

> [!warning] DM Transition Note — Say This Before Moving On _"The light is going soft and amber in the canopy. You're familiar with this path now — you've walked it once. Your own footprints from the outward journey are pressed into the softer sections of the mud."_ [Beat.] _"You notice them because they're on top of yours."_

Move directly into Scene 06 without breaking.

## Scene 06 — Closing Hook: "Boots in the Mud"

**[[06_close]] | 5–10 min | The path back to Rootspire | Fixed — always last**

### What This Scene Does

One beat. Military boot prints, fresh, heading toward the clearing the party just left. The "three days out" claim is revealed as false. The party is not ahead of the Concord. They have never been ahead of the Concord.

### Must-Hit Beats

> [!important] ① Fresh Concord boot prints — military standard, on top of the party's own outward prints, heading toward the Listening Rock. ② The callback: _"Three days out."_ Three words. The entire weight of Scene 01 landing retroactively.

### Running the Beat — Step by Step

1. Establish the walk back — one or two sentences of normalcy. The light is good. The party is tired but intact.
2. The incidental discovery — do not announce that something significant is about to happen.
3. Let the realisation land — give the physical details. Do not editorialise.
4. The callback. Three words.
5. **End the session.** Give each player one sentence of reaction if they want it. Then close.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — The Discovery The path back to Rootspire is familiar now — you've walked it twice, the light has gone soft and amber in the canopy overhead, the birdsong has come back around you. Your own footprints from the outward journey are visible in the softer sections of the path, pressed into the mud from this morning.
> 
> You notice them because they are on top of yours.
> 
> Fresh prints. Military-standard sole — the kind of deep, even tread that comes from boots made for long distances and hard ground. Four sets, overlapping slightly, moving in the same direction: toward the clearing you just left.
> 
> They are not from this morning. The mud at their edges hasn't had time to dry.
> 
> They were made while you were at the stone.

_[Hold for reaction.]_

> [!quote] Read Aloud — The Closer Look One set of prints is heavier than the others — deeper heel impression, longer stride. The others are even, measured, the prints of people who have been walking a long time and have found their pace. All four sets continue past you on the path and do not return.
> 
> They did not come back this way.
> 
> Three days out.

**End session.**

> [!warning] DM Only If a player says "I follow the prints" — let the trail go cold within ten minutes in failing light. Award the instinct. End on the cold trail. It's a sharper hook. If the table goes completely quiet — perfect. That silence is the scene working. End there.

### [[EXT_champions_return|EXT: Champion's Return]] — Hog Arc

**Primary PC:** Hog (Dawson) | **15–20 minutes** | Slot at The Split Bark

A road-worn fighter named Pellin Brast enters the tavern. Scans the room. Finds Hog. Sits down uninvited.

_"Thunderwood. I heard you were dead."_

> [!quote] _(He taps it once with one finger.)_ "First round buy-in. Consider it a gift. Or throw it in the river. Up to you."

Near-death exertion and crowd fear produce a harmonic yield compatible with Veil containment. ### [[EXT_heat_without_flame|EXT: Heat Without Flame]] — Fergie Arc

**Primary PC:** Fergie (Paul) | **20–35 minutes** | Slot after surfacing

The notice board in Rootspire has a posting about "a woman walking through smoke untouched" in the lower eastern roots. A worker named Seldris filed it.

The burn site: geometric, cold, inverted — heat that moved inward rather than out. The figure who left it had blue flame for hair.

**Fergie-specific beat:** Within 5 feet of the scorched section, the Porter Stone goes briefly, utterly silent. The constant hum stops. A held breath. Then resumes. **He alone feels this.** Do not explain it.

If they pursue the figure: she's fast, knows the tunnels, and is gone through a sealed root junction. She leaves a handprint — palm flat, cold at the centre.

If they report back to Seldris: _"We call it ashbirth. Fire that doesn't want to burn. I thought it was a children's story."_ He pays the reward: 3 gp.

> [!warning] DM Only This is **Maria Zurita**. She leaves the cold-centre handprints passively wherever she channels — she doesn't know she does it. The Concord Vision branch tracks her movement through Rootspire by finding them. If the party takes a rubbing: file it. It becomes a clue when Concord agents arrive asking questions. Her Porter Stone reaction to Fergie is the thread that will eventually connect them.
