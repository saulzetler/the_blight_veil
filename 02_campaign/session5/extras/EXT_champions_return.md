# EXT — Champion's Return

> **Modular Scene — Slot into Session 5 if running long.**
> Source hook: [[return_to_rootspire]] Notice Board
> Primary PC: Hog Thunderwood
> Session 5 content: foreshadow only — full arc is a future campaign thread.

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene gives Hog a moment of unexpected public recognition — being *seen* by someone from before — and plants the seed of a long arc around the Orrenval underground fighting circuit. Nothing is resolved. The point is the specific weight of being found by your past in a place where you thought you were just passing through.

**Estimated length (Session 5 foreshadow):** 15–20 minutes.

---

## Session 5 Beat — The Recognition

This happens naturally inside **The Split Bark** tavern, during any moment of relative calm — the party drinking, watching the arena game, catching their breath.

A fighter enters. Big. Road-worn. Cauliflower ear. A jaw that's been reset once, badly. Knuckles like cobblestones. He's traveling light — a fighter's pack, nothing decorative. He scans the room the way fighters do: threat assessment, then exits.

His eyes find Hog.

He stops scanning.

---

### Read-Aloud: The Recognition

> [!quote]
> The man goes still in the way that only happens when something genuinely surprises someone who stopped being surprised years ago.
>
> He crosses the room without hurrying. Sets his cup down uninvited. Pulls a stool out and sits across from Hog like he was always going to be there.
>
> "Thunderwood." Not a question. Low, flat, certain. "I heard you were dead."

---

## Pellin Brast — NPC Brief

**Name:** Pellin Brast. No circuit name — he was a handler, not a headliner. He managed three fighters under the Orrenval underground circuit, including one who faced Hog in a semifinal bout years ago. He didn't bet against Hog that night. He knew better.

### What He Knows

- Hog's record. The specific bouts people still talk about.
- The circuit went dark roughly two years ago — Concord pressure, they said. Some fighters disappeared. A few came back wrong — quieter, slower, not from injury.
- He's heard the circuit is reopening. Concord-sanctioned this time. Rebranded. Cleaner on the surface.

### What He's Doing in Rootspire

Traveling. Nominally. He's actually quietly sounding out fighters willing to sign with the new operation. He doesn't say this explicitly.

He is not a villain. He is a pragmatist who misses the work and hasn't yet asked the right questions about it.

---

### Dialogue Guidance

> [!quote]
> "They're calling it the *Vindication Rounds* now. Cleaner name. Concord's got their hands in it — which means money, which means fighters." *(He drinks.)* "Which means it's not clean at all, but that was always true."

> [!quote]
> "There's a name you'd know on the roster. Borlan Ketch. Called himself your brother once, the way fighters do. He's signed. First wave."

> [!warning]
> **DM Note — Borlan Ketch:** Ketch is Hog's former training partner from his early circuit days — someone who trained beside him, taught him something, or owed him a drink after a hard bout. Establish this lightly and let Dawson fill in the texture. Ketch has gone Concord-aligned — for money, protection, or something he wasn't given a choice about. He may genuinely believe in what the new circuit stands for. He may have run out of other options. Leave it undefined for now.

---

### The Token

Before he leaves, Pellin sets something on the table:

> [!quote]
> A stamped metal disc. The size of a large coin but twice as thick. One face is blank. The other is pressed with a mark — a circle bisected by a vertical line. Old circuit token. Fight invitation, back in the day.
>
> *(He taps it once with one finger.)*
>
> "First round buy-in. Consider it a gift. Or throw it in the river. Up to you."

He doesn't wait for an answer. Finishes his drink and leaves.

**The token is real.** It grants entry to the first Vindication Round if presented at the correct gate in Orrenval. The party doesn't know this yet.

> [!important]
> **Hog's moment.** After Pellin leaves, give the table silence. The token is sitting there. It points directly back to who Hog was.
>
> What does he do with it?
>
> Don't push for an answer. Let the question live in the room.

# Full Future Arc — The Vindication Rounds

> [!warning]
> **DM Eyes Only — Far Future Campaign Content**

---

## What the Vindication Rounds Actually Are

The Concord-sanctioned circuit is not entertainment. It is a **resonance harvest operation** run by the Might branch.

The original underground circuit was chaotic — uncontrolled adrenaline, pain, near-death. The Might branch recognized it as an accidental resonance engine years ago. Crowds of thousands, fighters pushed to the edge of dying, the harmonic frequency of near-death exertion and collective crowd fear: the resonance signature is compatible with Veil containment conduits. What the execution yards do deliberately, the circuit was doing accidentally.

The Vindication Rounds formalize this. Purpose-built arenas with resonance receivers embedded beneath the fighting floor. Fighters sign contracts with language engineered to obscure what they're consenting to. The closer a bout gets to a death, the harder the conduit draws. Champions — long-term fighters who survive season after season — are slowly, imperceptibly drained. They don't age faster. They simply feel *less*. Dream less. Their memories flatten at the edges. They become very good at fighting and very empty at everything else.

**Borlan Ketch** is three bouts into this. He doesn't know why he feels wrong. He thinks it's training fatigue. He sleeps ten hours and wakes exhausted.

---

## Arc Beats

### Beat 1 — The Invitation Becomes Relevant *(Mid-Campaign)*

Orrenval draws the party back for any reason — resonance sites, Concord tension, another thread. Hog's token gets them through a guarded sector. The underground fighters' quarter is one of the few parts of Orrenval where Concord oversight runs loose enough to move without scrutiny. The token opens a door.

---

### Beat 2 — Finding Ketch

Hog locates Borlan Ketch. He's changed in ways Hog can't fully name. Quieter. Cooperative in a way that feels wrong for someone Hog knew. The competence is still there — he can still fight — but the *fire* is gone. He tries to get Hog to sign on.

> [!quote]
> "It's legitimate now. Good money. They leave you alone between bouts."

He believes what he's saying. That's the horror of it.

---

### Beat 3 — What's Under the Floor

If the party investigates — Hog's instinct, the party's snooping, or an accidentally-discovered access hatch beneath the arena — they find resonance conduit infrastructure. It's not immediately clear what it does. But it pulses in the aftermath of fights. It draws hardest when someone nearly dies.

There are records here. Fighters listed not by name but by *resonance yield*. Some entries run for many seasons. Some stop mid-season without explanation.

---

### Beat 4 — The Main Event

The circuit pushes Hog to fight. Publicly. Spectacularly. The crowd wants Thunderwood back. The Concord wants the resonance output from a circuit legend's return — a beloved, famous fighter performing at peak produces a harmonic yield worth more than a hundred ordinary bouts.

Hog can refuse. He can fight and expose the circuit mid-bout. He can fight and not understand what's happening until it's done.

All three are valid outcomes.

---

### Beat 5 — Ketch's Choice

When the truth of the Vindication Rounds becomes clear, Ketch has to decide: the organization that has been slowly hollowing him out, or the people telling him the truth of what's happening to him.

This is not a guaranteed rescue. Ketch may not be able to process it. He may be too drained to want to change. He may have given enough of himself to the machine that part of him *belongs* to it now.

> [!important]
> **The thematic core:**
>
> The circuit was always feeding on something — it just wasn't honest about what. Hog's arc asks whether the thing he was known for, the violence he built a name on, was ever really his — or whether it was always being harvested by something larger that never asked his permission.
>
> The answer is not that fighting is wrong. It's about consent, knowledge, and what you do when you find out the floor was hollow the whole time.

> - Hog gains either a formal title or a specific infamy in Orrenval depending on how it ends. Both have downstream use.
