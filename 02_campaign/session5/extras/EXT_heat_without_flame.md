# EXT — Heat Without Flame

> **Modular Scene — Slot into Session 5 if running long.**
> Source hook: [[return_to_rootspire]] Notice Board
> NPC source: [[maria_zurita]]
> Primary PC: Fergie

---

## Scene Purpose

This scene introduces the **first trace of Maria Zurita** without naming her. It rewards investigation, gives Fergie an unexplained and personal jolt, and seeds the Echo thread as a slow-burn. Nothing is resolved. Nothing is named. The scene is about *wrongness* — fire that doesn't behave like fire, a presence that chose to leave rather than engage.

Drop in after the party surfaces from the lower roots, or as a detour if they follow the notice board before descending.

**Estimated length:** 20–35 minutes.

---

## Entry Point

The party reads or re-reads the notice board post:

> [!quote]
> *"Seeking information regarding controlled flame events near lower eastern roots. No embers found. Witness reports describe 'a woman walking through smoke untouched.' Reward offered. Speak to Seldris at the Spiral Loom."*

---

## Seldris — Brief NPC

Spiral Loom worker. Middle-aged. Soot on his forearms. He filed the post and now half wishes he hadn't, because someone confirming it means it was real.

He does not follow them down. He gives directions and gets out.

> [!quote]
> "I wasn't imagining it. The bark was dark — scorched — but there was no heat. I checked with my bare hand. Cold as stone. And the smoke... it just *rose*, like something had been burning the air itself. And there was a woman. Blue light. She didn't look at me. She just — moved through it."

**Location:** Lower eastern root-shaft, below the sap collection platforms, near a junction marked with two crossed iron hooks.

---

## Scene Location — Lower Eastern Root-Shaft

### Read-Aloud: Arrival

> [!quote]
> The shaft narrows here.
>
> Wood presses close on both sides — bark ridged like old scar tissue, lanterns replaced by bioluminescent lichen that pulses slow and irregular.
>
> The air smells wrong. Not rot. Not smoke. Something beneath smoke — the memory of burning, without the burning itself.
>
> A section of root wall ahead is dark. Matte, flat black, in a shape too deliberate for accident — not a charred streak, but a pressed absence. Like heat was applied from the inside out.

---

### Investigation

| Check | DC | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Investigation | 12 | The burn pattern is geometric — concentric arcs, precise. Not fire damage. Not blight. Something structured. |
| Arcana | 14 | The residue is cold and carries faint harmonic resonance. This is not mundane fire. |
| Arcana | 17 | The signature matches Genasi flame-craft, but *inverted* — as if the heat moved inward rather than out. |
| Nature | 13 | No insects here. The lichen near the mark has stopped pulsing. Not dead — just *still*. |
| Perception | 15 | Footprints in the damp bark-dust. Small. Precise. They stop mid-corridor, as if whoever made them simply ceased to walk. |

---

### Fergie-Specific Beat

> [!important]
> If Fergie gets within 5 feet of the scorched section — or touches it — his **Porter Stone reacts**.
>
> It does not pulse outward. It does the opposite: it goes briefly, utterly *silent*. The constant low hum he has felt since his ritual simply stops. A held breath.
>
> Then it resumes. Normal. As if nothing happened.
>
> **He alone feels this.** No one else notices unless he says something.

Do not explain this. Do not flag it. Let it sit with the player.

---

### The Sighting

After the investigation beat, Perception DC 14:

> [!quote]
> At the far end of the junction — perhaps forty feet away, where the corridor bends and the lichen thins to nothing — something moves.
>
> A figure.
>
> Layered in geometric fabric. Bronze filigree catching the dim light. Her hair is on fire.
>
> Not red. Not orange.
>
> **Blue.**
>
> She turns her head slightly — not enough to show a face — and then she steps around the bend and she is gone.

Pursuit is possible. She is faster than she looks and knows the tunnels.

---

### If They Pursue

| Check | DC | Result |
|---|---|---|
| Athletics | 13 | They gain ground. |
| Stealth (opposing) | — | She is not sneaking. She is simply *fast* and knows where she's going. |
| Perception | 16 | A glimpse: her palm pressed flat against a root junction, a faint blue ember sealing a gap in the wood. Then she's through it. |

The sealed gap is warm. Not hot. Warm like a held hand.

The corridor beyond is a dead end. She is gone.

---

### What Remains

At the sealed junction, on the ground:

> [!quote]
> A single handprint burned into the bark. Palm flat, fingers spread.
>
> The center of the print is not burned. It is *cold*. A small dark circle where the heat never touched — like the eye of something looking up from inside the wood.

> [!warning]
> **DM Note:** The cold-center handprint is an Echo marker. Maria leaves these passively wherever she channels — she doesn't know she does it. The Concord Vision branch tracks her movement through Rootspire by finding them.
>
> If the party takes a rubbing or records the print: file it. It becomes a clue thread when Concord agents show up asking questions.

---

## What the Party Knows When They Leave

- Someone with fire abilities has been moving through the lower roots.
- Their fire is cold, or inverted, or *wrong* in a way no one can articulate.
- She was watching the party. She chose to leave rather than engage.
- Fergie's Porter Stone responded to something. He may or may not have mentioned this.

**Her name is not given. Her allegiance is not given. That is correct.**

---

## Return to Seldris

If they report back:

> [!quote]
> "Cold burn." *(He exhales slowly.)* "Yes. That's — yes. That's exactly what I saw."
>
> He is quiet for a moment.
>
> "There's a word for that, among the old weavers here. We call it *ashbirth*. Fire that doesn't want to burn. I thought it was a children's story."

He pays the reward: **3 gp**. He has nothing else. He's visibly unsettled that someone confirmed it.
