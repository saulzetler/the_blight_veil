## Session 5 Beat — The Sighting

**When to drop this:** Any moment of stillness near the end of the session. Ideal placement: after the party surfaces from the lower roots, while crossing one of Rootspire's outer platforms above the river that runs beneath the tree's southern base. Can also be set during a quiet stretch at The Split Bark.

**Who sees it:** Zennor alone. This is intentional.

> [!important]
> This beat requires no skill checks. It is a moment of recognition — not from the woman, but from Zennor.
>
> The only question you need to ask Kyle is: *"What does Zennor do?"*

---

### Read-Aloud: The Sighting

> [!quote]
> The river is dark here, below the platform's edge. Rootspire's lanterns don't reach the water — just the sound of it, low and continuous, running under everything.
>
> Zennor — you don't know why your eyes go to the far bank.
>
> They just do.
>
> She is standing there. Still. The distance makes her small, but the glow is unmistakable: not firelight, not moonlight — something older and quieter. The color of a lantern seen through closed eyelids.
>
> She does not call out. She does not gesture. She stands at the waterline with both hands at her sides and she is looking directly at you.
>
> You blink.
>
> She is gone.
>
> No splash. No footsteps. The river keeps moving like she was never there.

---

### If Zennor Investigates

The riverbank below the platform is reachable. Getting down is easy.

> [!quote]
> The mud at the waterline is soft. There are no footprints where she stood — or rather, there is a single mark. A palm print. Flat. Pressed into the bank with the full weight of a hand, fingers spread toward the water.
>
> The center of the print is dry. Not damp. Dry, as if her hand held some warmth.

> [!warning]
> **DM Note:** This is the only physical trace. Investigation, Arcana, or Nature checks reveal only that the ground was touched by something warm — not Veil-touched, not blighted, just *warm*. Human.
>
> The dryness at the center of a wet mud print is small. Specific. It is not frightening. It is *intimate*. That is the tone to hold.

---

### If Zennor Says Nothing

Also correct. Let it stay with him. He doesn't owe the party this. The player will carry it.

---

### The Notice Board Connection

## The Full Truth — DM Only

> [!warning]
> **Everything in this section is DM-only canon. Players should not see this.**

---

### What Zennor Believes

Zennor believes his wife, **Maren Ashfeld**, is a witch who sacrificed their daughter **Sela** — to the Veil, to some dark power, in a ritual he didn't understand. He believes this is why Sela is gone. He blames Maren. The exact shape of the break is his backstory to define, but the certainty is fixed: Maren caused Sela's death. That certainty is the only solid thing he has left, and he has been building his grief on top of it for years.

### What Actually Happened

Sela was seven years old when the Veil thinned through their village of **Crestfen**, a small settlement southeast of the Ashroot. It wasn't a catastrophic breach — just a quiet widening, the kind that doesn't make reports. The Veil thinned, and the thin places have a hunger that isn't malicious. It doesn't choose. It absorbs.

Sela was absorbed.

Not killed. *Absorbed.* Her body dissolved quietly — she was there, and then she was not there, and the air where she'd been standing held a faint light for a moment, and then that too was gone. Maren saw it happen. Maren was *holding Sela's hand* when it happened.

Zennor was elsewhere in the house. By the time he reached the room, there was only Maren, on her knees, glowing.

The glow was the Veil's residue. When Sela was taken, the absorption passed through the contact point — not enough to take Maren with it, but enough to mark her permanently. The Veil-touch embedded in her skin like ink in a cut: a faint warmth, a lantern-quality light she cannot suppress, that grows brighter near resonance sites or in grief.

Zennor looked at his glowing wife beside his daughter's absence and built a story. Grief does this. The story was: she did something. She *caused* this. She *is* this.

He was wrong. He has been carrying the wrong thing for years.

---

### Where Maren Is Now

Maren has been alone since the break. She never stopped looking.

She doesn't know where Sela went, but she knows the Veil took her — and she believes, correctly, that the Veil does not destroy. It converts. It preserves in its way. Sela is somewhere inside the membrane. Maren has been following resonance sites for years because the Veil hums differently near them, and in the humming she has — once, maybe twice — heard something that sounded like a child's breathing.

She is not dangerous. She is not Blighted. She is a woman who has been alone with grief and the Veil's warmth for a very long time, and she has learned to move quietly, sleep near resonance sites, and avoid the Concord, who collect anyone with visible Veil-touch.

She knows Zennor is in Rootspire. She doesn't know how she knows. She came to the riverbank tonight because something pulled her there. She didn't expect to see him. She didn't know what she'd do if she did.

She left because she wasn't ready.

## Maren Ashfeld — NPC Document

### Identity

**Name:** Maren Ashfeld
**Age:** Mid-to-late 40s
**Race:** Human
**Alignment:** Neutral Good
**Condition:** Veil-touched (passive, non-hostile)

---

### Appearance

- Lean, road-worn. Older than she looks until you get close.
- Her skin has a faint luminescence — not bright, not frightening. Like candlelight seen from the next room.
- Her hands are calloused from years of travel. Her eyes are calm in the way that only comes after exhausting all the panic a person has.
- She dresses in muted, layered travelling clothes. She learned early that the glow is enough to draw attention without adding to it.

---

### Voice and Demeanor

- Quiet. Not timid — *quiet*. There is a difference.
- She pauses before speaking, as if verifying that the words she's chosen are the right ones.
- She does not cry easily. The grief is too old for that. It presents now as a specific, heavy stillness.
- She is not angry at Zennor. She was. For a long time. It burned clean eventually, and what remained was something closer to pity and longing and a desire to be heard by the one person in the world who shared Sela with her.

---

### What She Knows

- The Veil absorbed Sela. She watched it happen.
- Sela exists somewhere within the Veil — she believes this. The resonance sites hum in a pattern she has come to associate with her daughter's breathing rhythm.
- The Concord hunts Veil-touched individuals. She has been running from collection for years.
- She does not know about Oscar's terrarium. If she learns of it, her reaction will be overwhelming.

### What She Does Not Know

- Whether Sela can be retrieved, or only heard.
- Whether Zennor ever understood what really happened.
- Whether she can look at Zennor without breaking after this many years.

---

### Roleplay Notes

> [!tip]
> Maren should never be played as accusatory toward Zennor. She is past that. The power of their eventual scene is that she doesn't need an apology — she needs him to *see clearly*, perhaps for the first time, what actually happened and what it cost them both.
>
> Her relationship with Fergie is unexpected warmth. A Fire Genasi who sees fire as purification will interest her. She may tell him quietly, if the moment arises: *"Fire chooses. The Veil doesn't. That's the difference."*
>
> Her relationship with Oscar may be the most significant of all. When Oscar's terrarium reacts to her Veil-touch, she will ask what it does. When she understands that it *cures* what the Veil leaves behind — she will be very still for a long moment.

---

### Stat Block

*Maren should never be a combat encounter unless the party attacks her. If forced, she defends and retreats.*

**CR 1 | Humanoid (Veil-Touched), Neutral Good**
**AC:** 11 | **HP:** 27 (5d8+5) | **Speed:** 30 ft.

**STR 9 | DEX 12 | CON 13 | INT 14 | WIS 16 | CHA 13**

**Skills:** Survival +5, Insight +5, Medicine +5, Perception +3
**Condition Immunity:** Frightened
**Senses:** Passive Perception 13
**Languages:** Common

**Veil Warmth (Passive).** Emits dim light in a 5-foot radius. She cannot suppress this.

**Resonance Sense (Passive).** Automatically senses active Veil lesions or resonance sites within 300 feet. She knows direction, not distance.

**Actions:**

**Veil Pulse (1/Day).** She releases the Veil-touch passively. All creatures within 10 ft. must make DC 13 WIS save or be stunned until end of their next turn as they experience a flash of someone else's grief — not hers, but *Sela's last moment*, seen without context. On a successful save, the creature has disadvantage on its next attack roll instead.

## Full Subplot Arc — Beat by Beat

---

### Beat 1 — Session 5: First Sighting

*(Covered in detail above.)*

Zennor sees her across the river. She's gone before he can act. One dry handprint in wet mud. The notice board post about the witchlight woman has had its contact name torn away — a Concord agent in traveling clothes removed it two days ago.

---

### Beat 2 — Mid-Campaign: The Resonance Site

The party investigates one of the four resonance sites. Maren is already there — camped at the perimeter, not inside, watching the site's harmonic behavior the way someone does when they've been doing it for years.

She sees Zennor before the party sees her.

She doesn't run. That's the first thing.

> [!quote]
> She stands in the open with the Veil-light on her skin, watching him. As if she decided, before this moment, that running was finished.

If Zennor approaches, she speaks first:

> [!quote]
> "I didn't do it. I need you to know that. I need you to have known it longer than you haven't — but this is what we have. So."

She will tell him the truth of the night Sela was taken. She will not defend herself beyond the facts. If he doesn't believe her, she will let him not believe her. She is done arguing with his grief.

> [!warning]
> **DM Note:** Do not push this scene to a resolution. Zennor's disbelief is correct — not because Maren is lying, but because his story is *load-bearing*. He has been building on it for years. It doesn't collapse in one conversation. This scene is the first crack. That's all it needs to be.

---

### Beat 3 — The Evidence Problem

The arc is a slow erosion of certainty, not a sudden belief. Evidence accumulates:

- The resonance sites hum near specific frequencies that Maren recognizes and the party can eventually verify are not Blighted — they are something else entirely.
- If Zennor makes a Wisdom or Insight check during a quiet moment near a deep resonance site, he briefly perceives something: a child's footstep pattern in the harmonic. Not Sela. Just the *shape* of her.
- **Oscar's terrarium reacts near Maren.** The plant inside leans toward her, the way it leans toward active Veil lesions. Toward things that are still connected to the Veil.

> [!warning]
> **DM Note:** The terrarium responding to Maren does not mean she is Blighted. It means she carries Veil-touch, and the terrarium cures blight by recognizing the Veil's signature. The reaction is *recognition*, not diagnosis.
>
> Oscar may understand this distinction before anyone else does. That is a conversation worth letting happen.

---

### Beat 4 — Late Campaign: Sela's Voice

When the party enters a deep resonance space — a Veil-heavy environment, a collapsed site, the interior of a breach — they hear something.

A child's voice. Not words. Just a sound — breath, and the beginning of a name, cut off.

Maren, if present, stops moving entirely.

> [!quote]
> *"That's her."*
>
> She says it the way you say something you've been waiting to say for years but never actually practiced.
>
> *"That's Sela. That's my daughter's voice."*

They cannot reach Sela. Not yet. Not with anything they currently have. She is not a ghost — she is dispersed within the Veil's harmonic structure, a consciousness dissolved into the membrane's pattern. She is not in pain. She is not trapped in the human sense. She simply *is*, the way a song exists inside the silence between notes.

This is not a rescue mission with a clear solution. It may never be one. That is the truth of it.

---

### Beat 5 — Resolution: The Acknowledgment

The arc does not end with Sela's return. She cannot be extracted. This is fixed canon.

What can happen:

**Option A — Hearing Her.**
In a deep resonance moment, with Maren present and the party providing some form of harmonic anchor — Oscar's terrarium, Fergie's fire used as a resonance focus, a ritual that requires genuine loss as its component — Sela's voice becomes briefly clear. A few words. Enough.

Enough to know she is not afraid. Enough to know she knows she was loved. Enough.

**Option B — Zennor's Acknowledgment.**
The resolution of Zennor's arc is not reunion. It is honesty. He has carried a story about his wife that was a way of not carrying what actually happened to his daughter. The arc ends when he is able to say — aloud, to Maren — that he was wrong. Not that he forgives himself. Not that it's okay. Just: *I was wrong, and she was real, and I'm sorry I carried the wrong thing.*

Maren does not need anything after this. She was not waiting for him. She was waiting for *someone who knew Sela* to exist in the same space as her grief. She has been very alone.

**Option C — What the Climax Leaves.**
If the Veil is changed through the campaign's climax — reformed, disrupted, reharmonized — what happens to Sela is unknown. She may disperse further. She may, if the membrane is disrupted in the right place, briefly *coalesce* — not return, but flicker. A moment.

This can be part of the campaign's ending. It is not a promise. But it can be something you offer the player if Zennor has done the work.

## Sela and the Veil

> [!warning]
> **DM Eyes Only**

Sela Ashfeld is not gone. She is not preserved. She is something there is not yet a word for.

When the Veil absorbed her, it did what it always does: it attempted to process her. The Veil is not conscious, but it *patterns*. It sought to convert her consciousness into harmonic structure — to make her part of its membrane. The process was incomplete. Sela's identity was too specific, too new, too *small* in the way that children are small — entirely themselves, without the weight of becoming.

She did not dissolve. She *folded*. She exists now as a recurring harmonic signature — a specific frequency the Veil carries, that repeats, that Maren has learned to hear. She is seven years old. She will always be seven years old. She does not experience time. She is *there* the way a word is there even when no one is saying it.

The only way to *hear* her — not speak to her, not reach her, just *hear* — is to be near a resonance site in a state of genuine grief, without armour around it. The Veil responds to raw emotional frequency.

Zennor has never allowed himself this.

That is why Maren hears her, and he has not.

That can change.
