## Must-Hit Beats

> [!important] Must Hit
> **① News arrives:** A Concord Harmonic Assessment unit is being dispatched to the Ashroot. Three days out.

> [!important] Must Hit
> **② The clock is established:** Something is coming whether the party is ready or not.

> [!important] Must Hit
> **③ The lower roots lead is surfaced:** A construct like Oscar was sighted below. This is the party's next move.

---

## Optional Beats

> [!tip] Optional — use if the scene has room
> - A sharp **Insight or History check (DC 14)** on Pell's travel documents reveals a Guile sigil scratched beneath Vision colours. The Concord is not sending scholars.
> - A nearby Rootspire local reacts visibly to the word "Concord." Use this to show what Concord presence means to the Free Peoples.
> - A separate note arrives via a Council of 8 proxy — three words only: *"We already know."* No signature. Plant this if you want the Council to feel omniscient this session.

---

## DM Prep Notes

> [!warning] DM Only
> - The messenger **is not an enemy and not a friend.** He is a courier doing a job. The threat is the news, not the man.
> - The **"three days out" timeline is false.** The Harmonic Assessment team is already in the Ashroot. This pays off in Scene 06. Do not telegraph it.
> - The **Guile sigil** on Pell's documents is real. The Assessment team is a Guile operation in Vision clothing. Only surface this if a player specifically investigates the documents.
> - **Source of the message:** deliberately ambiguous. A sympathetic Vision scholar, a Free Peoples spy, or a Guile defector — the DM decides, but Pell does not know and will not say.
> - Pell **does not know he is carrying false information.** He was told three days and passed that on. He is not complicit.

## NPC: Pell, Courier

| | |
|---|---|
| **Full name** | Pell (no family name — not unusual for courier-class) |
| **Role** | Independent message runner. Works for coin, not ideology. |
| **Appearance** | Late 30s. Lean, road-worn. Brown coat with a dozen pockets, all full. Boots caked with Ashroot mud. A sealed satchel he keeps one hand on at all times. Grey at his temples. Moves like someone always calculating the nearest exit. |
| **Demeanour** | Professionally detached. Polite but not warm. Slightly uncomfortable being this deep into Rootspire. Not afraid — alert. |
| **Voice / tone** | Flat and efficient. Short sentences. Does not editorialize. Will repeat the message verbatim if asked but adds nothing. |
| **What he knows** | He was given a sealed letter and a verbal summary at Ashroot's eastern edge. Paid in Concord silver — he noticed. Told to find the party by name. That is all. |
| **What he wants** | To deliver the message, collect his fee, and leave before dark. |
| **Verbal tic** | Touches the satchel strap when lying or omitting something. Uses it **twice:** once when he says he doesn't know the source, once when he confirms the three-day timeline. |

---

## Running the Scene

Let the party have a line or two of post-Session-4 conversation. Then:

> [!quote] Read Aloud — Pell Arrives
> The square is busy. Rootspire mid-afternoon: traders calling from rope-suspended stalls, a group of children racing along a walkway above you, the smell of something roasting nearby. The Great Tree's bark rises behind it all, wide as a city block, damp and dark and alive.
>
> You notice him before he notices you. A man in a travel-stained coat moving against the flow of the crowd, scanning faces. He has a hand on a satchel strap and the look of someone who wants to complete a transaction and be elsewhere. When his eyes land on your group, he stops. Recalibrates. Walks toward you with purpose.
>
> *"You're the ones from the Council meeting."* Not a question. He reaches into the satchel and produces a sealed envelope — plain wax, no crest. *"Message for you. Came through the eastern settlements two nights ago. I was asked to find you specifically."*

*Pause here. Let the party respond — take the letter, question Pell, be cautious. The letter's contents are the same regardless.*

> [!quote] Read Aloud — The Letter
> The handwriting is small and precise. No salutation.
>
> *"The Concord is sending a Harmonic Assessment unit to the Ashroot. Official stated purpose: investigation of atmospheric irregularities near Rootspire. Unofficial composition: three Vision scholars, one Might escort — four bodies total. They departed Orrenval six days ago. Estimate three days until Ashroot border crossing.*
>
> *Do not approach. Do not engage. Observe and report to your principals.*
>
> *The scholars are not the concern. The escort is not listed on any official manifest."*
>
> No signature. The wax seal has been cleanly cut — it was already opened before it reached Pell.

## Pell — Sample Dialogue

These are reference lines. Adapt freely. Keep his cadence short and flat — he does not volunteer.

**On being greeted:**
> *"You're the ones from the Council meeting. Good. I've been in this city two hours. I'd like to not be here for a third."*

**On the message source:**
> *"Coin changes hands. Message gets delivered. I don't ask and I don't remember."*
> *[If pushed:] "A woman. Eastern settlements, Fenwick Crossing. Middle-aged, Vision robes. That's the edge of what I have."*

**On the unlisted escort:**
> *"I only carry the letter."*
> *[Long pause.]*
> *"I don't read them."*

**On leaving:**
> *"My fee was half up front. I'll collect the rest and be on the road before dark. You need anything else — find someone else. I'm done with this job."*

## If / Then — Player Responses

| If the party... | Then... |
|---|---|
| Ask Pell who sent it | He doesn't know and won't say he does. If pushed hard: a woman, Fenwick Crossing, Vision robes. That's all. He touches the satchel strap when he says he doesn't know the source. |
| Ask about the unlisted escort | Pell goes very still. *"I only carry the letter."* DC 16 Insight: he's read it, or part of it. He will not say more. If the party presses further, he asks for his fee and leaves. |
| Detain or threaten Pell | He doesn't fight. Hands up, goes quiet. He has nothing more to give — he is genuinely just a courier. Holding him accomplishes nothing and risks word getting back. |
| Ask about the three-day timeline | He repeats it flatly. He believes it. He touches the satchel strap here — same tell as before. He was given false information and passed it on. |
| Try to trace the Concord silver | Pell describes the coin — standard Concord mint, recent year. A DC 14 History check: paying an Ashroot courier in Concord coin doesn't hide the origin. It signals it deliberately. |
| React publicly / loudly | Nearby locals catch fragments. A vendor closes their stall and moves away quickly. A guard on a walkway above looks down, then deliberately looks away. The word "Concord" is not welcome here. |

## Atmosphere — Layer These In

These are ambient details. None are plot-critical. Use them to keep the world breathing.

- The Great Tree's bark near the square's edge has a hairline fissure about the width of a hand. Nobody seems alarmed. It's been there for weeks, people say. Getting wider.
- A street vendor has carved figures that look vaguely like a warforged construct — crude, clearly a guess at the shape. She has three of them. Made them after someone described seeing one below.
- While the party reads the letter, a child stops and stares at Oscar. Not afraid — fascinated. She asks: *"does it hurt, being made of metal?"* Then runs away before anyone can answer.

## Transitioning Out

| If the party... | Then... |
|---|---|
| Head toward the lower roots *(intended)* | Transition directly to **Scene 02: Descent.** The letter has given them urgency — something is coming, and they want to find the construct first. |
| Try to gather more intel on the Concord unit | Allow one or two beats in Rootspire — a contact, a rumour. Keep it to 10 minutes. Then: someone in the lower market says the construct was seen again this morning. Redirects them downward. |
| Go straight to the Council of 8 | The Council already knows. One of the Eight: *"We are aware. This changes your timeline, not your task. Begin the descent."* Cold, efficient. Back to Scene 02. |
| Split the party | Let one thread run briefly, then have Pell or another NPC pull them back. The lower roots require all of them — Vessel-Null responds specifically to Oscar's presence. |

> [!warning] If This Scene Runs Long
> Over 25 minutes — cut all optional beats and atmosphere. The three must-hit beats are non-negotiable: news arrives, party reads the letter, lower roots is the move. That's the scene.
>
> Pell leaves Rootspire the same day. He is not available for follow-up. If the party tries to find him in a future session — he's already gone, and yes, someone was watching which direction he rode out.

- **Oscar's arc** — The vendor's carved figures and the child's question about being "made of metal" seed the identity theme before it lands fully in Scene 04.
