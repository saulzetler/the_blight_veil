## DM Prep Notes

> [!warning] DM Only
> - The lower roots are not a dungeon. There are no random encounters on the descent. The danger here is atmospheric and psychological — save the physical threat for Scene 03.
> - The terrarium's orientation is the first mechanical signal that Oscar is something specific, not just something unknown. Do not explain it. Let it sit. Oscar's player should feel the weight of the question without being handed the answer.
> - The offerings are Maren's. She is not a villain, not a plant, not a trap. She is an old woman who found the lower roots thirty years ago and has been tending them quietly ever since. She understands what the lower roots are — or rather, she understands them the way a person of faith understands a cathedral: in feeling rather than fact.
> - The pre-Concord carvings depict figures kneeling around a radiant centre — not a sun, not a god, something that defies clean categorisation. The figures' faces are turned away from the centre, as if they cannot look directly at it. This is a visual echo of the Veil Core and the Choir of Binding, though the party has no way to know that yet.
> - **The Hollow Nave is at the bottom.** The descent should feel like going somewhere inevitable — as if the party was always going to end up here.

## The Physical Space — Description Notes

The lower roots of Rootspire are accessed through a grated iron door set into the base of the Great Tree, maintained by a Rootspire groundskeeper who asks no questions as long as you have a Council chit or a convincing story. Below the door, a stone stairway carved into the root-mass itself spirals downward for about forty feet before opening into the root network proper.

The space is not a cave. It is not underground in the way a mine is underground. It is more like the inside of a living thing — root systems the width of a man's torso arc overhead and through the walls, the stone foundations of old pre-Rootspire structures cracked apart by centuries of growth. The air is cold and damp and carries a faint metallic sweetness.

There is no natural light below the stairway. Torches work normally. The darkness between them feels attentive.

**The route to the Hollow Nave** is approximately one hour of careful movement — narrow passages between root walls, a low-ceilinged crawl through a collapsed foundation chamber, a broader gallery where the roots thin and the old stone is most visible. Mark time loosely: the journey is a vehicle for atmosphere and discovery, not a navigation challenge.

## The Offerings

Tucked into hollows in the root-walls every twenty feet or so: small arrangements of objects left with obvious care. They are not random.

Each hollow contains some combination of:
- Candle stubs burned to the base, the wax pooled and hardened
- Small carved figures — humanoid, featureless, made from root-wood
- Strips of cloth knotted around the roots in specific patterns — not decorative, deliberate
- In some hollows: small bowls of soil, dried flowers, or river stones arranged in a ring

A DC 10 Perception check reveals they are recent — the wax is soft, some flowers still hold colour. Someone was down here within the last two or three days.

A DC 13 Investigation check reveals the cloth knotting follows a repeating pattern — the same sequence of knots in every hollow. Not a language exactly, but structured. Ritualistic.

> [!warning] DM Only
> The knot pattern is a form of resonance notation that Maren developed herself over thirty years of observation. She doesn't know what it means in any academic sense — she just knows it feels right to make this shape in this place. It is, without her understanding it, a rough approximation of the harmonic frequency the lower roots emit. If a Vision Scholar ever saw it, they would be stunned.

## The Pre-Concord Carvings

*Optional beat — found in the low-ceilinged crawl chamber, about halfway down.*

The carvings cover a ten-foot section of exposed stone that the roots haven't yet claimed. They are old — the edges worn smooth, the stone darkened with age.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — The Carvings
> The stone here is different — older than the foundations above it, darker, carved with the kind of deliberate patience that takes years. Figures, maybe thirty of them, all kneeling. They face inward, toward a centre point that the carver left blank — not unfinished, blank intentionally, a smooth circle with nothing in it.
>
> Every figure's face is turned away from the centre. They are kneeling toward something they cannot look at directly.
>
> At the bottom, almost worn invisible: a single line of text in a script none of you immediately recognise. Old Concord script, before the standardisation. It reads: *"We held it here before the city knew to fear it."*

| Check | DC | Result |
|---|---|---|
| History (the script) | 14 | This predates Rootspire by at least 400 years. Someone was living in the roots, and worshipping something within them, long before the city above existed. |
| Religion (the iconography) | 13 | The blank centre is a pre-Concord convention for depicting the divine — not because the gods were featureless, but because depicting their face directly was considered dangerous. The divine was shown as an absence surrounded by devotion. |
| Arcana (the blank centre) | 15 | There's a faint residual energy in the carved circle — almost imperceptible. Whatever was venerated here, something of it still is here. The blank is not empty. |

## Veil Atmospheric Cues — One Per PC

*Use one or two of these during the descent — don't use all of them at once. Space them out. Each one is personalised to a PC's inner life.*

> [!tip] Zennor
> A hollow in the root-wall at eye level. Inside it: a child's carved figure, small enough to fit in a palm. Similar to the others — featureless, root-wood — except this one has been dressed. A tiny scrap of cloth, carefully tied. Zennor alone notices it. It doesn't do anything. It just sits there.

> [!tip] Oscar
> Passing a section of root-wall that has cracked and healed over itself dozens of times — layers of old wood and new growth visible in cross-section, each ring a scar from something that broke and was rebuilt. Oscar's reflection in a patch of still water on the floor shows them standing completely still while the world moves around them. When Oscar moves again, the reflection moves normally.

> [!tip] Fergie
> There is no fire down here, but the darkness between light sources has colour — a deep ember-orange, barely visible, like the last light inside a coal that looks black from the outside. It is not heat. It is something that remembers heat. It moves when Fergie isn't looking directly at it.

> [!tip] Hog
> The roots in one section have grown in a pattern that looks — unmistakably, absurdly — like a fighting stance. Wide base, torso twisted, one arm extended. It's just root growth. It's definitely just root growth. But the longer Hog looks at it, the more it looks like someone who knew what they were doing.

## NPC: Maren

*Optional beat — encountered mid-descent if you want a warm human moment before the darkness of Scene 03.*

| | |
|---|---|
| **Full name** | Maren Ashwick |
| **Age** | Late 60s |
| **Appearance** | Short, broad-shouldered, built like someone who has carried heavy things her whole life. Grey hair pulled back practically. Wears a canvas apron with deep pockets — always has her hands in them. Carries a small lantern, the flame unnaturally steady even when she moves. |
| **Demeanour** | Unflappable. Not fearless — she has a healthy respect for what lives down here — but she is not surprised by much. She has seen stranger things than a warforged construct in her thirty years of visits. |
| **Voice / tone** | Warm, slightly amused, speaks slowly like someone used to choosing words carefully. |
| **What she's doing here** | Leaving offerings. She does this every few days. She calls it "tending." |
| **What she knows** | She knows the lower roots better than anyone alive. She knows the Hollow Nave — calls it the Old Shrine. She knows something changed recently: the air tastes different, the candles gutter toward the centre instead of toward the draft. She doesn't know why. |
| **What she'll share freely** | The history of her visits. The name of the Hollow Nave. The fact that something has changed recently. Her opinion that whatever is down here is not evil — "just very, very old and very, very hungry to be understood." |
| **What she won't share** | She will not lead them. She will not go as far as the Hollow Nave with them. "That place isn't for me today," she says, and does not explain further. |

**Sample dialogue:**

**On being found mid-descent:**
> *"Oh. Well. I didn't expect company."*
> *[A beat, reassessing.]*
> *"You're from above. Council business?"*

**On the offerings:**
> *"Thirty years I've been coming down here. Don't ask me why I started — I couldn't tell you anymore. It just felt like something that needed doing. Like keeping a light on in a room where someone might come home."*

**On the Hollow Nave:**
> *"The Old Shrine, we used to call it, when there was a 'we.' Most people stopped coming years ago. Too far. Too dark. Too strange."*
> *[Quieter:]*
> *"Something's different lately. The air down near the Shrine — it hums. Didn't used to hum."*

**On Oscar:**
> *[She looks at Oscar for a long moment. Not unkind. Thoughtful.]*
> *"You know, I've left figures down here for thirty years. Never thought I'd meet one that could walk."*

## Transitioning Out

The scene ends when the party reaches the Hollow Nave — a larger chamber where the roots thin and old stone forms something like walls, arching overhead into a rough vault. There is a stillness here that is different from the quiet of the descent. The descent was silence. This is held breath.

> [!quote] Read Aloud — Arriving at the Hollow Nave
> The passage widens and then opens — properly opens, into a space large enough to stand in fully, to spread your arms. The roots have pulled back here, or been held back, curving around the walls like pillars that decided not to fall inward.
>
> The stone beneath your feet is worn smooth in a rough circle at the centre, the way stone gets worn by feet returning to the same place over centuries.
>
> And at the far end — still. Watching. A shape that is not root and not stone. That has a chassis. That has eyes, and those eyes are lit.

*Cut to Scene 03.*

> [!warning] If This Scene Runs Long
> Over 30 minutes — cut Maren, cut the carvings, cut the personalised Veil cues. Run only the three must-hit beats: the atmosphere lands, the terrarium orients, the offerings are found. The Hollow Nave read-aloud closes the scene. Keep moving.

## Threads This Scene Advances

- **Oscar's arc** — The terrarium's orientation is the first hard signal that Oscar is something specific. Vessel-Null will confirm it in Scene 03.
- **The Veil's nature** — The shadow pulse, the ember-dark, the knot patterns are all early manifestations of the Ashroot Resonance. The players don't know that yet. They will.
- **The pre-Concord world** — The carvings suggest something was being held or worshipped in the lower roots long before the Concord existed. The line *"We held it here before the city knew to fear it"* is a direct ancestor of what the Concord does with the Veil Core — containment as devotion.
- **Maren** — A potential recurring NPC. She knows more than she understands. If the party builds a relationship with her, she becomes a valuable source of lower-roots lore in future sessions.
