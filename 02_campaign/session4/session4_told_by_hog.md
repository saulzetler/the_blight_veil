# THE BLIGHT VEIL

## Session 4 — As Told by Hog Thunderwood

_Dwarf. Monk. Pants Optional._

---

RIGHT so gather round because I'm only tellin this once and also I might tell it again later but THIS is the official version.

So there we were. Big tree city. Council of the eight scary floaty people. They put these glowing symbols in the air and said "know us" which, fair enough, very dramatic, love the commitment. The others — clever lot when they want to be — figured out the puzzle using stuff we remembered from the trial. I helped by being present and having good energy.

Then this portal opens up. Big crack in the floor, pale light pouring out, shapes moving around in there that I couldn't quite make out and honestly didn't love the look of. Everyone sort of stood there for a moment going hmmmm.

Then we went in. One by one. Into the big scary light hole.

Inside was — alright look I'm not going to pretend I fully understood what was happening in there. The room didn't work right. Big floaty presences everywhere, not sitting, not standing, just _there_ in the way that makes your knees want to make a decision about the floor. The Council told us to go find the spots where the Veil is going wrong, have a look, don't try to fix anything, come back with information. Very reasonable request from a group of entities that exist outside the normal rules of space.

THEN — and this is the good bit — someone asked what we're actually supposed to _do_ when we get to these anomaly places. Perfectly sensible question. And one of the Council — the Whispering Mask, sinister little thing — just absolutely _erupts_ out of nowhere:

**"PURIFY THEM."**

Just like that. Boom. Into the room. Very ominous.

And the Veil Watcher turns to the Mask — you could FEEL it even though none of them have faces you can read — and says, cool as anything: _"Silence. You are on this council. But you are not granted the power to speak."_

The Mask shut right up. The room went quiet. I made a mental note about the Whispering Mask. Filed it under: watch that one.

---

Before they booted us out, the Council squished some of their floating symbols down into a little disc and gave it to us. Metal thing. Incomplete markings all over it. Little seam down the middle that wobbles like it's listening for something.

Fergie took it. Attuned to it during a rest — sat with it, breathed with it, did the whole thing — and while that was happening the disc told Fergie its name.

Jaihroquai.

Now. I want to be very clear about my relationship with Jaihroquai.

I think Jaihroquai is fantastic. I think Jaihroquai is doing great work. I have nothing but respect and admiration for Jaihroquai and I would very much like Jaihroquai to acknowledge that at some point.

Jaihroquai has not done this.

Jaihroquai, as far as I can tell, does not register my existence in any meaningful way. I am exploring whether this is something that can change. I remain hopeful. The disc has not given me reason to be hopeful. I remain hopeful anyway.

---

Back in Rootspire, which just carries on doing Rootspire things regardless of what cosmic horror you've just been standing in front of, we found a tavern. The Split Bark. Excellent establishment. Ceiling like the inside of a tree, which makes sense, because it IS inside a tree. Lanternlight. The smell of ale and wood resin. A chalk circle on the floor.

Now a chalk circle on a tavern floor means one thing and one thing only and that is that someone is going to get hit in it. I was ready. I was VERY ready. I had been ready since before we walked in.

Zennor and I stepped in.

Turns out it was a board game. A strategy game. Ashroot Arena. You pick little champions and move them around and roll dice and apparently that is the version of fighting that happens here.

Fine. I adapted. I picked a Champion and a Healer — solid combination, very hard to kill, very good at stalling — and Zennor went Fighter, Juggernaut, Pawn. Bold. Aggressive. Wrong.

I won. It was not exciting. The Healer kept healing, the Champion kept not dying, Zennor's team kept failing to do anything about it. The crowd watched. The crowd had feelings about what they were watching. Those feelings were complicated.

I kept glancing at the chalk circle on the floor.

It was right there.

We each had a Moss-Foam Lager, the second cheapest ale. We slept there. Jaihroquai did not acknowledge that I had won a strategic contest of significant duration.

---

Morning. Leaving the tavern. We ran into this fellow.

Thin. Mossy. Tilting his head at angles I didn't know heads could do. Sitting on the tavern steps like he'd been there the whole time.

He said his name was Timoor. Someone else heard Tumor. Look, names are hard, mornings are hard, we'd just woken up. The point is we needed to establish whether this individual was dangerous.

So we asked him questions.

We asked him his favourite colour. We asked him some other things that I'm told were not obviously useful for determining threat levels. Someone suggested punching me in the face as a way of gathering data. He did not do this. I respect that actually. Shows restraint.

We did not conclusively determine whether Tumor — Timoor — whether _he_ was dangerous.

What we DID determine was that he was good company, because he and I skipped off together shortly after and it was a genuinely pleasant few minutes. We asked him to come with us. He said yes in the way that felt like he already knew he was going to.

---

Jaihroquai — which I would like to point out I have been pronouncing correctly this entire time, I have been PRACTICING — was pointing west. Toward a place called the Spiral Loom.

We headed that way. Found a notice board first. Lots of things pinned up. The one that grabbed us was about a construct — like Oscar, sort of — seen wandering the lower roots. Emitting geometric light. Non-hostile, the notice said, but nobody knew what it was or why it was there.

Lower roots. That's where we're going. The Spiral Loom can wait.

---

Then —  as were arriving back to the center of town — a runner shows up. Woman. Road-dusted.

She had a message cylinder. Concord seal, already cracked open. The parchment smelled of ash.

Three districts in Orrenval. Protests. Unrest. All of it kicking off after what happened to Captain Deyran — you know, the Captain Deyran we killed, that one. Purity enforcement going up. Insurrections being put down.

And then a second line, different handwriting:

_"The fire sings louder than they can silence."_

The others asked a lot of questions. I thought about the chalk circle and the ale and the way normal things keep happening while the world rearranges itself around you.

---

_So that's where we are._

_Big scary disc that ignores me. Mysterious construct in the roots. Council with a secret it won't let one of its own say out loud. A city on fire over a dead captain._

_And Timoor._

_Can't forget Timoor._

_Anyway — pants are where I left them, spirit is willing, and Jaihroquai is going to acknowledge me eventually._

_I feel good about this._
