Below are **high-impact questions** designed to expose fracture lines in your party and reinforce each Council member’s philosophy.

Use them sparingly. Let silence follow each one.

---

# 🌿 Rootkeeper — Continuity, Cost, and Consequence

These questions stretch beyond the moment. They are about aftermath.

### 1.

> “If the Blight vanished tomorrow… what would you become without an enemy?”

This tests:

- Who is defined by opposition?
    
- Who has an identity beyond crisis?
    

Zennor and Fergie especially.

---

### 2.

> “What will outlive you — your mercy, or your violence?”

Let that sit.

If someone answers “both,” Rootkeeper leans in slightly.

---

### 3.

> “If saving this forest required the Concord to endure… would you allow it?”

This is a trap question.

It tests:

- Ideology vs preservation.
    
- Whether they are rebels or protectors.
    

---

# 🔥 Ember Speaker — Sacrifice, Will, and Conviction

These questions should feel like heat against skin.

### 1.

> “Name one thing you would burn without hesitation.”

Push for specifics. Not concepts.

A building? A belief? A person?

---

### 2.

> “Who among you would take power — even if it changed you?”

This is aimed directly at:

- Fergie (mastery of fire)
    
- Oscar (origin and purpose)
    
- Zennor (weapon and certainty)
    

Let them look at each other.

---

### 3.

> “When the Blight spreads to someone you love — do you cure them… or end them?”

This is brutal.

It foreshadows Oscar’s terrarium limits.  
It hits Zennor hardest.

Do not soften it.

---

# 👁 Veil Watcher — Motive, Self-Deception, Hidden Truth

These should feel surgical.

### 1.

(to Zennor)

> “If your wife is innocent… who are you?”

Let the air change.

---

### 2.

(to Oscar)

> “If you learn your purpose was to spread the Blight… would you continue existing?”

No emotion. Just curiosity.

---

### 3.

(to Fergie)

> “If fire answers you… what do you owe it?”

Not “what will you do with it.”

What do you owe it?

---

### 4.

(to Hog)

> “When you are not performing… who remains?”

Simple. Devastating.

---

# Optional Group Question (Delivered After Individual Ones)

> “Do you stand as four… or as four directions?”

This tests unity.

Let them answer as a group or separately.

Their choice matters.

---

# How to Use These

Do not ask all of them.

Pick:

- One Rootkeeper question
    
- One Ember Speaker question
    
- One Veil Watcher question
    

Then let the conversation evolve naturally.

The power is not in quantity.  
It is in stillness after the question.
