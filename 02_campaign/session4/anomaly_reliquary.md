
# The Anomaly Reliquary

_Wondrous Item (Rare)_  
Requires Attunement

---

## Read-Aloud: The Gift

> As the harmonics begin to fracture, one of the presences steadies.
> 
> A cluster of sigils collapses inward — not extinguishing, but condensing.
> 
> They fold into a small object, suspended between you.
> 
> It is not ornate. Not ceremonial.
> 
> It looks… unfinished.
> 
> “You will require orientation,” a voice says.
> 
> “This will suffice.”

The object drops — but does not fall. It simply becomes heavy.

---

## Physical Description

A palm-sized disc of dark, matte metal.  
Across its surface are faint concentric rings etched with incomplete sigils.

At its center is a thin vertical seam — not a crack, not quite a needle.

When held, the seam trembles faintly — like a compass needle listening for something deeper than north.

The metal is cool, but not cold. It hums softly when the Veil is thin.

---

## Function

The Reliquary does **not** show direction like a normal compass.

Instead, it responds to **Veil instability gradients**.

### Passive Effect

- When within 1 mile of a Veil anomaly, the inner seam begins to drift and vibrate faintly.
    
- The closer the party gets, the more:
    
    - The seam aligns toward the source.
        
    - The rings rotate slowly in opposing directions.
        
    - A low harmonic becomes audible.
        

This is perceptible but not overwhelming.

---

### Active Use (1/Long Rest)

As an action, the attuned user may focus on the disc.

- The rings lock into alignment.
    
- The seam sharpens toward the nearest significant Veil fracture within 10 miles.
    
- The user experiences a brief sensory flash:
    
    - Smell of ozone
        
    - Sudden temperature shift
        
    - A single fragmented image (forest bark splitting, stone breathing, shadow flickering, etc.)
        

This flash is symbolic, not literal.

---

## Limitations (Important)

- It only detects **active instability**, not historical damage.
    
- It cannot distinguish between “minor” and “catastrophic” anomalies.
    
- If two anomalies are near each other, it may oscillate or behave erratically.
    
- It does not function within a stabilized Veil chamber (like the council space).
    

---

## Risk Mechanic (Optional)

Each time the Active Use is triggered:

Roll 1d6.

On a 1:

- The user briefly hears overlapping council voices.
    
- They must succeed on a DC 12 Wisdom save or suffer disadvantage on their next concentration check.
    
- This does not deal damage — it is cognitive interference.
    

This reinforces that the device is **not stable**.

---

## Subtle Lore Hooks

- The sigils on the rings do not match any known arcane tradition.
    
- The metal feels neither forged nor grown.
    
- A careful Arcana check (DC 15) reveals:
    
    - It is not locating anomalies.
        
    - It is resonating with them.
        
    - It may itself be partially Veil-infused.
        

---

## Why This Item Works

- It preserves mystery.
    
- It provides direction without railroading.
    
- It reinforces that the council’s knowledge is incomplete.
    
- It subtly implies that the Veil operates on harmonic logic, not geography.
    

---

## Escalation Potential (Future Use)

You can later:

- Have it crack slightly after major exposure.
    
- Have it align to something that is **not** an anomaly.
    
- Have it stop working entirely when the party most expects it.
    
- Have Oscar’s terrarium interfere with its resonance.
    
- Let Fergie’s fire cause it to hum violently near ignition sources.

# How Attunement Works in 5e (Quick, Practical Version)

In **Dungeons & Dragons 5e**:

### Core Rules

- A creature can be attuned to **up to 3 magic items** at a time.
    
- Attuning requires:
    
    - A **short rest** (at least 1 hour).
        
    - Physical contact with the item.
        
    - Focus and intent.
        
- The creature must meet any prerequisites (class, alignment, spellcasting, etc.).
    
- Attunement can be ended voluntarily during a short rest.
    
- If a character exceeds 3 attuned items, the newest fails.
    

That’s it mechanically.

No ritual circle. No spell slot. Just focused connection.

# The Real Question: How Should _You_ Present It?

You have three strong options, depending on tone.

---

## Option 1 — Mechanical Clarity (Low Mystery)

Best if you want clean table understanding.

When they inspect it:

> “You get the sense this object won’t function fully unless someone forms a deeper connection with it.”

If someone studies it:

Arcana DC 12:

> “It requires attunement.”

This keeps things clean and avoids confusion.

---

## Option 2 — Diegetic Discovery (More Blight Veil)

Instead of saying “attune,” show what that means.

If someone holds it for a while:

> The rings slow slightly. The seam steadies.  
> It feels like it’s waiting.

If they sit with it during a short rest:

> As your breathing settles, the faint hum aligns with your heartbeat. The seam sharpens. The disc feels heavier — anchored.

At that point you can say:

> You believe you’ve formed a bond with it.

No need to say the word attunement unless asked.

---

## Option 3 — Council-Inflected Instruction (Most Thematic)

When the council hands it over:

> “It will answer one of you,”  
> a voice says.
> 
> “But only if you allow it to.”

That implies:

- One wielder
    
- A conscious decision
    
- A bond
    

Players will naturally try during their next short rest.

---

# What I Recommend for _The Blight Veil_

Use **Option 2 + 3 combined**.

Do not say “attunement” immediately.

Let them:

- Feel it resist casual handling
    
- Notice it stabilizes when one person holds it longer
    
- Realize during downtime that it requires focused bonding
    

If they directly ask:

> “Is this an attunement item?”

Then answer clearly: yes.

You’re not trying to obscure rules — just let fiction carry the weight first.

When the attunement completes:

Have the attuned character briefly hear:

> “Orientation acknowledged.”

Not threatening. Not comforting.

Just procedural.

# Attunement Side Effect — **Resonant Imprint**

When a character attunes to the **Anomaly Reliquary**, the Veil does not simply align with them.

It listens back.

Upon completing the short rest required for attunement:

> The rings still.
> 
> The seam sharpens.
> 
> For a breath — the hum is not external.  
> It is _you_.

The item forms a minor imprint based on the attuned character’s nature.

This effect is subtle, flavorful, and persistent while attuned.

---

## Universal Effect (Applies to Any Character)

- The attuned character can always faintly sense the **direction of the nearest instability** (even if the device is not actively used).
    
- However, when within 300 feet of a significant anomaly:
    
    - Their emotions sharpen slightly.
        
    - Sleep is lighter.
        
    - Dreams may feel unfinished.
        

No mechanical penalty — just texture.

---

# Personal Resonance Effects

Use the relevant one depending on who attunes.

---

## If Zennor Attunes

**Effect: Echo of Doubt**

When near a Veil fracture, Zennor occasionally hears a familiar voice — not clearly, not accusatory — just _present_.

- Once per long rest, he may reroll a failed Wisdom saving throw.
    
- When he does, describe a brief flicker of memory tied to his wife or daughter — ambiguous, not confirming anything.
    

**Tone:** The Veil touches unresolved guilt.

---

## If Hog Attunes

**Effect: Ground Against the Pull**

Hog feels the Veil as pressure against his skin — like wind trying to push him off balance.

- Once per long rest, when forced to move or knocked prone, he may choose to remain standing.
    
- When he does, faint sigil-lines briefly trace across the floor beneath his feet.
    

**Tone:** His physical presence resists cosmic instability.

---

## If Fergie Attunes

**Effect: Ember in the Static**

The Reliquary hum subtly syncs with Fergie’s internal heat.

- When within an anomaly, small flames flicker slightly toward the source.
    
- Once per long rest, Fergie may add +1d4 fire damage to a spell or attack.
    
- The fire briefly burns colder at the edges — almost colorless.
    

**Tone:** Fire interacting with unstable reality.

---

## If Oscar (05C4R) Attunes

**Effect: Pattern Recognition**

The Reliquary’s rings rotate in sync with faint geometric shifts in Oscar’s chassis.

- Once per long rest, Oscar may gain advantage on an Investigation or Arcana check involving anomalies.
    
- Occasionally, Oscar perceives Veil distortions as mathematical patterns rather than emotional phenomena.
    

**Tone:** Construct meets cosmic geometry.

---

# Subtle Risk (Shared Across All)

Each time the active ability of the Reliquary is used:

Roll 1d8.

On an 8:

- The personal resonance intensifies briefly.
    
- The character experiences a 2–3 second intrusive flash tied to their imprint.
    
- No damage.
    
- Just unease.
    

This reinforces that the bond deepens over time.

---

# Why This Works

- It personalizes the quest immediately.
    
- It rewards attunement without overpowering.
    
- It ties Veil exposure to character arcs.
    
- It gives you long-term escalation hooks.
