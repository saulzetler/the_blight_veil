## Session 4 Finale — _“The Fire Spreads”_

**Bombshell Reveal: Uprisings in Orrenval**

This moment should feel like a distant tremor that will soon become an earthquake.

You are not just delivering news — you are showing the players that their actions in (the killing of Captain Deyran) have begun to reshape the world.

---

# Scene Setup

**Location Options (Choose One):**

- The Rootspire tavern, late night, lantern-fungus dimmed.
    
- The rebel camp at Ashroot’s edge.
    
- Immediately following a council audience, as they are dismissed.
    
- Around a quiet fire with Cadran and Rilwen.
    

The key: this is a _moment of quiet_ before disruption.

---

# Read-Aloud

> The forest wind shifts.
> 
> Not colder — sharper.
> 
> Somewhere above, the canopy trembles as something cuts through it at speed.
> 
> A hawk?
> 
> No.
> 
> A darting streak of ember-red light threads through the branches before striking a trunk nearby with a hiss.
> 
> A message cylinder, still smoking.
> 
> The wax seal bears the Concord’s sigil — cracked clean through.
> 
> Someone has already broken it.
> 
> The parchment inside smells faintly of ash.
> 
> The script is hurried.
> 
> **“Orrenval unrest escalating. Three districts ignited in protest following Captain Deyran’s execution. Purity enforcement intensified. Minor insurrections suppressed. More expected.”**
> 
> A final line has been added in different ink:
> 
> **“The fire sings louder than they can silence.”**

Pause.

Let that line breathe.

---

# DM Notes — Delivery Variants

### If Delivered by a Rebel Runner

- They are breathless.
    
- They’ve lost two riders on the road.
    
- They mention increased patrols.
    
- They are afraid — but also hopeful.
    

### If Delivered by a Cindersong Contact

- Calm.
    
- Almost pleased.
    
- Speaks in metaphor.
    
- Implies this was inevitable.
    

### If Delivered Through Council Knowledge

- The Council already knows.
    
- They are observing.
    
- They do not intervene.
    
- One member (perhaps Ember Speaker or Veil Watcher) studies the party carefully.
    

---

# What Has Happened in Orrenval

Draw directly from canon in :

- Captain Deyran was a public executioner figure.
    
- His death was public.
    
- It occurred during a trial.
    
- The Veil tore open immediately after.
    
- The Sanctara bells tolled.
    

The public narrative is fractured.

### Rumors in the Capital:

- Some believe the party were clumsy criminals.
    
- Some whisper they were chosen.
    
- Some believe Deyran’s death triggered the Veil event.
    
- Some believe the Veil event triggered his death.
    

Uncertainty breeds instability.

---

# Indications of Shifting Power

Give subtle political signals:

- Varric Thane (Might) pushes for harsher enforcement.
    
- Elira Voss (Vision) calls for investigation into “ideological contamination.”
    
- Silven Corvane (Guile) is quiet.
    

The Trifarix is not unified.

This is the first visible crack.

---

# Cindersong Implication (Subtle, Not Confirmed)

Do not confirm outright.

Layer hints:

### Clues:

- Protesters in Orrenval are singing forbidden harmonies.
    
- The phrase “The fire sings” appears in multiple graffiti tags.
    
- A Concord courier was found burned with no visible flame damage.
    
- Three-ring sigils have been spotted in alleyways.
    

Reference the sigil from (Cindersong hideout symbol).

You may say:

> “It’s not just anger,” the messenger says quietly.  
> “Someone is teaching them the songs.”

That line is enough.

---

# Emotional Hooks (Per Character)

### Zennor

The unrest began in his old district.

Someone shouts his name in the streets — either in praise or curse.

His past is now political.

---

### Hog

The fighting pits in Orrenval are closed.

Crowds that once cheered him now chant slogans instead.

Chaos is replacing spectacle.

---

### Fergie

Some protesters are wielding flame openly.

Fire as symbol.  
Fire as rebellion.  
Fire as purification.

Is this what mastery looks like?

---

### Oscar

Reports mention a construct seen in the lower district helping civilians escape patrols.

Was it coincidence?

Or are others like them awakening?

# Skill Checks (Optional Layering)

**Insight (DC 13):**  
The messenger is holding something back.

**History (DC 15):**  
Orrenval has not seen unrest in decades.

**Religion (DC 14):**  
Singing as rebellion is theological heresy under the Sanctara.

**Investigation (DC 16):**  
The added line (“The fire sings louder…”) was written by someone trained.

---

# Escalation Beat (Optional Shock)

After the reveal, interrupt the moment:

- A second message arrives — intercepted Concord orders declaring martial law in two districts.
    
- Or a blighted refugee arrives from Orrenval.
    
- Or the Council of Eight comments cryptically:
    

> “You have moved a stone. Now watch the mountain.”

---

# Consequences

Immediate:

- The party becomes symbols, willingly or not.
    
- The rebels grow bolder.
    
- Concord patrol intensity increases in Ashroot.
    

Long-Term:

- The Concord may send an Inquisitor-level hunter.
    
- The Trifarix fractures publicly.
    
- Cindersong may attempt direct contact.
    
- The Sanctara may declare theological war.
    

---

# Tone Guidance

This should not feel like victory.

It should feel like inevitability.

The party did not “start a revolution.”

They removed a keystone.

And now the arch is shifting.

---

# Final Line to End Session

Let someone — not the party — say:

> “They killed one captain.”
> 
> “But they didn’t kill the fear.”
> 
> “They killed the illusion.”

Fade to black.
