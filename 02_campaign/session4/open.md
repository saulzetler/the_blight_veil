## Session 4 — Opening Scene: **Through the Veil, Before the Council**

“The Council will see you now.”

The eight figures remain seated in their crescent of root-carved thrones, their forms half-shadowed by the amber glow bleeding through Rootspire’s living walls. The air smells of sap and distant rain. Beneath your boots, the floor pulses faintly — not a heartbeat, but something older.

The members who voted against you withdraw their gaze. But the majority stands.

Roots begin to shift.

From the center of the chamber, the wood splits without splintering. Pale light spills upward through the widening seam. It is not fire. Not sunlight. It is Veil-light — thin, overexposed, humming at a frequency your bones recognize before your mind does.

Oscar’s terrarium flickers in answer.

Zennor — the bracelet on your wrist warms.

Fergie — the air tastes like the moment before flame catches.

Hog — the hairs on your arms rise as if before a coming storm.

The seam widens into a perfect vertical aperture. Through it you see no landscape — only shifting pale geometry and the faint silhouettes of enormous shapes beyond, indistinct and watching.

The hum deepens. Harmonizes.

Then — the light collapses inward, condensing into eight burning sigils suspended in the air before you.

Each glyph floats, slowly rotating. Simple. Ancient. Distinct.

They are unlabelled.

The Council speaks as one:

“Know us.”

The chamber falls silent.

Each path leaves a mark.  
Each mark reveals a truth.

# If the Players Fail

## 1. Harmonic Rejection (Soft Fail — Information Penalty)

**What Happens:**  
The chosen glyph sequence resonates incorrectly. The chamber vibrates out of tune.

- The glyphs shudder.
    
- A discordant tone fills the air.
    
- The light flickers sickly green.
    

The sequence dissolves and reforms.

> “You listen… but do not hear.”

### Mechanical Effect

- Each character makes a **DC 14 Con save**
    
- On a failure: take 2d6 psychic damage and gain **Disadvantage on the next Insight or Perception check** in this chamber.
    
- On a success: half damage, no disadvantage.
    

### Why This Works

- Stakes without derailing momentum.
    
- Reinforces the harmonic theme.
    
- Signals this is about understanding, not guessing.

## 2. The Veil Pushes Back (Dangerous Option)

The wrong sequence causes the portal to destabilize.

The chamber fractures. Gravity tilts.  
For one round, the party sees into the Veil.

Run a **1-round surreal combat encounter**:

- 2–3 Veil Echoes manifest.
    
- Not deadly — but overwhelming.
    
- The Council does not intervene.
    

When it ends:

> “Order is not arbitrary.”

This makes the glyph puzzle feel cosmically important.

### Read-Aloud: Entering the Portal

The air folds inward. Color drains. Sound thins. For a breathless instant, you feel pulled through something far too narrow.

Then—

You stand on pale stone that casts shadows the wrong direction. Light hums here, source unseen. The air vibrates with an unfinished chord.

Sigils hang at impossible depths — some within reach, others distant despite appearing close.

Ahead, the space widens into a vast circular expanse.

Eight presences manifest.

Not seated. Not standing.

Simply **there**.

You have the unmistakable sense they were already speaking —

And stopped when you arrived.

---

### DM Notes: The Council Chamber

- The chamber is **not physically coherent**. Distance, scale, and orientation subtly shift depending on who is speaking.
    
- Each council member manifests differently, reinforcing that this is not a unified body.
    
- When one speaks, the harmonics of the chamber subtly retune — sigils brighten, dim, or drift out of alignment.
    
- The council does not introduce itself formally. They assume the party understands the _weight_ of where they stand.
    

Use silence here. Let the players speak first if they want to.

---

### The Council Speaks (Initial Exchange)

One presence — steady, resonant — addresses the party first.

> “You have crossed a threshold few are permitted to approach intact.”

A second voice overlaps slightly, sharper, impatient.

> “Intact enough. That will have to suffice.”

A third presence lingers before speaking, its voice echoing half a second late.

> “What matters is not _how_ they arrived. It is _why the Veil allowed it_.”

The others do not contradict this — but several manifestations subtly shift, as if bristling.

---

### Truth / Lie / Omission (Initial Statements)

|Statement|Nature|
|---|---|
|“You were permitted entry.”|**Misleading** — the portal responded to Veil instability, not permission|
|“Few survive passage.”|**True**|
|“The Veil is under strain.”|**True**|
|“Your actions have drawn attention.”|**True**, but not all of it benevolent|
|“We are in agreement on this matter.”|**Intentional Omission** — they are not|

---

### Player Interruption Points

**Moment 1: Questioning Authority**  
If a player asks _who_ the council truly is, or _why_ they should listen:

> “Names are simplifications. Functions endure.”

If pressed further, one manifestation briefly destabilizes — its outline flickers, sigils scattering before reforming.

**Moment 2: Challenging Motives**  
If a player accuses the council of manipulation or secrecy:

> “Secrecy implies ownership of truth. We possess no such luxury.”

This is technically accurate — and evasive.

---

### Skill Checks (Optional)

**Insight (DC 14)**

- Success: The council members are not aligned emotionally. Some are wary. Some are urgent. One is openly hostile but restrained.
    
- Failure: The discord feels overwhelming — it’s hard to tell disagreement from ritual.
    

**Arcana (DC 15)**

- Success: The chamber’s harmonics shift depending on _which_ presence speaks. This place is being actively stabilized.
    
- Failure: The magic feels vast but unreadable.
    

**Religion (DC 13)**

- Success: These manifestations resemble _no single divine tradition_. If gods are involved here, they are not whole.
    
- Failure: You can’t map this to any known cosmology.
    

---

### Conditional Beats

**If the Players Challenge the Council’s Authority**

- The light in the chamber dims slightly.
    
- Sigils rotate faster, some cracking at the edges.
    
- One voice responds, colder:
    

> “Authority is not claimed here. It is endured.”

This does not escalate into hostility — but it _is_ remembered.

**If the Players Remain Deferential**

- The council speaks more freely — but still selectively.
    
- One presence adds:
    

> “Your restraint is noted. It will not go unrewarded.”

What that means is not clarified.

---

### DM Notes: Fracture Made Visible

- Allow subtle contradictions between council members.
    
- Avoid consensus language.
    
- Let overlapping speech happen once — just enough to signal internal fracture.
    
- No member fully explains themselves. They speak in _functions_, not identities.
    

---

### Scene End Beat (Before the Quest)

One final statement, delivered not by a single voice — but by several, slightly out of sync:

> “The Veil is failing in places you cannot yet see.
> 
> And it has noticed you.”

The harmonics drop in pitch. The sigils dim.  
The chamber waits.

_End the scene here. Transition into the quest briefing next._
