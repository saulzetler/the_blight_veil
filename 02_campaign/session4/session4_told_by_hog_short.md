# Session 4 — As Told by Hog Thunderwood

_Dwarf. Monk. Pants Optional._

---

Right. Council of Eight. Eight scary floaty presences, glowing symbols in the air, big crack in the floor pouring pale light. We solved their wee puzzle using things we'd remembered from the trial — clever lot when we want to be — and went in. One by one. Into the light hole.

Inside, the room didn't work right. The Council told us to go find the places where the Veil is going wrong, have a look, don't touch anything, come back with information. Very reasonable. Then someone asked what we're actually supposed to _do_ when we get there.

And the Whispering Mask — sinister little thing — just _erupts_:

> **"PURIFY THEM."**

The Veil Watcher turned to it, cool as anything: _"Silence. You are on this council. But you are not granted the power to speak."_

The Mask shut right up. I filed that under: _watch that one._

---

Before they booted us out, the Council pressed some of their symbols into a metal disc — incomplete markings, a seam down the middle that wobbles like it's listening. Fergie attuned to it during a rest, and the disc told Fergie its name.

_Jaihroquai._

I think Jaihroquai is doing great work. I have nothing but respect for Jaihroquai. Jaihroquai does not register my existence in any meaningful way. I remain hopeful. The disc has not given me reason to be hopeful.

---

Back in Rootspire we found a tavern, had a drink, I won a board game against Zennor — brilliant tactical display, don't ask him about it — and in the morning there was a fellow sitting on the steps outside.

Thin. Mossy. Tilting his head at angles heads shouldn't do. Said his name was Timoor. We asked him questions. He answered them strangely. We invited him along. He said yes in the way that felt like he already knew he would.

---

Then a runner arrived. Road-dusted woman, Concord seal on the cylinder, already cracked open. Parchment smelled of ash.

Three districts in Orrenval. Protests. Unrest. All of it traced back to Captain Deyran — aye, _that_ Captain Deyran, the one we killed. Purity enforcement ramping up. And a second line, different handwriting:

> _"The fire sings louder than they can silence."_

---

_So. Mysterious disc that ignores me. Construct spotted in the lower roots that looks like Oscar. A council with a secret it won't let one of its own say out loud. And a city burning over a dead captain._

_Can't forget Timoor._

_Pants are where I left them. Let's go._
