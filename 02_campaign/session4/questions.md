
# Step 1 — Send This to the Group Chat

Keep it simple. No explanation.

> Before next session I have a question for each of you:
> 
> **What deity (if any) does your character believe in?**
> 
> If none — what do they believe _is_ divine?
> 
> Don’t overthink it. First instinct answers are best.

That alone will make them nervous.

---

# Step 2 — Follow-Up Questions (Based on Their Answers)

You send these privately to each player after they respond.

## If They Believe in a Specific God

Ask:

1. What does your character think that god wants from them?
    
2. Has that god ever answered a prayer?
    
3. What would make them doubt that god?
    

This lets you measure:

- Devotion
    
- Transactional faith vs reverence
    
- Fear of abandonment
    

---

## If They Don’t Believe in Gods

Ask:

1. Do they believe power comes from within, nature, fate, or something else?
    
2. Do they think gods are real but unworthy?
    
3. If they were proven wrong, how would they react?
    

This reveals:

- Ego vs skepticism
    
- Trauma-based disbelief
    
- Hidden longing for meaning
    

---

# Step 3 — Quietly Link Each PC to a Council Member

Now let’s tailor this to your canon party.

---

## Zennor → Dawnbinder or Oath-Severed

Zennor’s grief and rage are already central .

Ask him privately:

- Does Zennor believe the gods allowed his daughter to die?
    
- If a god offered to return her at a cost, would he pay it?
    
- Does he believe justice is divine — or something mortals enforce?
    

If he answers with:

- **Vengeance** → Oath-Severed
    
- **Redemption / second chances** → Dawnbinder
    
- **No gods deserve worship** → Veil Watcher twist
    

Whichever path he leans toward?  
Have that council member speak to him differently next session.

---

## Fergie → Ember Speaker (Obvious Surface Link)

Fergie is a fire genasi cleric seeking ultimate mastery of fire .

But don’t assume fire = faith.

Ask:

- Is fire a tool, a god, or a test?
    
- Would Fergie extinguish their own flame to save someone?
    
- Is mastery about control or surrender?
    

If they say:

- Fire is sacred → Ember Speaker leans in
    
- Fire is power to be mastered → Stone Teller challenges them
    
- Fire is rebellion → Whispering Mask probes corruption
    

You can create tension if the Ember Speaker subtly disapproves of Fergie’s interpretation of flame.

---

## Hog → Stone Teller or Rootkeeper

Hog is chaotic on the surface, but monks often believe in discipline beyond humor .

Ask:

- Does Hog believe in destiny?
    
- Does he think strength comes from training or divine blessing?
    
- When he fights, does he feel watched?
    

If:

- Discipline is sacred → Stone Teller
    
- Nature balance matters → Rootkeeper
    
- “Gods don’t care” → Veil Watcher marks him
    

Imagine the Council member who represents patience suddenly addressing Hog as if he sees through the pants joke.

That’s unsettling.

---

## Oscar → Veil Watcher (Extremely Strong)

Oscar has no memory and carries a terrarium that purifies blight .

Ask:

- Does Oscar believe they have a soul?
    
- If they discovered they were built by a god, would that change anything?
    
- Does healing blight feel divine or mechanical?
    

If Oscar says:

- “I don’t know what I am” → Veil Watcher connection deepens
    
- “Purpose defines divinity” → Dawnbinder may claim them
    
- “I was made, so I cannot be holy” → Whispering Mask presses hard
    

Oscar is your metaphysical hinge.

---

# Step 4 — The Reveal Tease

After everyone answers privately, drop this into group chat a day later:

> Interesting.
> 
> It’s always revealing what mortals think about gods.
> 
> Especially when the gods are listening.

Then say nothing else.

---

# Step 5 — How to Use It In-Session

Next session, during the Council audience (which they just earned ):

Have one Council member:

- Quote a line from a player’s private answer.
    
- Refer to a doubt they admitted.
    
- Challenge their definition of divinity.
    

Not omniscient.  
Just enough to feel like they were overheard.

---

# Optional Escalation

If you want to go harder:

Have one council member say to a PC:

> “You do not worship me.
> 
> But you are already mine.”

And don’t explain.
