## Session 4 — Council Scene II: **Mandate and Dismissal**

---

### Read-Aloud: The Weight of Purpose

> The chamber retunes.
> 
> The low harmonic beneath your feet tightens, sharpening from a hum into something more deliberate. Several of the suspended sigils extinguish entirely, as if dismissed from relevance.
> 
> One presence shifts closer — not in distance, but in _clarity_. Its outline steadies while others remain half-fractured, half-withdrawn.
> 
> “The threshold you crossed was not meant to open,” it says. “Yet it did. That alone makes you… applicable.”
> 
> Another voice interjects, quieter but edged.
> 
> “We are not here to debate fortune. We are here because the Veil is failing faster than it should.”
> 
> The chamber seems to lean inward, waiting for the implication to land.

---

### DM Notes: How the Council Frames the Quest

- The council presents the task as **containment and observation**, not heroism.
    
- They emphasize _localized anomalies_ — places where the Veil has thinned, torn, or begun behaving unpredictably.
    
- The party is being sent because:
    
    - They have already survived exposure.
        
    - They are _not yet politically entangled_.
        
    - Their loss, while regrettable, would not destabilize the Concord.
        

Do not state that last point aloud — let it be felt.

---

### Quest Delivery: The Mandate

One presence speaks with measured restraint:

> “There are fractures forming beyond Orrenval’s immediate reach. Points of strain where the Veil buckles, resonates, or bleeds into what lies beneath.”
> 
> “You will go to these places. You will observe. You will intervene only where collapse accelerates.”

A second presence overlays the statement, sharper:

> “Do not attempt repair. That knowledge is… incomplete.”

A third adds, reluctantly:

> “Bring back _patterns_. Not solutions.”

---

### Quest Summary (For the Players)

- **Objective:** Investigate multiple, geographically distinct Veil anomalies.
    
- **Primary Task:** Identify what is happening, not to fix it.
    
- **Secondary Task:** Report signs of escalation, resonance, or spread.
    
- **Unspoken Expectation:** Survival is uncertain; improvisation is assumed.
    

---

### Truth / Uncertainty / Downplayed Risk

|Element|Reality|
|---|---|
|“The anomalies are localized.”|**Contested** — some council members believe they are connected|
|“Observation is sufficient.”|**Misleading** — intervention may be unavoidable|
|“You will recognize escalation.”|**True**, but only in hindsight|
|“Others cannot be spared.”|**Partially true** — some _have_ refused|

---

### Player Interruption Points

**Asking for Support or Protection**

If players ask for resources, artifacts, or guarantees:

> “What you request requires consensus,”  
> a voice answers.
> 
> Another presence shifts uncomfortably.
> 
> “Consensus is… unavailable.”

They may offer _information fragments_ later — not protection.

---

**Attempting to Negotiate Terms or Rewards**

If the players press for payment, titles, or authority:

> “You misunderstand the nature of this arrangement,”  
> one presence replies.
> 
> “You are not being compensated. You are being _trusted_.”

Whether that trust is genuine is unclear.

---

### Optional Skill Checks

**Insight (DC 14)**

- Success: The council is not aligned on whether the party is expendable.
    
- Failure: The mixed tone reads as ritual formality.
    

**Arcana (DC 15)**

- Success: The chamber is already destabilizing — this meeting is being cut short out of necessity.
    
- Failure: The magic feels controlled, but tense.
    

**History or Religion (DC 13)**

- Success: This resembles ancient mandates issued during crisis periods — rarely ended cleanly.
    
- Failure: The procedure feels unique, unprecedented.
    

---

### Consequences of Pushback

- **Excessive Resistance:** The dismissal becomes abrupt and forceful.
    
- **Measured Acceptance:** The council allows one final question — answered incompletely.
    
- **Defiance:** The chamber reacts physically; sigils fracture, light drops, and the removal is immediate.
    

No hostility. Just removal.

---

### The Dismissal

The harmonics begin to unravel.  
Sigils collapse inward, extinguishing one by one.

> “You will not be summoned again lightly,”  
> a voice says — not as a warning, but a statement of fact.
> 
> “When next we speak, circumstances will be worse.”

The space beneath your feet gives way — not violently, but decisively.

---

### Read-Aloud: Ejection

> The light folds.
> 
> The chamber inverts, depth collapsing into a single sharp point of sensation. For a breathless instant, you feel _unobserved_ — then the world reasserts itself all at once.
> 
> Sound returns first. Then weight. Then gravity.
> 
> You are no longer before the council.
> 
> Whatever protection their presence offered is gone.

---

### DM Notes: Transition

- Do **not** allow immediate follow-up questions.
    
- Move directly into descent, relocation, or aftermath.
    
- Let the players process _absence_ — the sudden lack of cosmic oversight.
    

This sets up Rootspire and the ground-level consequences cleanly.
