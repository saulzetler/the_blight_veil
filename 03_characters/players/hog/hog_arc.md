# Hog Thunderwood’s Arc

## **Echo of the Stormfather — Full DM Guide**

A cinematic, atmospheric arc woven through _The Blight Veil_, built around unstable harmonics, old forest spirits, Concord interference, and Hog’s lightning-marked fate.  
Below is a **modular, session-ready structure** with **read-alouds**, **DM notes**, **decision points**, and **consequences**.

---

# **I. Act One — Whispers in the Static**

### **1. The First Surge (Early Campaign)**

**Trigger:** Moments when the Veil tremors, or when Hog confronts strong emotion or danger.

> **Read-Aloud (short surge)**  
> A sharp static crackles across Hog’s skin. For an instant, a pale sigil — jagged, branching like a storm-wracked tree — flickers along his gauntlet token. The air hums with a low, discordant note, as though the forest itself inhaled.

**DM Notes:**

- This sigil is the _Stormfather’s Fragment_, a sliver of a forest guardian sundered when the Concord dismantled the sky-wards.
    
- The sigil reacts to **Blight harmonics**, **Cindersong melodies**, and **ancient Ashroot sites**.
    

**Skill Check Opportunity**

- **Arcana DC 12:** It’s resonating with ambient magic, not Hog’s emotions alone.
    
- **Nature DC 14:** The pattern resembles storm-blasted branches… or old druidic lightning marks.
    
- **History DC 15:** Lightning guardians appear in a few banned texts about pre-Concord druids.
    

---

### **Decision Point 1: Does Hog chase or ignore the storm-sigil?**

**Choices & Consequences:**

- **Investigates:** Hog gains earlier lore, sparks NPC interest, and the sigil becomes more active.
    
- **Suppresses/ignores:** The sigil grows volatile. Later surges become dangerous (1 lightning damage splash on fails). NPCs may misinterpret it as corruption.
    

---

# **II. Act Two — The Broken Sky-Wards**

### **2. The First Vision: The Stormfather’s Fall**

**Trigger:** Entering Rootspire, the Harmonium Chambers, or any Veil disturbance.

> **Read-Aloud (vision)**  
> Thunder rolls without sound. A colossal figure of stormlight stands amid shattered stone spires. Runes spiral upward like lightning paths — then snap. A scream of wind. A blade in the dark. The guardian collapses, its essence splintering like glass into the canopy.

**DM Notes:**

- This is the **moment the Concord struck down the Stormfather**.
    
- The splinter Hog carries is one of several scattered fragments.
    

**Skill Checks**

- **Insight DC 13:** The Stormfather wasn’t attacking — it was defending something.
    
- **Arcana DC 15:** The runes were designed to channel weather harmonics, not weaponize them.
    

---

### **3. The Sky-Ward Ruins (Mid Campaign)**

A site the party may reach: a shattered stone spire overgrown with blight-scarred vines.

> **Read-Aloud**  
> A broken tower of pale stone leans like a finger pointing toward a cloudless sky. Carved channels spiral its surface, blackened as though scorched from within. Every footstep here feels too loud — as if the air is holding its breath.

**DM Notes:**

- Touching the runes triggers a **Storm Memory**: Hog sees **Concord sappers** placing sigil-breakers, acting under the Trifarix of Might.
    
- Hog feels a pull — like unfinished business in the clouds.
    

---

### **Decision Point 2: Does Hog restore the sky-wards or leave them dead?**

**Choices & Consequences:**

- **Begins restoration:**
    
    - Gains **Stormfather’s Boon (Dormant):** once per long rest, deal +1 lightning damage per level on a hit.
        
    - Cindersong druids become curious allies.
        
    - Concord agents begin tracking Hog as a “latent storm asset.”
        
- **Leaves them dead:**
    
    - The Ashroot weakens; small weather anomalies appear (drought pockets, erratic winds).
        
    - Hog avoids Concord attention… but the sigil warps under strain.
        

---

# **III. Act Three — Gathering the Fragments**

### **4. Hunt for the Splinters**

There are **three fragments** besides Hog’s. Each can be a mini-arc, optional or integrated into main quests:

1. **The Gale Shard** — carried by a wandering monk fleeing the Concord.
    
2. **The Tempest Knot** — buried in a blight-nest; corrupt and dangerous.
    
3. **The Thunder Seed** — sealed by druids who fear the Stormfather’s return.
    

**DM Notes:**  
Each fragment strengthens Hog… but also accelerates the Stormfather’s influence.

**Mechanical Progression:**

- 1 Fragment: +1 to lightning spell/save DCs
    
- 2 Fragments: reaction to reduce lightning damage taken
    
- 3 Fragments: once per long rest, call a minor storm (fog cloud + rumble)
    
- 4 Fragments: unlocks the full mantle (endgame choice)
    

---

### **Decision Point 3: Embrace or Resist the Stormfather’s influence?**

**Choices & Consequences:**

- **Embraces the power:**
    
    - Hog becomes faster, more volatile; emotional storms can cause collateral damage.
        
    - NPCs express awe/fear.
        
    - Concord deploys storm-breaker squads.
        
- **Resists:**
    
    - Hog remains grounded; fragments stay dormant longer.
        
    - Stormfather visions become pleading, almost mournful.
        
    - Cindersong druids may see him as a failed “chosen.”
        

---

# **IV. Act Four — Truth of the Concord’s Crime**

### **5. Revelation of the Betrayal**

An encounter with a Concord Archivist or captured operative.

**Key Lore:**

- The Stormfather refused to allow the Concord to siphon Ashroot harmonics.
    
- The Trifarix of Might ordered the sky-wards dismantled under the claim of “security.”
    
- The Stormfather was ambushed — not defeated honorably.
    

This moment changes Hog’s understanding of the conflict.

---

### **Decision Point 4: Does Hog seek justice or balance?**

**Choices:**

- **Justice (vengeance):** confront Concord leadership later; becomes a symbol for rebel cells.
    
- **Balance (restoration):** focus on mending the forest and Veil, avoiding political escalation.
    
- **Indifference (walk his own path):** keeps storms internal; may break from both sides.
    

**Campaign Consequences:**

- Rebels rally or fracture depending on Hog’s stance.
    
- Concord mounts a propaganda push labeling Hog a “Storm Zealot.”
    
- The Ashroot’s weather begins mirroring Hog’s emotional arc.
    

---

# **V. Act Five — The Mantle of Storms (Endgame)**

### **6. The Heart-Spire Convergence**

A final ancient spire, still intact but dormant, buried deep beneath the Ashroot.

> **Read-Aloud**  
> The chamber opens like the hollow heart of a thundercloud. Runes coil upward, awaiting a spark. In the center stands a stone dais — cracked, blackened, and humming with a single plea: **“Choose.”**

**DM Notes:**  
This is where Hog decides the Stormfather’s fate — and his own.

---

# **Final Decision Point — The Stormfather’s Fate**

## **A. Reclaim the Mantle**

Hog becomes the new Stormfather.  
**Consequences:**

- Gains a storm-aspect form in future (wings of lightning, booming voice).
    
- Weather in Ashroot stabilizes.
    
- Concord loses control over weather sigil-lines; political fallout erupts.
    
- The Veil grows calmer around Hog but more violent elsewhere.
    

## **B. Destroy the Mantle**

Break the cycle of sacrificial guardianship.

**Consequences:**

- Permanent weather chaos until other solutions are found.
    
- Hog gains a powerful but finite lightning burst ability.
    
- Cindersong druids see him as a heretic; Concord sees opportunity.
    
- The Veil destabilizes briefly but reveals hidden truths in the chaos.
    

## **C. Free the Stormfather’s Spirit**

Neither claim nor destroy — release it back into the Veil.

**Consequences:**

- The Stormfather becomes a neutral, semi-mythic ally.
    
- Hog keeps subtle storm powers, but no mantle.
    
- The Ashroot gains a wandering guardian spirit that may intervene unpredictably.
    
- Concord panics — this wasn’t in their political calculus.
    

---

# **NPC Reactions (Summary)**

- **Cindersong:**
    
    - Support restoration or release; oppose destruction.
        
- **Concord (Trifarix of Might):**
    
    - Fear restoration; prefer mantle destroyed; try to manipulate Hog.
        
- **Rootspire Druids:**
    
    - Seek balance; guide toward releasing or restoring.
        
- **Party:**
    
    - Social consequences based on collateral storm events, moral choices, and the final mantle decision.
