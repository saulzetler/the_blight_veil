# Hog Thunderwood

## Hill Dwarf, Way of the Elements Monk

Trinket: Small iron tile with a gauntleted closed fist wreathed in lightning

Picture the most magnificently mediocre wood, deep in the auburn forest. Surrounded by unremarkable hilly knolls, a boy named Hog \- bestowed the name of one of the regal and powerful beasts of the forest, as is custom in these Hill Dwarves culture \- grew up leading an aggressively plain life. 

Like any coming of age story, this wee \- and I do mean wee, for our boy was the runt of the litter \- lad experienced his gawky adolescence by struggling to fit in and being relentlessly picked on by the beefier dwarves in his tribe. Which was, to say, all of them. Hunky and beefy. Boys. 

In one particularly disturbing instance, when Hog had finally mustered the courage to retaliate against his buffed up peers, by some divinely comical twist of fate, the drawstring to his trousers had burst free, plunging his second-hand pantaloons to rest forlornly at his ankles. But Hog, of single-mind and brain cell, rushed forward none the wiser. Distracted by the absurdity of the situation, his assailants were left gawking, welcoming the first blow to land squarely upon their jaw \- marking with a wet squelch the start to a wonderful story…

That is until the beefy buff boys gathered themselves and laid poor ol’ Hog out in a single, half strength blow square to the nose.

But that was enough. Hog had had a taste of beefy boy blood and he was desperate for more. The problem was, the only time he had ever landed a punch was full cock out pugilism. Though, that is to say, not many people wanted to fight Hog after that. 

Desperate for more bouts, and eager to breathe fire back into the coals of his family's ancient and powerful warrior legacy \- the Thunderwoods \- Hog eventually left his family and comely forest village in search of glory in the more densely populated world. He traveled from city to city, searching for any place he could test his mettle. And he found it \- in spades. In underground fighting pits, in dark alleys, in front of grand castles. To this day, Hog’s *official* fighting record is an astounding 3-71. It has been rumoured he may have the single worst fighting record of any breathing mammal known to man. But the hardy dwarf fights. And many more than you might think have been eager to fight the hopeless, the notorious Hog the Bottomless.

Praised for his tenacity and uncharacteristically positive demeanour, he has been conscripted (or coerced) into numerous guardsmen, orders, and armies over his many years. Hog joins this adventure as a plucky middle-aged dwarf with a twinkle in his eye that just never seems to go out. With his ancestors’ thunder newly awakened in his heart, and their lightning pumping through his veins, this bottomless fury of fists is ready to show anyone who’s watching just how low it hangs… that is to say strictly average with a perplexing curvature to the right.
