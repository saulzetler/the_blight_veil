## 🌲 Hometown & Placement in the World

* **Hometown:** **Bramblethane**, a modest dwarven settlement tucked into the **Auburnwilds** (southern rim of the Ashenwood). Known for its ochre-red forests and rolling hills, it’s a place of good ale, middling craftsmanship, and a reputation for being “just fine.”
* The Thunderwood clan is remembered in dwarven histories as legendary warrior-smiths who once wielded storm-forged weapons against giants in the Godfall era. These days, their legacy has dwindled to little more than family stories, woodcutting, and mediocre brawling tournaments.

---

## ⚡ Major Events Tied to Backstory

1. **The Fall of the Thunder Arena**
   Centuries ago, the Thunderwoods maintained an arena-temple devoted to Kord, god of storms and strength. The site was struck by a freak lightning storm during the Godfall, toppling its great pillars and burying it beneath the Auburnwild hills. Local lore claims that the Thunderwood legacy slumbers there until a true heir awakens it.
   *Tie-in:* Hog’s trinket (the iron tile) is a fragment of the temple’s shattered altar. His family passed it down, never knowing it was more than just a memento.

2. **The Bottomless Fight**
   Hog’s most infamous fight — the trousers incident — somehow made its way into Concord gossip, bards’ tavern songs, and even city posters (“*See Hog the Bottomless! He Fights! He Falls! He Smiles Again!*”). Though humiliating, this notoriety means Hog is already **known** when he arrives at the Concord capital. Some might sneer, but others (especially underground fighters and the Cindersong) respect his refusal to quit.

## ⚡ The Trinket: Iron Tile of the Gauntlet

* **Name:** *Stormfist Fragment*.
* **Appearance:** A rough palm-sized iron tile etched with a gauntlet clenched in lightning. The design hums faintly when storms gather.
* **Lore:** This was once part of the altar at the Thunder Arena, consecrated to Kord. The shattering of the altar scattered fragments across Thunderwood bloodlines, said to act as a “spark” waiting to reignite the clan’s storm.
* **Symbolic Significance:** To Hog’s family, it’s just a keepsake. But to storm priests and Concord archivists, it’s a dangerous relic that could awaken elemental storms.
* **Magical Properties (scales with campaign):**

  * *Dormant:* Hog can cause harmless static sparks to crackle around his fists when concentrating. Once per long rest, he can add **1d4 lightning damage** to an unarmed strike.
  * *Awakened (later):* Hog can reroll a missed unarmed attack once per long rest, as lightning arcs guide his strike.
  * *Fully Awakened (major arc):* May channel **Stormstrike** — adding 2d6 lightning damage to all unarmed strikes for 1 minute (once per long rest).

## 🎭 Campaign Integration

* **Session 1 Hook:**
  As the Concord prepares to burn Marla, storm clouds gather unnaturally fast. Hog’s iron tile hums and grows warm, as though the gauntlet carved upon it is clenching harder. If Hog holds it during the scene, faint static crackles around his knuckles.
* **Concord Connection:**
  Some inquisitors recognize the gauntlet mark — archived as a forbidden symbol of pre-Concord storm cults. Hog could be targeted as a potential heretic, even if he doesn’t understand why.
* **Cindersong Connection:**
  Members of the Cindersong see Hog’s resilience as proof he embodies the *song of storms*. They may try to recruit him as a folk hero, seeing him as the living spark of Thunderwood legend.

## 🌀 Narrative Potential

* **Underdog Rising:** Hog’s laughable fighting record becomes mythic — the “Bottomless” dwarf who just won’t stay down. NPCs (and players) will love him for it.
* **Prophecy of the Thunderwood:** The Blight Veil itself reacts violently to storms; Hog’s stormline blood may hold the key to shaping or resisting that corruption.
* **Crossroads of Reputation:** Does Hog embrace his role as a “joke turned legend” or strive to prove the Thunderwood name truly belongs among heroes?
