# Zennor’s Arc — _The Hollow Thread of Memory_

## **Core Theme**

_Memory, guilt, and the fragility of truth under a breaking Veil._  
Zennor’s arc centers on uncovering who truly killed his daughter—and whether the memories he holds are even real. The unstable Veil will twist recollection, making the players question how much of the past can be trusted.

His bracelet, given by his daughter, becomes the anchor and the key.

---

# **Act I — The Fractured Remembering**

### **Read-Aloud Prompt (optional scene trigger)**

> A soft harmony shivers through the trees. Zennor’s bracelet warms—just barely—and a fleeting image flares behind his eyes: a candlelit room, a girl’s laugh, and then a scream swallowed by static.

### **DM Notes**

- Establish that Zennor’s memories conflict with reports he’s heard.
    
- The Concord keeps sparse records of the incident; Cindersong whispers that a witch was involved but never arrested.
    
- The Veil’s instability causes harmonics that interact with personal relics; his bracelet responds to these moments.
    

### **Key Nodes**

1. **The First Echo**  
    While interacting with any Veil-touched artifact (monoliths, sigils, harmonics), Zennor glimpses a _fragment_ of the night his daughter died.
    
    - He remembers someone watching from the doorway—he thinks it's his wife.
        
2. **Discovery: Missing Records**  
    A Concord scribe or rebel archivist reveals that official records of his daughter’s death are incomplete or sealed.
    
3. **Choice 1 — Who Does He Trust?**
    
    - **Trust the rebels’ version:**  
        He’s told his wife practiced forbidden sigilmancy.  
        **Consequence:** Cindersong treats him as a potential ally; Concord becomes wary.
        
    - **Trust the Concord’s version:**  
        They say his wife had no magical capacity.  
        **Consequence:** He receives guarded cooperation from Concord agents; rebels distrust him.
        
    - **Refuse to pick a side yet:**  
        **Consequence:** Both factions remain neutral; information flow slows.

# **Act II — The Witch’s Footprint**

## **The Bracelet Awakens**

Zennor’s bracelet attunes to Veil harmonics and begins showing clearer visions.  
You can treat this as a milestone upgrade:

- **Dormant:** brief emotional flashes
    
- **Awakened:** short sensory visions
    
- **Fully Awakened:** shows the missing truth, but only after a final confrontation
    

### **The Witch of Orrenval Rumors**

Whispers emerge that his wife fled Orrenval shortly after the murder.  
Two distinct leads appear:

1. **A Concord bounty log** showing an unnamed “sigil-user of interest” who escaped questioning.
    
2. **A rebel tale** about a woman who healed blighted creatures with strange, humming sigils.
    

### **Choice 2 — Does Zennor Pursue Her?**

- **Yes, actively:**  
    He’s granted a trail of clues—ritual sites, cleansing marks, or notes in her handwriting.  
    **Consequence:** He becomes emotionally exposed; future visions intensify.
    
- **He avoids the truth:**  
    The visions force themselves on him anyway at dramatically inconvenient moments.  
    **Consequence:** More confusion, slower progression.
    
- **He wants her dead:**  
    NPCs will warn him that the memory may not be real.  
    **Consequence:** He risks making a grave mistake later.

# **Act III — The Veil’s Echo-Twins**

## **Major Development & Twist**

The Veil has torn realities open.  
Zennor begins seeing **echo-versions** of his daughter—fleeting, non-corporeal, incomplete copies caught between harmonics.

### **Scene: Echo in the Ashroot**

The party reaches a semi-blighted grove where harmonics distort light.  
Zennor’s daughter appears—half-memory, half-harmonic.

**What she says depends on earlier choices:**

- If he trusted rebels: she begs him not to blame her mother.
    
- If he trusted Concord: she warns him they lied.
    
- If he remained neutral: she says she doesn’t know the truth either.
    

### **Skill Checks**

- **Arcana (DC 15):** This is not a ghost but a harmonic imprint.
    
- **Insight (DC 14):** She is afraid of something approaching from behind her in the memory.
    
- **Wisdom Save (DC 13):** Resist emotional overload; failure leaves Zennor shaken.
    

### **Choice 3 — His Response to the Echo**

- **Speak gently:**  
    Echo stabilizes, showing a clearer moment from the night.
    
- **Demand answers:**  
    Echo shatters abruptly; clarity is delayed.
    
- **Reach out physically:**  
    Zennor is thrown into a vision that knocks him unconscious and reveals a new clue.

# **Act IV — The True Night**

## **The Reconstructed Memory Ritual**

A place must be found where Veil harmonics are strong—Rootspire’s sigil chambers, an Ashroot monolith, or the Sanctara itself.

### **DM Notes**

This ritual should feel dangerous. The party helps anchor Zennor.

**During the ritual he sees:**

- His wife arguing with a Concord officer about forbidden experiments on their daughter
    
- His daughter screaming as a sigil-circle destabilizes
    
- A shadowed figure pulling his wife away before Zennor arrived
    
- The moment the Concord sealed the records
    

### **Truth Revealed (Core Canon Option)**

_Zennor’s wife did not kill their daughter._  
She tried to save her from a Concord experiment involving harmonic resonance.  
The unstable sigil is what killed her—accidental, tragic, avoidable.

### **Optional Alternate Truth (if you want Concord less sinister):**

His wife was experimenting alone; the Concord covered it up out of shame or political fear.

---

# **Final Confrontation Options**

### **Choice 4 — What Does Zennor Do With the Truth?**

1. **Seek his wife (if alive):**  
    Leads to a later chapter of reconciliation or confrontation.
    
2. **Bring the truth to the Concord:**  
    Lights a political fire.  
    Consequence: He becomes a target.
    
3. **Give the truth to Cindersong:**  
    They weaponize it.  
    Consequence: His grief fuels a rebellion narrative.
    
4. **Keep it private:**  
    The arc closes with emotional resolution but no political fallout.
    

---

# **Resolution & Character Growth**

Zennor’s arc ends with one of three tones:

### **1. Healing**

He accepts the truth, forgives his wife if appropriate, and binds the memory as part of himself.  
His bracelet becomes a protective relic once fully awakened.

### **2. Vengeance**

He hunts the Concord officer responsible or helps destabilize the Concord in the Trifarix’s name.

### **3. Collapse**

If he refuses truth at every stage, visions intensify until he breaks—becoming reckless or corrupted by false memories.
