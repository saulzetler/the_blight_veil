## **Character Integration: “Zennor”**

**Name:** Zennor
**Race/Class:** (TBD — likely martial, fighter or paladin archetype)  
**Alignment:** Chaotic Neutral (once Lawful Good)  
**Tone:** Gritty, disillusioned, tragic

---

### **Early Life: The Gutter-Son of Orrenval**

He was born in **Orrenval**, the gilded capital of the **Concord**, though he saw none of its splendor. His earliest memories were of the city’s underbelly — the **Canal Warrens**, where the run-off from marble streets flows through tunnels of sewage and shadow. There, he fought to survive among other gutter-children.

Street brawls became his currency — fists for food, bruises for bedding. His knack for violence caught the attention of the **Order of the Dawnshield**, a militant holy order sworn to “preserve the light of the Concord.” The Dawnshield knights were seen as paragons of faith and law — but in truth, they were the **Trifarix’s iron hand**, enforcing Concord rule through fear and purges.

---

### **The Knight of False Light**

He rose swiftly through their ranks — a weapon made loyal through gratitude. They taught him obedience, devotion, and righteousness, but only insofar as it made him useful.

For years, he fought under the Dawnshield banner, leading crusades into the outlying provinces, stamping out “heresy.” His missions grew increasingly cruel: burning villages accused of witchcraft, executing “blighted” dissidents, and purging those who spoke against the Sanctara’s authority. He told himself it was for the greater good — protecting the innocent from the corruption of the Veil.

But his faith began to crack when he met **Eris**, a healer from a border settlement razed during one of his campaigns. She saw through his armor, both literal and moral. She told him she forgave him — and he believed her. He left the Order to be with her, turning his back on the only life he’d ever known.

---

### **The Fall**

Eris’ forgiveness was a lie. Her family had been among those burned alive by the Dawnshield. She seduced him out of vengeance, her love a mask for the witchcraft she had long hidden.

When their daughter was born, she bound the child’s soul to a ritual meant to **bring ruin upon the Order** — a blood sacrifice to expose the hypocrisy of the Concord. On the night of the ritual, he discovered her plan too late: their child vanished in a storm of black fire. When the dawn came, both Eris and the Dawnshield fortress were gone.

He was left among the ashes — alive by what he calls “a horseshoe up his ass.”

---

### **Now: The Sword with No Banner**

He wanders the realm now — a **sellsword**, drunk, violent, and haunted. He clings to two things:

* **A pink twine bracelet**, made by his daughter. It’s worn, frayed, and useless — yet it keeps him from putting a blade through his own heart.
* **An amulet of silver and bone**, once belonging to Eris. It hums when he draws near places of strong magic, and when he sleeps, it drags him into dreams where he hears her voice. Some whisper it’s cursed — others say it’s a thread connecting him still to his lost child.

He no longer believes in law or good. The Concord, the Trifarix, and the gods of the Sanctara — all of them, to him, are lies built on graves. And yet, somewhere deep down, he still dreams of saving his daughter — or avenging her.

---

### **Potential World Hooks**

**1. The Order of the Dawnshield (Faction Hook)**

* Once one of the Concord’s most powerful knightly orders. Publicly disbanded after “heresies” within their ranks were exposed — but in truth, fragments remain, working as inquisitors, mercenaries, or zealots.
* He might be hunted by them as a deserter or sought after as the only one who knows what truly happened.
* Their downfall could have been caused (partly) by the witch’s ritual — a secret shame the Concord buries.

**2. Eris’ Fate (Mystery Hook)**

* She may have joined a **coven tied to the Blight itself**, working beneath the veil to undo the Concord’s divine monopoly.
* Her “death” might be false — she could now serve as a **“Veilmother”**, a witch leading others to strike back at the church.

**3. The Daughter’s Soul (Quest Hook)**

* The child’s soul could have been **bound to the Blight**, existing somewhere between life and death.
* Perhaps the pink bracelet glows faintly in places where her spirit lingers — or where witches’ rituals echo.

**4. The Amulet (Trinket Hook)**

* The amulet is **attuned to Eris’ presence** — whenever he draws near where she’s been, it burns cold.
* Secretly, it’s a **tether**; if destroyed, her spirit (or whatever she became) might be released — or unleashed.

---

### **Tone & Personality in Play**

He’s a man running from memory — bitter, blunt, but deeply wounded. He masks despair with sarcasm and drink, but when he fights, he becomes frighteningly precise — a man who has nothing left to lose.

Expect a lot of “Fuck this,” “Fuck them,” and “Fuck me most of all.”
But the pink bracelet stays tied around his wrist — a small, stubborn hope in a world where faith has rotted.
