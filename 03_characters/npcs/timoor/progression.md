# 🌱 Twigmoss Timoorik — Campaign Progression Arc

_“Small roots still break stone.”_

---

## **ACT I — The Helpful Sapling**

**Theme:** Innocence, belonging, earned affection  
**Level Range:** Early campaign (Lv 2–5)

### Role in the Story

Timoor is a **recurring helper NPC**, not a follower. He appears:

- in Rootspire’s market
    
- along forest paths near the city
    
- after the party stabilizes or disturbs minor Veil anomalies
    

He idolizes the party. He mimics their words. He tries to help in small, sometimes inconvenient ways.

### Development Beats

- Gives the party charms, snacks, small boons
    
- Appears slightly “luckier” than expected
    
- Reacts _strongly_ to Veil disturbances (headaches, glowing freckles, emotional surges)
    

### Mechanical Growth (Optional)

- Gains _Guidance_ → _Resistance_ (1/day, touch, flavoured as rooting someone)
    
- Seed-bolts occasionally create difficult terrain **without him intending it**
    

### Player Perception

> “He’s adorable.”  
> “We must protect him.”  
> “Absolutely do not let the Concord see this little guy.”

---

## **ACT II — The Root Remembers**

**Theme:** Unease, responsibility, foreshadowing  
**Level Range:** Mid campaign (Lv 6–10)

### Shift in Tone

Timoor becomes **aware that something is wrong**, though he doesn’t know what.

He starts asking questions:

- “Do roots dream?”
    
- “If something is broken, does it _want_ to be fixed?”
    
- “If the tree asked you for help… would you say yes?”
    

### Revelations (Partial)

- Ancient Rootspire spirits recognize him
    
- Minor Veil anomalies stabilize faster when he’s nearby
    
- Concord agents take an _interest_ in him
    

### Mechanical Evolution

- Can unconsciously stabilize a **minor Veil tear** once per arc
    
- Gains an aura-like effect when frightened or resolute (wild magic-lite)
    

### DM Notes

Never tell the party outright. Let NPCs react _around_ him:

- Wardens bow slightly
    
- Sanctara harmonics hum when he’s present
    
- Veil sigils subtly align toward him
    

---

## **ACT III — The Weight of the Seed**

**Theme:** Choice, agency, fear of destiny  
**Level Range:** Late campaign (Lv 11–14)

### The Truth (Revealed)

Timoor carries a **Veil-Seed**, a proto-core formed when the Veil first tore beneath Rootspire.  
It is:

- ancient
    
- sentient in a quiet, instinctive way
    
- meant to _replace_ what was lost when the Veil fractured
    

The Seed has been **growing with him**.

### What This Means

- If allowed to mature, it could **heal a major tear** permanently
    
- Doing so would require a living anchor
    
- Timoor is the _only viable vessel_
    

He learns this **before the party does**.

### His Choice

He doesn’t want to die.  
But he doesn’t want Rootspire to suffer.

He asks the party:

> “If the tree needs me… will it hurt?”

---

## **ACT IV — The Impossible Choice**

**Theme:** Sacrifice vs defiance  
**Level Range:** Endgame (Lv 15+)

This occurs during a **major Veil crisis**:

- Rootspire is destabilizing
    
- The Sanctara’s core is failing
    
- The Concord demands decisive action
    

### The Choice (No Perfect Outcome)

### **OPTION A — Let Timoor Become the Root**

Timoor merges with the Veil-Seed and Rootspire’s core.

**Outcome:**

- Rootspire is healed
    
- The Veil stabilizes across the region
    
- Timoor loses his individuality
    
- He becomes a **gentle, ever-present spirit of the tree**
    

> Later, roots shift to protect the party.  
> Charms still appear where he once stood.

**Cost:**  
They save the world.  
They lose their friend.

---

### **OPTION B — Defy the Root**

The party refuses the sacrifice and extracts or destroys the Veil-Seed.

**Outcome:**

- Timoor survives, fragile but alive
    
- Rootspire becomes slowly blighted
    
- The Veil continues to fracture elsewhere
    
- The Concord brands the party as reckless or traitorous
    

**Cost:**  
They save him.  
The world pays the price.

---

### **OPTION C — The Third Path (Hidden, Earned)**

Only available if:

- The party stabilized multiple mini-cores
    
- Treated Timoor with consistent care
    
- Challenged the Concord’s narrative
    

They attempt to **share the burden**.

**Outcome:**

- The Veil-Seed fractures into harmonics
    
- Each PC permanently carries a fragment
    
- Rootspire survives, imperfect but alive
    
- Timoor lives—and remembers everything
    

**Cost:**  
Each PC bears a permanent mark, mechanical or narrative.  
The Veil will _never_ fully heal.

---

## **Epilogue — What Becomes of Timoor**

Regardless of choice, Timoor’s final moment mirrors his first:

> Small hands.  
> A hopeful look upward.  
> Trust, given freely.

He will be remembered.  
By the tree.  
By the world.  
By the players.
