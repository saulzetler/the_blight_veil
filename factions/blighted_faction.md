# **The Blighted**

**Overview:**  
The Blighted are those forever marked by the Veil’s corruption — beings caught between life and decay. Once human, now transformed by exposure to the Veil’s energy, they are shunned, hunted, or pitied. Their existence defies the natural order, yet among them burns a fierce will to survive and redefine what it means to be alive.

---

## **Nature of the Blight**

The Blight is both curse and revelation. It mutates flesh and spirit, awakening strange abilities and warped perceptions. No two Blighted are alike: some channel impossible strength, others speak with unseen entities, and some simply waste away. The Concord brands all as heretics and monsters — but in the wastes, the Blighted call themselves *the Next Dawn*.

---

## **Leadership**

### **The First Veilborn**  
A mythic figure — the first human to survive direct contact with the Veil. Said to be immortal, ageless, and suffused with Veil energy. The First Veilborn is less a ruler than a living symbol, guiding the Blighted through vision and prophecy.

**Principles:** Survival through adaptation; transcendence over purity.  
**Allegiances:** None publicly, though many whisper of secret pacts with rogue Concord scholars.  
**Notable Events:** The Dreaming Exodus; the Veilheart Accord.

---

### **Esmir the Hollow**  
Once a Faith inquisitor, Esmir was Blighted in the field and left to die. Now, his hollowed form radiates pale light from within, and his followers see him as a messiah. He preaches that the Blight is evolution — the next stage of existence beyond flesh.

**Principles:** Transformation as salvation; rebellion as freedom.  
**Allegiances:** Allied loosely with radical Free Peoples leaders and Veil cults.  
**Notable Events:** The Siege of Wornspire; the massacre at Glassfall.

---

## **The Veilborne Creed**

A growing belief among the Blighted that the Veil is not destruction, but awakening. They see themselves as envoys of a new world order, destined to inherit what remains when the pure consume themselves.

---

## **Relations with Other Factions**

- **The Concord:** Mortal enemies — hunted relentlessly as abominations.  
- **The Faith:** Objects of fear and fascination; heretics to most, divine to few.  
- **The Free Peoples:** Occasional allies of necessity, especially smugglers and outcasts.
