# **The Free Peoples**

**Overview:**  
The Free Peoples are a scattered confederation of exiles, nomads, smugglers, and survivors who refuse the yoke of the Concord. Bound by necessity rather than law, they move between the Veil’s scars and the remnants of fallen nations, living off trade, salvage, and sheer defiance.

---

## **Culture and Ideals**

Freedom is their creed — but freedom, in this age, is brutal. The Free Peoples reject the Concord’s hierarchy and the Faith’s dogma, living instead by the rule of the road: *no crown, no altar, no chains.* They preserve songs, crafts, and stories lost to the Blight, carrying the memory of what the world once was.

---

## **Leadership**

### **Captain Mirella “Ashhand” Coren**  
A smuggler queen and folk hero, Ashhand made her name running food, medicine, and forbidden knowledge through Veil-infested lands. Her burned hands — forever blackened by Blightfire — are a mark of courage and defiance.

**Principles:** Freedom through cunning; survival through loyalty.  
**Allegiances:** Trades with both Blighted enclaves and Faith pilgrims; maintains a tense peace with the Concord’s border guards.  
**Notable Events:** Brokered the Pact of the Free Winds; led the rescue of the Emberfield Convoy.

---

### **The Wanderer’s Council**  
A loose circle of clan leaders, free captains, and elder traders who meet once each turning to decide on trade routes, truces, and codes of conduct. The Council’s authority is symbolic, but their decisions carry moral weight among the Free Peoples.

**Principles:** Community through choice, not coercion.  
**Allegiances:** Sometimes coordinates with sympathetic Concord defectors.  
**Notable Events:** Declared neutrality during the Silver Roads Blockade; outlawed Faith missionary encampments.

---

## **Economy and Survival**

The Free Peoples thrive on adaptability. They scavenge, repurpose, and barter across a fragmented world. Their caravans are both marketplaces and fortresses, guarded by those who know no home but the horizon.

---

## **Relations with Other Factions**

- **The Concord:** Enemies in law, but indispensable traders in truth.  
- **The Faith:** Viewed with disdain; missionaries rarely survive long among them.  
- **The Blighted:** Allies of necessity, often offering sanctuary or safe passage.
