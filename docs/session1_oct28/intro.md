# Session 1 Intro & Key NPCs

## Intro Hook (Read Aloud)
> The city of Concord’s capital is alive with tension. Stone towers loom over crowded streets, banners snap in the wind, and the air is thick with the scent of incense and fear. You stand among a restless crowd gathered in the central square, where justice is swift and mercy is rare. The distant toll of a bell signals that something — or someone — is about to change the fate of this city forever.

## Overview
Session 1 begins in the capital of the Concord during a public demonstration. A woman, **Marla Veinwright**, accused of heresy, is dragged to a stage by Concord Inquisitors. The players arrive as her fate is about to be decided. This scene branches into multiple possible paths depending on player choices.

## Key NPCs & Boxed DM Dialogue

**Marla Veinwright** – Defiant heretic, voice shaking but determined.
> “They call it heresy, but it is only truth — a song older than their chains. Will you let them burn me for a melody?”

**Inquisitor Rellis** – Fanatical, but with a spark of doubt.
> “The law is clear. Heresy must be purged… and yet, my heart falters. Why would the Dawnfather gift a song that tempts the innocent? Speak quickly — should I silence her?”

**Captain Deyran** – Brutal enforcer, eager to crush dissent.
> “Enough of this dithering, Rellis! The people crave justice! By fire and blade, we show them the price of rebellion!”

**Onlooker: Fearful Citizen** – Terrified of Concord authority.
> “Please, just let them burn her and be done with it.”

**Onlooker: Sympathetic Citizen (Cindersong sympathizer)** – Whispering support.
> “The song she sings… I’ve heard it in my dreams. Stranger, if you value truth, do not let them take her voice.”

## Items & Notes That Can Be Found
- **Scrap of Hymn**: A torn fragment of parchment from Marla’s belongings. The script is written in an old dialect, partially burnt at the edges. A successful DC 13 Religion or History check reveals it is part of the *Cindersong*, a hymn said to echo with divine resonance. Its words shift slightly when read aloud, unsettling and beautiful.
  - DM Exposition: "You uncover a scrap of parchment, its edges burnt and the script shifting as you read—a fragment of something ancient and powerful."
- **Concord Proclamation Poster**: A large sheet nailed to a post near the square. In bold ink it reads: *“By decree of the High Council of the Concord, heresy shall henceforth be met with flame and steel. Any who harbor dissidents are subject to the pyre.”* Players can take it, use it as proof of Concord severity, or later show it as propaganda.
- **Hidden Symbol (Cindersong Sigil)**: A small mark carved into the underside of the stage — three interlocked spirals forming a flame. A DC 15 Religion check identifies it as the mark of the Cindersong. Touching it briefly fills the character with warmth and the sound of faint singing, like a chorus from underground. It suggests the Cindersong have infiltrated the capital itself.
