Session 1 opens with everyone in the town square. 

Cassie Thongington asks everyone who they are, purely as a plot device to get them to be bonded to each other and to explain the basics of their character to the other players.

Ashton Zennor introduces himself. A grizzly man who was a former soldier in the concord army who grew up on the rougher side of Orrenval.

Fergalous introduces themselves  
They are a follower of the temple of radiant dawn.

The un-named Robot introduces themselves. They have just woken up and have no knowledge of how they were powered down or how long they have been off for. 

Lastly Hog introduces himself he is a scrappy dwarf and doesn't say much else.

Marla is brought onto the dias in the center of the square by captain Deyran and Inquisitor Rellis. 

Marla stands accused of heresy for singing a forbidden melody and will be burned on the pyre.

The party huddles quickly to discuss their mutual disdain for the concord and decide to act swiftly. The un-named Robot has not made up their mind about how they feel about the concord yet.

The party speaks to captain Deyran and he accuses them of trying to interrupt the “trial.”

Inquisitor Rellis chimes in to say that maybe the party should be heard out and that maybe they should appeal to the desires of the people. 

Deyran refuses and states that justice must be delivered by sword and by flame.

Hog suggests trial by combat and captain Deyran agrees but asks who will stand for Marla. Hog tries to suggest anyone other than him but quickly retracts that statement and suggests a 4v4. 

Captain Deyran draws his sword and all the townsfolk scurry out of the square.

Combat begins.

During combat Marla is struck by an ice knife explosion and almost dies. She then tries to run away.

Inquisitor Rellis calls out for reinforcements and concord soldiers close in from almost all the exits to the square.

A shadowy figure appears from one of the alleyways and beckons Marla and the rest of the party to follow him.

Captain Deyran is killed by the unnamed robot’s Moonbeam. The party successfully escapes down the alleyway with a spell that hides their movement. 

They follow the shadowy figure into a hideout. The hideout has a symbol at the center that is three rings bound together encased in flame. Hog draws a crude representation of what he assumes is the cindersong sigil.

The shadowy figure introduces himself as Vaereth. Vaereth explains that the party has drawn a lot of heat from attacking the captain in the middle of the central square. 

Vaereth also explains that the party has 3 options once they leave town. 

1. The eastern gate towards the Ashroot, a large forested area where rebel groups, peaceful villagers under constant threat from the concord, and powerful shamans and druids all live.  
2. The southern gate toward the Godscar. A war ravaged land mostly devoid of civilization. LIkely a dangerous place if unprepared.   
3. The northern gate towards the coast. It is said that there is potentially a merchant town there. Not much else is known about this place.

The party briefly discusses before deciding they will travel towards the Ashroot. 

They try to get more information out of Vaereth but he does not have more information to share. 

Marla is taken somewhere to recover from her wounds.

The party is told it can rest in the hideout but must be gone before first light. 

Before resting the party briefly talks about their aspirations.

Fergie wants to be trained by a master of fire

Unnamed Robot wants to know where their creator is and why they turned you off and how long you have been asleep for. 

They long rest in the cindersong hideout

When they wake up in the morning a rumor has started in Orrenval that  the party members are good for nothing scumbags and also they are extremely clumsy. No one is at the hideout any more. 

They leave early in the morning around 4am. They head through the empty streets towards the eastern gate. 

When they come to the eastern gate they see two guards standing on either side of the gate about 150 feet out. 

They devise a plan on how to distract the guards and escape out of the city. After finding out that Unnamed robot can summon a raven. Zennor, remembering from his youth, explains that ravens are a bad omen in this city and all of them must be purged.

Unnamed robot casts a familiar spell to send a raven out to distract the guards. One of the guards leaves to track down the raven. Then Zennor climbs to the roof of a nearby building and shoots the other guard. 

Combat ensues and the other guard is called back to help. The guards are both swiftly defeated. 

The party exits the city but as the do they look back and see 

A thin, vertical tear splits open in midair — no wider than a handspan, but impossibly deep. Inside, the world looks… wrong. Not dark, but overexposed, like staring into a reflection of stars drowning beneath an ocean.

Then, something moves within it — a silhouette. A tall, veiled figure stands just beyond the tear, visible for an instant through the light. Its face cannot be seen. Its hand presses against the barrier — almost tenderly.

A whisper fills your mind, layered and distant:

“The veil... remembers what it was made to forget.”

The fissure snaps shut. A pulse of force rolls outward — dust lifts from the marble, banners whip in all directions, and every torch within sight gutters out at once.

Then — silence again.

The Sanctara’s bells begin to toll.

Before ending the session the part members all inspect the unnamed robot and find a serial number 05C4R. They decide to call them Oscar.
