## **Eris’ Amulet**

*(Also known in whispers as the Widow’s Sigil)*

**Wondrous Item, rare (requires attunement by a Fighter)**

Forged of silver and bone, the amulet bears an etched spiral symbol — the same mark once worn by his wife Eris, a witch of the Veil. It pulses faintly with warmth when he dreams, whispering her voice through the static of memory. Though he believes it cursed, it grants him uncanny resilience in battle — a gift, or perhaps a leash.

---

### 🩸 **Stage I — Dormant (“The Weight of Memory”)**

*Unlocked upon attunement.*

The amulet feeds on guilt and blood. Its magic remains faint, flickering like a dying candle.

**Properties:**

* **Echo of Her Voice.** When reduced to 0 HP but not killed outright, you automatically succeed your first death saving throw. The amulet lets out a faint whisper — your wife’s voice calling your name.
* **Relentless Surge.** Once per long rest, when you reduce a creature to 0 HP, you regain 1d10 + your Constitution modifier HP.
* **Cursed Bond.** When you first attune to the amulet, you gain recurring nightmares of Eris and your lost daughter. You cannot willingly unattune from it until the curse is broken or transformed.

**Flavour:**
At this stage, the amulet feels like both protection and punishment. It keeps him alive when he would rather die, binding him to a promise unfinished.

---

### ⚔️ **Stage II — Awakened (“The Witch’s Promise”)**

*Unlocked after a major confrontation with remnants of the Dawnshield, or discovering evidence of Eris’ survival.*

The amulet awakens, its spiral mark glowing faintly crimson. It hums during combat, almost anticipating your strikes. The witch’s power entwines with your martial instinct.

**Properties:**

* All previous effects, plus:
* **Veilstrike.** Once per short or long rest, when you use **Action Surge**, your weapon attacks deal an additional 1d8 necrotic damage until the end of your turn. Each creature damaged this way must succeed on a DC 15 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened of you until the start of your next turn.
* **Witch’s Resilience.** When you take damage that would reduce you below half HP, you can use your reaction to gain temporary HP equal to your Fighter level. When you do, your eyes briefly glow with the same hue as the amulet.

**Curse Deepens:**
The amulet begins to whisper mid-battle — sometimes with tactical clarity, sometimes with haunting phrases (“She’s waiting.” “They burned her.” “You left us.”). Failing a DC 13 Wisdom save after each long rest may impose **one level of exhaustion** as nightmares bleed into waking thought.

**Flavour:**
He begins to fight with supernatural efficiency — movements guided by memory and magic both. But it becomes harder to tell whether he’s drawing strength from his will, or from the thing that lives in the amulet.

---

### 🔥 **Stage III — Fully Awakened / Transformed (“The Breaking of the Bond”)**

*Unlocked upon completing the character’s major arc — when he either destroys or transforms the amulet (his choice). This marks the resolution of his personal story.*

At this point, the amulet’s power either becomes **cleansed** (if he chooses redemption) or **corrupted** (if he chooses vengeance).

You can choose one of the following outcomes:

---

#### ✨ **Path of Redemption — “The Cleansed Sigil”**

The amulet’s spiral cracks open, light pouring through the fissures. The whispers cease.
He learns to forgive — not Eris, perhaps, but himself.

**Properties:**

* You gain advantage on death saving throws.
* **Purged Surge.** When you use **Second Wind**, all allies within 10 feet regain HP equal to your Fighter level.
* **Soul Untethered.** You can no longer be frightened or charmed. The amulet becomes inert, a symbol rather than a source of power.

**Flavour:**
The amulet is no longer a prison but a memento. It no longer glows or hums — only warms faintly when he speaks of his daughter.

---

#### 💀 **Path of Vengeance — “The Widow’s Heart”**

Instead of breaking the amulet, he merges with it — accepting the witch’s curse as part of himself. His body bears faint runic scars where the chain once rested.

**Properties:**

* All previous Awakened effects, plus:
* **Vengeful Spirit.** When you reduce a creature to 0 HP, you can immediately move up to half your movement and make another weapon attack (no action required).
* **Death Denied.** Once per long rest, when you would be reduced to 0 HP, you instead drop to 1 HP and deal an additional 2d8 necrotic damage with your next attack.
* **Aura of Dread.** Enemies within 10 feet who start their turn must make a DC 16 Wisdom saving throw or be frightened until the start of their next turn.

**Flavour:**
The amulet fuses with his flesh, its spiral etched across his collarbone. His eyes burn red in combat; his voice carries the echo of another. Whether it’s Eris or something darker, no one can tell.

---

### Narrative Function

The amulet acts as the **axis of his entire character arc**:

* **Dormant:** It symbolizes his grief and guilt.
* **Awakened:** It merges his martial and magical instincts, forcing him to confront what’s left of Eris’ influence.
* **Transformed:** It embodies his choice — redemption or corruption.

In gameplay, it rewards survival, aggression, and the thematic tension of his story: he’s too stubborn to die, too broken to rest, and too dangerous to ignore.
