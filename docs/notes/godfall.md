# The Godfall: Birth of the Blight Veil & The Concord

## The World Before the Godfall

Before the war, the world was a tapestry of vibrant cultures, city-states, and wild frontiers. Gods walked among mortals, their temples rising above bustling markets and sacred groves. Magic was abundant, woven into daily life—healers, seers, and artisans all drew upon divine gifts. Politics revolved around alliances between priesthoods, merchant guilds, and noble houses. Festivals honored the pantheon, and disputes were settled by ritual or council.

## Instigating Events

- **The Shattering of the Dawnfather’s Pact:** A powerful god’s promise of peace was broken by mortal ambition, leading to jealousy and strife among the divine.
- **The Rise of Heretical Cults:** Secret societies began to worship forbidden powers, undermining the old order.
- **Assassination of the High Oracle:** The murder of a beloved spiritual leader shattered trust between factions.
- **The Theft of the Godseed:** A relic capable of granting godhood was stolen, sparking a race for power.

## Major Players

- **The Concord:** A coalition of city-states and priesthoods seeking order and unity.
- **The Ashroot Tribes:** Guardians of ancient nature magic, resisting Concord expansion.
- **The Free Peoples:** Nomads, traders, and rebels caught between warring powers.
- **The Pantheon:** Gods of light, shadow, nature, and fate—some noble, some vengeful.
- **The Godscar Cults:** Fanatics who sought to harness divine death for their own ends.
- **Heroes & Villains:**
	- *Varric Thane:* Concord general, later the first Lord Commander.
	- *Matriarch Elira Voss:* Visionary priestess, survivor of the Godfall.
	- *Ashhand Coren:* Smuggler queen, broker of forbidden alliances.

## Notable Battles & Turning Points

- **The Siege of Lume:** Concord forces besieged the city of Lume, shattering its guardian deity and unleashing the first Veil.
- **The Night of Falling Stars:** Multiple gods were slain in a single night, their deaths tearing rifts in reality.
- **The Burning of Ashroot:** Concord pyres swept through sacred forests, driving the tribes into exile.
- **The Battle of the Godscar:** Cultists attempted to resurrect a dead god, resulting in a catastrophic explosion of Veil energy.

## Consequences of the Godfall

- **Creation of the Blight Veil:** The deaths of gods tore open the fabric of reality, releasing the Blight Veil—a shimmering haze that warps magic, time, and life itself.
- **Rise of the Concord:** The Concord emerged as the dominant power, enforcing order through martial law, faith, and propaganda.
- **Transformation of Magic:** Divine magic became unstable; new forms of Veil-touched sorcery appeared.
- **Societal Upheaval:** Old priesthoods fell, new cults rose, and survivors struggled to adapt to a world haunted by divine loss.

## Lasting Scars & Mysteries

- **The Godscar:** A region where reality remains broken, haunted by echoes of divine agony.
- **The First Veilborn:** Legends speak of mortals transformed by direct contact with the Veil—some revered, others hunted.
- **Lost Relics:** Artifacts from the war, such as the Godseed and the Lantern of Dawn, remain hidden or fiercely guarded.
- **Unanswered Questions:**
	- What truly happened to the slain gods?
	- Can the Blight Veil be healed, or is it the world’s new destiny?
	- Who benefits from the chaos that followed?
